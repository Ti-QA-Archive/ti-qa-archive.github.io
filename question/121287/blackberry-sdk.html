    You need a paid subscription (or trial) to get access to the Blackberry beta SDK. Once it goes public – scheduled I believe for yet in June – it will be available to the community free. Early access to unreleased things like Blackerry or the Mobile Web SDK is restricted to the subscription plans.

    That said, I would suspect that taking them up on the 30-day trial to a paid plan would also give you access immediately, and by the time that expires the Blackberry SDK "should" be public and available free to everyone.

    AFAIK.
