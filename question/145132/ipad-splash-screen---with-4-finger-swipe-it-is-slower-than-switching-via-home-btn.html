iPad Splash Screen - With 4 finger swipe it is slower than switching via home btn

I have an app that is used as a reference while using another app as well - i.e. the user is frequently switching between my app and another.

Switching via the home button double-tap - the splash screen shows for about 1 second.

Switching via a 4-finger swipe on the iPad - the splash screen shows for 3-4 seconds. This is too long.

First of all, why does the Splash screen take longer when using a 4-finger swipe? Is there something at the Titanium level that is causing this to occur?

Any clues or tips appreciated.

    This is the answer Matthew Delmarter has worked out himself after Malcolm Hollingsworth spent time determining that the situation being experienced by Matthew was not the expected behaviour, leading Matthew to have to dig deeper to find the solution.

    Please note this is only duplicated so that an answer can be marked due to restrictions in the Q&A.

    Thanks for confirming that this behaviour is not expected Malcolm. It forced me to look deeper, and I have located the issue.

    In my tiapp.xml I had the following entries:

    <ios>
    <min-ios-ver>4.3</min-ios-ver>
    <plist>
        <dict>
            <key>UIRequiresPersistentWiFi</key>
            <false/>
            <key>UIPrerenderedIcon</key>
            <true/>
            <key>UIStatusBarHidden</key>
            <false/>
            <key>UIStatusBarStyle</key>
            <string>UIStatusBarStyleBlackTranslucent</string>
            <key>UISupportedInterfaceOrientations~ipad</key>
            <array>
                <string>UIInterfaceOrientationPortrait</string>
                <string>UIInterfaceOrientationPortraitUpsideDown</string>
                <string>UIInterfaceOrientationLandscapeLeft</string>
                <string>UIInterfaceOrientationLandscapeRight</string>
            </array>
            <key>UISupportedInterfaceOrientations</key>
            <array>
                <string>UIInterfaceOrientationPortrait</string>
            </array>
            <key>UIBackgroundModes</key>
            <array>
                <string>audio</string>
            </array>
        </dict>
    </plist>
</ios>

    I trimmed this right back and then managed to isolate that the following entries were entirely responsible for this behaviour:

    <key>UIStatusBarHidden</key>
<false/>
<key>UIStatusBarStyle</key>
<string>UIStatusBarStyleBlackTranslucent</string>

    Maybe this changed with iOS6 with the newer handling of how the status bar is colored or something. I could not tell you when this issue started to be honest - i.e. when the splash screen started showing every time I switched apps and back again.

    However I can confirm that taking these lines out has removed the issue altogether.

    This is the original confirmation showing that the behaviour experienced was not expected.

    Well done Matt.

    Lots of noise here but I wonder if the following is true:

    In my experience, this is something handled by iOS (I am going with experience because I have not seen any documentation about this). If the OS can restore the application state nice and quickly, it will display a screenshot of its previous state while that state is restored.

However, if something will delay the process, such as the app not being in the background properly yet (such as during rapid task switching), or if something else predictable will delay the startup, then it reverts to the splash screen (instead of the screenshot), to ease the user experience.

Short answer: it is out of your hands.
