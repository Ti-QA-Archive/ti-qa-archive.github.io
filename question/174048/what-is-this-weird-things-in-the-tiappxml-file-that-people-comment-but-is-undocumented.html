WHAT IS THIS? Weird things in the tiapp.xml file that people comment but is UNDOCUMENTED

I'm trying to get my app to be "portrait only". There are many, many questions about how to do this - none of them work. But that's now what I am asking here!

Instead, I've noticed that a lot of answers have this kind of jazz:

         <android:label="Mytest" android:name="MytestApplication"
        android:debuggable="false">

        <activity android:name=".MytestActivity"
            android:label="Mytest" android:theme="@style/Theme.Titanium"
            android:configChanges="keyboardHidden"
            android:screenOrientation="portrait">
            <intent-filter>
                <action android:name="android.intent.action.MAIN" />
                <category android:name="android.intent.category.LAUNCHER" />
            </intent-filter>
        </activity>

What is this Mytest/MytestApplication/MytestActivity crap? From a reading of the android manifest doco (found here: http://developer.android.com/guide/topics/manifest/activity-element.html) says that the name should refer to a class.

    Whilst there are correct ways to ask a questions these ones point specifically to you;

    • Your subject line should contain something suitable that explains what you are trying to solve

      • General annoyance should not be included
      • Suggestions someone/something/documentation somehow has not be telling you things - add no value or point to any question
      • I imagine this should look something more like this;

      Can I lock Android app to portrait?

    • Providing an initial paragraph should aid the person offering assistance to determine your specific needs

      • Suggesting that nothing you have found has assisted you - is not very helpful
      • If that is the case provide some links so we can get a sense of what you have tried
      • I imagine this should look something more like this;

      How do I make my Android app portrait only?

    • Providing seemingly unrelated code snippets
      • If you provide code snippets - make them relevant
      • Do not add something just so you can moan about it
    • Tone down your language
    • Undocumented things
      • Things are not undocumented just because you cannot find them where you are looking
      • Android and iOS specific sections can included many native elements that go beyond what are provided by Titanium - this is for you benefit not to make you feel annoyed
      • Those native elements are documented on the Apple and Android sites

    So by all means ask a good question - ask one that aims to get you an answer - but if you ask one that is designed to annoy or to insult - you are unlikely to get a good answer, worse you are likely to get ignored by everybody - as nobody like spending time with a person like that in real life.
