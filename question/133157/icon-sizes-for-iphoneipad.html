    • No, it does not. Simply outputs all the icon variants.
      For splash screens (targeting phones) I build a 480x960 (this will be the iPhone retina version) then work from there into the standard iPhone splash screen and various Android sizes. When doing the original design, just leave enough free space around texts/logo to that you can crop down to the correct size.

      When targeting tablets, you can then just increase the size of your canvas up to the sizes you need and fill with your background color.

      We have a set of photoshop actions that (we may make this available in the future) handle a lot of this for us.

      There is an article over on bjango that is great starting place for designing for retina displays and converting back down to your stand res. Worth looking at.

      All of our tablet apps already have retina quality graphics in them in preparation of the iPad 3. Something to be thinking about.
