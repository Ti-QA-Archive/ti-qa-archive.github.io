    Hi Rainer :)

    As for my understanding, an IPA is a signed package of the app. Re-signing an IPA is theoretically possible but could be very painful.

    Exchanging the App as an xcode-archive seems to be the better solution. You can export it, they can import it and sign it with their own certificate (and send it straight to iTunes connect through Xcode).

    I'm afraid you won't be able to generate a distributable IPA. I've had a similar issue. We told that other company, that they have 3 options:

    • give us full access on their developer program (easiest for them)
    • take the xcode-archive and sign/release the app (easiest for you)
    • take the source-code and build/release the app (difficult for them / bad for you as you give away your code)

    I'm not aware if there are other or even better opportunities but I hope this helps.

    Manuel
