  • Still not sure what you want to achieve:

    A touchstart/touchend combination is usually used when the user touches one part of the object and ends up in another part hence the event properties having the x & y values;

    A single tap is when the user intends to tap the object for an action to happen.

    What I suggest you might want to do is record the touchstart x & y and then in the touchend event if the touchend x & y is the same as the touchstart x & y then perform the action for a singletap otherwise perform the action for touchend, and dont have a singletap.

    This is the best I can suggest without understanding what you want to achieve.
