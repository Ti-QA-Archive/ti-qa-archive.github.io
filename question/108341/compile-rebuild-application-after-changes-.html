    • >Oh , every time i would make changes i would have to wait forever to actually see those changes , since launching the emulator takes minutes !

      Really disappointed about all this approach by appcelerator.<

      Building for android is a bear compared to iOS, but that is due to how the android emulator works, not so much because of appcelerator. For android, do NOT stop the emulator each time. It takes WAY too long to restart. Leave it running and just launch the new version. It should copy to the emulator and start much faster – though still slower than iOS.

      Others have reported they find skipping the emulator and just directly launching on a device to be the fastest method for Android. YMMV.

      But the bottom line here is most of the time delay when using the android emulator is the emulator itself – not appcelerator.

      Personally, I find it much more productive to do most development/debug/test cycles under iOS then as features work there, just do minimal testing/tweaking under android.

      And that is coming from someone who never had osx before. I picked up an old used macbook pro because I had to be able to build for the iphone. Then I discovered that even with the machine being much slower than my Win7 machine, my debug cycles were sooo much faster I now to all my appcelerator stuff from it.

      But again, I think the issue is not appcelerator – it is the speed (or lack of speed) of the android emulator from Google.

      YMMV
