Retina Display & imageView Dimensions

Ok, I have figured out how to handle the image.png and image@2x.png with no issues at all! It's tricky to understand the paths and how it work, and where to place the images..! but all is ok!

my question now is this.. I have the following imageView

var logo = Titanium.UI.createImageView({
   image:'/images/image.png'
   width:100,
   height:200
})

The question is, are the above dimensions (width & height) denoted for the non-retina screen? (which sounds logical as the image is the non-retina).

Please confirm …

George

  • iOS layout is done on a "points" grid with the iPhone having a 320x480 grid and the iPad having a 768 x 1024 grid. If you're working with non-retina devices, those points correspond 1:1 with pixels. For retina displays, it's essentially 1:2 (1 point = 2 pixels).

    The net effect is that your 100 x 200 image would be shown at 100px x 200px on a non-retina device and 200px by 400px on a retina display, but take up the same physical dimensions. The results can be less than stellar on a retina display (as your image is effectively stretched to double it's native size). So, provide an @2x version that is double the pixels and you'll be all set.

    var logo = Titanium.UI.createImageView({
   image:'/images/image.png',
   /* 
      make sure there's an images/image@2x.png which is
      200px by 400px and it will be used automatically on
      retina displays
   */
   width:100,
   height:200
})
