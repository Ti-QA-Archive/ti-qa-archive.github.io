      • The one article stated that the "system stores your source code in a resource folder for each device …", therefore enabling a thrid party to view it. Is this true?

      All apps that are created using any platform are capable of being opened by third-parties and code being read that is the nature of the storage type used by the platforms. The storage format is a zip file containing all the assets for your app.

      So in answer to your question - no more or less than any other app. You should always take whatever measures you deem necessary to reduce the risks - perceived or otherwise.

      • Does the current version of Titanium leak memory?

      Yes of course, but so do all other languages, memory leaks are caused by bad coding styles and failure to tidy up after yourself if you have attached things to objects that persist beyond housekeeping. However as with all platforms memory leaks can be reduced and even removed by following good programming standards.

      • And lastly, is the Android app less efficient than the iOs app?

      No and yes or yes and no - depending on your view point. The question is loaded (I know not intentionally) but the question should be does using Titanium favour iOS or Android and does that favouring mean that the Android app is not as good as the iOS app?.

      Still a problem here as well - as the thing this does not take into account is the actual platform and not the development system. Are iOS and Android the same or equal? No is Android better than iOS? Yes and no. Is iOS better than Android? Yes and no.

      The problem is a much more complicated one, in the old days Titanium did favour iOS, but before that they favoured Android in my opinion - right now it is 50/50 (again IMHO). Why? Partly due to the platforms themselves, they both have roll outs of new features at different times and with different levels of ability. Some features are so wildly great and direction changing they are put in right away - some less ones are not, the quickest are put in right away - or sometimes later on.

      This is a business after all. So maybe we ask this question Can I create great apps using all the features of the standard OS for both Android and iOS without worrying that my app will be limited on one or the other? Yes

      I will add a final question;

      • Does Titanium make you a better developer?

      No - certainly not. Can you become a better developer with Titanium - Yes, but not everyone will. You have to be willing to put the effort in (sounds like you would) and you have to make the most of the platform.

      I am significantly better at writing apps now than I did the very first day - because I now know how to get the very most out of the platform. But in 6 months I will tell you I am much better then as I learnt more than I thought possible right now. We have to learn more we have to get better and the platform is only a tool to do out job.

      Remember that saying "a bad workman blames his tools" - same stuff here.

      Another thing to keep in mind (if you are a gamer or have a passing knowledge of the consoles). When the XBOX and PS came out (does not matter which generation) the games looked great, much better than the previous stuff we saw. Look at the games now on the same version and they are MUCH better, same hardware, same development system, same coders - they learnt to get more out of the platform.

      If nothing else you have learnt I like to type.
