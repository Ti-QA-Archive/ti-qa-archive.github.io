  • Accepted Answer

    Looks like the module doesn't exist any more, but the functions are available in alloy/sync/properties. However, require-ing modules like this is not recommended, as from your experience shows, they may get removed, renamed or not expose the methods/properties they used to.

    Might be best to take a copy of what you are using within there, and move it to your code so you can control it.
