I have been trying to hunt down an issue here for a few days with no luck. Hoping someone out there will be able to help out!

This is happening on Android.

I have a window with a scrollView. I have added a button to the scrollView. When I click on the button I show an Activity Indicator. About 10%-20% of the time this causes the app to crash.

I have included three files below. The structure of these files is based on the structure of the app we are trying to develop.

The file that is causing the problem is logon.js

The logon window would normally have a username and password field and a logon button. This would then contact the database… but for this example there is just a logon button. When you click on this button we display an Activity Indicator for a second…

When I click on this final logon button, about 10%-20% of the time the app crashes before the Activity Indicator is displayed.

  • You can put in logs and read them as they are fired while on an Android device to see the error.

    1. Navigate to your android-sdk folder, then to the 'tools' subfolder in terminal. It might look like:
      cd android-sdk&#x2F;tools

    2. Again, with terminal, run the command './ddms`

    3. This should open a tool. Select your connected device from the list and you'll notice the logs at the bottom. You can now see what information your device outputs.

    4. To narrow down the logs, click the plus sign in the bottom left, and add a filter called Titanium and filter by log tag TiAPI and log message titanium.

    Hope this helps!
