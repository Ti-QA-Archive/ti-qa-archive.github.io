  • I'm a user, so these are just my personal opinions, not official answers.

    1) Blackberry support: I think you would have to define what you mean by "support". By definition, beta products should not be used for production applications, and IMHO "support" in a beta product suggests more accepting bug reports than any guaranteed response. Moreoever, with Appcelerator's model I would say there is no "support" at all until you reach the Pro level of membership – even on products not in beta. Even at the Indie level you have read-only access to the tickets but can't open a ticket with a guaranteed response time.

    2) Blackberry development on OSX: While you can use Eclipse to write code, Research In Motion has only released simulators for Windows. So to do testing, you must do so on either a real or virtual Windows machine. I have not tried it, but suspect BootCamp or Parallels would let you do so on an Apple machine.

    Note that this is the opposite case of Apple providing only OSX based simulators. Neither are really a limitation imposed by Appcelerator. They are just at the mercy of the tools available for native development.

    3) Features available: see next question. Since it is in beta, I don't know if it is acceptable to discuss them here.

    4) Where to find it: You may need to log out and back in. When you do, there should be a Helpdesk link in the top frame. Under that there will be a Resources section with a link to Early Access Programs. You will find the Blackberry beta listed there.
