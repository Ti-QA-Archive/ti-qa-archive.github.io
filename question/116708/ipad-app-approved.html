iPad app approved

Just got my first iPad app approved (I have 2 iphone ones). It's called PlotMe for iPad, basically lets you enter all of your addresses or locations that you're going to be visiting, and organizes them as to which is closest to you, so you don't spend time and gas driving around.

On a side note, it got rejected twice before, and I tracked it to locking the app into Landscape mode. Worked fine on the Simulator, but I dont have an iPad, so I couldnt test completely. Once I took that out it got approved.

I did have to tweak my "Details" splash screen, so it wouldnt be blank, I just made it "designed for Landscape mode" and it looks fine.

Special thanks to Aaraon Saunders (@aaronksaunders) for his tech help on getting my Splitview working.

http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/plotme-for-ipad/id417666843?mt=8&ls=1

  • Here is my basic code, I stripped out some non-related code.

    
// this sets the background color of the master UIView (when there are no windows/tab groups on it)
Titanium.UI.setBackgroundColor('#000');
SplitViewNav = {};

// WINDOWS
SplitViewNav.masterWindow = Ti.UI.createWindow({
    title:'Actions',
    width:'40%',
    barColor:'#32680d',
    backgroundImage:'bg.png',
    backgroundColor: '#2e6409'
    });
SplitViewNav.detailWindow = Ti.UI.createWindow({
    title:'Plot Me',
    width:'60%',
    barColor:'#32680d',
    backgroundColor: '#dce1eb'
    });


// MASTER NAV GROUP
SplitViewNav.masterNav = Ti.UI.iPhone.createNavigationGroup({
       window:SplitViewNav.masterWindow
});

// DETAIL NAV GROUP
SplitViewNav.detailNav = Ti.UI.iPhone.createNavigationGroup({
       window:SplitViewNav.detailWindow
});

// SPLIT VIEW
SplitViewNav.splitView = Titanium.UI.iPad.createSplitWindow({
       masterView:SplitViewNav.masterNav,
       detailView:SplitViewNav.detailNav
});


SplitViewNav.splitView.open();
