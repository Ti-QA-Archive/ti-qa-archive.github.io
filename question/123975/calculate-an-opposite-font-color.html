Calculate an opposite font color

Hello

I am wondering, is there a way to calculate or get an opposite font color from a given color? Well, not really an opposite color, but when you have a dark background color you'll get a bright font color and visa versa.

    Not an opposite color, that would be complementary color (blue & yellow).

    Anyway, first you need to transform RGB color value to HSB color value (Google how to do that) and then increase brightness. That should work I think. :)

    • Just increasing brightness won't work if you have any channel already towards 255 (i.e. white, pure red / green or blue). This gives a somewhat reasonable approach, basically using a threshold figure out if your colour is light or dark and use black or white for text depending on the outcome. Anything else using pure calculation would probably give some pretty ugly results.

      Another approach if you want more than black or white text would be to create a table mapping background colours to their appropriate foreground colour then scanning it to find the closest to your background colour and selecting it's foreground colour. This would let you pick pretty good contrasting colours (hopefully by a decent graphic designer who has a clue about these things).
