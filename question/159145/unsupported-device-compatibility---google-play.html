Unsupported Device Compatibility - Google Play

Hi,

I have many users complaining that my app is not available for their Android device, it's simply not there when they search in Google Play, or it says that their device is unsupported. Especially Samsung Galaxy Tab customers. In Google Play I can see there are 1230 unsupported devices, 133 of them are Samsung! :(

I have been researching minSDK, targetSDK, android:required="false" options for the manifest in tiapp.xml, but nothing seems to change anything.

Here is the <android /> section of my tiapp.xml:

<android xmlns:android="http://schemas.android.com/apk/res/android">
        <tool-api-level>14</tool-api-level>
        <manifest android:installLocation="preferExternal"
            android:uiOptions="splitActionBarWhenNarrow"
            android:versionCode="62" android:versionName="3.42">

            <application>
                <activity android:configChanges="orientation|keyboard"

                    <intent-filter>

                        <action android:name="android.intent.action.VIEW"/>
                        <category android:name="android.intent.category.BROWSABLE"/>
                        <category android:name="android.intent.category.DEFAULT"/>
                    </intent-filter>
                </activity>
            </application>
        </manifest>
    </android>

My app makes extensive use of the Action bar so this is why I have the tool-api-level and targetSDKVersion set to 14.

Any advice would be most helpful!

  • Ok, lets start walking through some of the things that could possibly affect device lockout.

    • Hardware exclusions. If you use a camera and the device has no camera, it could be locked out. This occured when the nexus 7 first came out as it only had a front camera and not a back camera.
    <uses-feature android:name="android.hardware.camera" android:required="false"/>

    I see that you have these in your tiapp.xml, but they sure dont seem to be carried over to the androidmanifest as i would expect. If these are not being carried over, you may have to do a custom androidmanifest from the platform/android directory.

    This can also apply to older hardware that did not have external storage and also failed to lie to the system and set a portion of storage aside for it.

    • The <uses-sdk> settings. Your settings say the lowest you will support is API 10, which is the upper end of Gingerbread. Your mean, tested target API is 14 . These are set at a reasonable level, although i tend to target 15 and not 14, but not necessarily the issue.

    Lastly, did the client pass along the actual device models or anything to you?
