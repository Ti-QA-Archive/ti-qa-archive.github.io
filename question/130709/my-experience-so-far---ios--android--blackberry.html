My Experience So Far - iOS + Android + Blackberry

I thought I'd post my experience so far with Titanium Appcelerator. I'll try to continue to update this post as I continue with my project. Hopefully my 2 cents helps other folks who're just getting started.

So - I decided to jump on Titanium because it offers what seems to be the silver bullet solution to writing apps for 3 platforms, and lets just say its been a wild ride. Comments and criticisms are welcome… of course. Here's a brief overview of writing for each platform:

iOS

  • Easiest to write apps for using titanium
  • Has the most compliance [pro] and the apps are pretty stable
  • Final apps are huuuuge! This is a major [con]. This is due to the Titanium Modules that are part of the package. Code bloat is inevitable with something like Titanium
  • No problems submitting to the Apple app store

Android

  • It seems quite a few elements are missing… the biggest being the Action Bar [con]
  • Android is a pain in the ass to develop for in general. Dealing with screen sizes and densities is a universal pain.
  • Fastdev helps speed up emulator testing with Titanium, but its still slow. So my suggestion is to check your code over again before testing on the emulator. You'll spend plenty of time twiddling your fingers [con/pro]
  • Enable snapshots for your emulator (in eclipse)… unless you want to sit around twiddling your fingers
  • Ti.API.info('xxxx') does not log in Titanium Studio. You'll have to use the Eclipse console log for this
  • V8 & Rhino are both sloooooooow! I don't see much difference
  • Including multiple files will slow your app startup considerably. My suggestion is to combine JS files whenever you can. In addition to that MINIFY the JS and/or use Google's closure closure compiler
  • Tableviews… bane of your apps existence. Not sure why tableviews suck the life out of the app [major con]. Titanium will have to do some work here
  • When using the emulator set your tiapp.xml to install to SD Card directly. The massive sizes of the APK's will guarantee you space issues otherwise
  • Use classnames for tableview rows… improves performance
  • Make absolutely sure that rows sharing a classname are the same type, otherwise you'll encounter force closes

Blackberry

  • You'll need paid membership with Appcelerator to get started
  • Getting Titanium Studio's Blackberry SDK to work will give you plenty of gray hair [con]
  • Windows Only
  • Once you get your dev environment going Blackberry is actually a joy to code and work with [pro]
  • Titanium apps for Blackberry are surprisingly fast, compared to Android
  • The Blackberry Emulator is freaking awesome and makes you feel like you're using a real phone. Every possible function is emulated perfectly
  • Rows in Tableviews work very differently. You'll have to consult the KitchenSink (Blackberry Edition) for this
  • KitchenSink comes with handy log generator code that will be invaluable for Blackberry Dev
  • You'll probably encounter permission issues once you install your app on the emulator, but this is not a big issue

  • Performance testing on Android emulator is not good idea and it does not reflect real performances. To see real performances you must use a device. v8 is a lot faster than Rhino (except TableViews, they perform slower from what I can see). Combining files does not make any difference.

    In general, developing on Android device is much more faster, stable and reliable.
