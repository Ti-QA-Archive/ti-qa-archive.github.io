    • OK, if you look at that log, you'll see these 2 important lines:

      [WARN] "android" folder found in "Resources", but not "app/assets". Not cleaning "Resources" folder...
[WARN] "mobileweb" folder found in "Resources", but not "app/assets". Not cleaning "Resources" folder...

      This means that you have platform-specific folders in your Resources folder (i.e., android, iphone, mobileweb) but you don't have them in your app/assets folder. Alloy will not clean your Resources directory like it usually does on each build if this is the case, in an effort to prevent you from accidentally deleting critical platform-specific files. There's 2 ways to get around this:

      1. Copy the folders (android, ios, mobileweb) from Resources to app/assets. Alloy will then clean Resources out on your next build now that the platform-folders will then be in sync again.
      2. If you don't plan on ever building an android or mobileweb app, you can just delete those folders from the Resources folder. Like option #1, this will sync up your platform-specific folders and Resources will get cleaned out on your next build.

      One thing to note is that this should never occur with new alloy apps. This situation really only occurred if you had an older alloy project and then tried to compile it with a newer version later. This was an old problem and should not ever occur anymore with new alloy apps built with 0.2.42… UNLESS you manually put them out of sync by moving around or deleting platofmr-specific folders from app/assets on your own.
