iOS7 background services terminating far more quickly?

I have working code which uploads large files in 1MB chunks, and when the app goes to background it resumes from where it got to in the foreground.

Previously, in iOS 6 the background process was usually stopped after around 5 minutes, or at least a dozen 1MB chunks. It's a bit annoying that it won't keep going till it's done, but I know there isn't much that can be done, so I can live with it.

Now, on iOS 7, I'm lucky if it does 2 chunks before it is terminated - which makes the entire concept of background uploading pretty much pointless.

Is there any known change to how iOS 7 limits background processes? Is there anything I can do to keep it open longer? Can anything be done with the new background app refresh to keep the app running for longer?
