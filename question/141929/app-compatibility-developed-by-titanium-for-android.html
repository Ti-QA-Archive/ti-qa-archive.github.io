    Hi Zheng,

    Shannon is right, When you are dealing with only one device or one platform then your application runs like a charm but when you are developing application for multiple platform or device then it will need change your coding style for generalization.

    for example:

    1) Do not use deprecated methods or properties in your code.

    2) Do not use platform specific method or property when you dont require.

    3) When you need to code platform specific requirement then you have to separate your code by platform conditions.

    4) Keep your layout general and dynamic, for example when your window or view size changed your view or control size will be also changed according to container.

    5) And at the last do testing on real device. sometime simulator or emulator misguide because of its speed, their speed is different then real device, and alos keep in mind that different device have different speed also.

    For reference, we have Kitchen Sink is the best example, and there is more examples are available for our reference also, you can find those here.

    And for object and layout you can find reference for here, practice makes you more accurate.

    Do coding and do post your error there is more experienced persons are here to help you, you will find more reliable solution from titanium community.
