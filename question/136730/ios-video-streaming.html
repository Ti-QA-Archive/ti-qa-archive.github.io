iOS Video Streaming

Hi have an app that streams video through wowza and it just got rejected due to:

9.4: Video streaming content over a cellular network longer than 10 minutes must use HTTP Live and include a baseline 64 kbps audio-only HTTP Live stream
May 9, 2012 01:38 PM. From Apple.
9.4

We found that your app does not use the HTTP Live Streaming protocol, with a baseline stream of 64 kbps, to broadcast streaming video, as required by the App Store Review Guidelines.

HTTP Live Streaming is required when streaming video feeds over the cellular network in order to provide an optimal user experience and utilize cellular best practices. This protocol automatically determines the bandwidth available to users and adjusts the bandwidth appropriately, even as bandwidth streams change. This allows you the flexibility to have as many streams as you like, as long as 64 kbps is set as the baseline feed.

My code is:

   var activeMovie = Titanium.Media.createVideoPlayer({
                url : contentURL,
                backgroundColor : '#000',
                mediaControlStyle : Titanium.Media.VIDEO_CONTROL_DEFAULT,
                scalingMode : Titanium.Media.VIDEO_SCALING_ASPECT_FILL,
                fullscreen : true,
                autoplay : true
        });

What can I do?
Thank you very much for your help!

  • There's a couple ways you can solve this. Whether or not your app gets rejected because of this is up to the reviewer and every reviewer is different.

    1) Your users will have the best experience if you go ahead and follow their instructions. Just download Apple's media stream tools and follow the instructions for converting your video to HLS.

    2) If for some reason you can't meet their requirements, then you can change your app to only work over Wi-Fi. My apps were streaming live HD video so it only made sense to require a high-speed Internet connection anyways. Here's the steps you need to follow:

    a) Modify your app's description to say that it requires a Wi-Fi connection.

    b) Wherever you use the network or create a video player in your app, add code to ensure that Ti.Network.networkType is not equal to Ti.Network.NETWORK_MOBILE. Also, you need to listen for Ti.Network.change and Ti.App.resume events, and destroy any video players you currently have playing if the network switches to mobile.

    c) In Xcode, add "Application uses Wi-Fi" in the Info tab and set the value to Yes.

    d) When your application gets rejected again for 9.4, reply in the Resolution Center with:
    i) What you did to solve a, b, and c above.
    ii) A note saying you are intentionally requiring Wi-Fi and disabling your app over the cellular network.
    iii) Ask for clarification on how 9.4 still applies to your app.

    • Just noticed you mentioned you're streaming through Wowza. You might want to take a look at this article <http://www.wowza.com/forums/content.php?208-Adding-Multi-bitrate-Audio-only-Rendition-to-iOS-Streams-(AppStore-64Kbps)>, but make sure that you're using 54kbps audio or lower in your video. If you have exactly 64kbps audio or higher, your app will be rejected because the additional overhead of HLS increases the bitrate above 64kbps.
