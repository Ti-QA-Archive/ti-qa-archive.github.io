Small mp3 files do not stream correctly in iOS Simulator (and maybe iPhone devices)

I'm using Ti SDK 3.4.0, iOS Simulator 8.0, and XCode 6.0.1. I have an app that attempts to play remote mp3 files by streaming over url. Here's the code:

var audioPlayer = Ti.Media.createAudioPlayer({ 
    url: 'http://www.myserver.com/mp3/fun.mp3',
    allowBackground: false
});

audioPlayer.start();

This works perfectly for all mp3 files over 40Kb. But any files under 40Kb won't work. I can hear the mp3 start to play for just a split second, then stops. The audioPlayer reports a state of "unknown (9)", then quits, immediately after starting to play.

You may be tempted to think that size has nothing to do with this, but rather CBR vs VBR, mp3 headers, version, layers, channels, sampling rate, khz…etc. But I have been down all those roads on another audio forum, and I can prove that the only delta that makes any difference is simply file size, as mentioned above. I have analyzed many different mp3 files, compared baselines and various attributes, and everything points back to file size, every time. It's a provable thing.

Anyway, WHY is my iOS simulator audioplayer choking on streamed files under 40Kb? And what can I do about it?

Here's an actual example of an mp3 file that's too small to work when streamed/played. Thanks in advance for any ideas.

  • We had similar issues with short mp3s and ended up downloading them to the device and playing with Titanium.Media.Sound rather than stream them with Titanium.Media.AudioPlayer. A bit of a hassle but it works quite well now, although it's a good idea to leave a very slight gap before the audio to give it a chance to load.
