  • I think the question can probably answer itself.

    1) Why do you want to use NoSQL.
    2) What functionality do you need from a NoSQL database.
    3) Anybody developing the application have knowledge of NoSQL.

    If your answer to one is, I don't like SQL, I am following the latest trend, or I really want to use NoSQL.

    Then you probably shouldn't be using a noSQL database.

    If your answer to ? 2 is You have investigated SQL, MongoDB and another NoSQL database and the facilities required are not available in SQL but are say in MongoDB, then use MongoDB.

    I am guessing by your question that your answer to no 3 is NO.

    So going on from that, do you have any SQL knowledge. To apply a solution without knowing the reason has the potential to come back and kick you hard.

    Rainer has given you some usage examples, but at the end of the day your own research has to give you the answers.

    We personally use mongoDB and favour it over the others, for the functionality it has. But your decision needs to be based on your requirements and technical knowledge.

    Hope this helps

    T.
