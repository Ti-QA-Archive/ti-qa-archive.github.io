  • Not really an answer - more an opinion based on having sold solutions that behave in the same way.

    Virtual page turning is a skeuomorphic technique that ends up slowing down the user rather than letting them quickly move from page to page. If your book will have a design that allows for variable text size (recommended) then the concept of an actual page is eroded anyway.

    The average touch device user is comfortable with pages of information sliding from side into the screen, the animations are faster and the 'hit points' are easier to use, plus it makes it easier for visually impaired users to enjoy your material (provided the pages are not simply images).

    I am not suggesting you abandon the page turn animation approach, just decide if the animation is worth the potential negatives.

    BTW Have you seen that Apple now owns a patent on the virtual page turn? Unlikely to cause you to and legal issues - but worth noting.
