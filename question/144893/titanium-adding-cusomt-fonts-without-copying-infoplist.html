  • Take a look at the new tiapp.xml schema in the 2.1 docs. They recently expanded the schema to have a plist section that gets copied over to the plist file at build time.

    Docs
    http://docs.appcelerator.com/titanium/2.1/#!/guide/tiapp.xml_and_timodule.xml_Reference

    This is what my tiapp.xml looks like:
    <ios>
    <plist>
    <dict>
    <key>UIAppFonts</key>
    <array>
    <string>Fonts/HelveticaCd.ttf</string>
    <string>Fonts/HelveticaNeue-Extended.otf</string>
    <string>Fonts/FasterOne-Regular.ttf</string>
    </array>
    </dict>
    </plist>
    <min-ios-ver>4.3</min-ios-ver>
    </ios>

    • btw, you don't have to always run an app with a custom Info.plist to include fonts. You can just use it once, the first time you compile it. After that, the info.plist inside your xcode project will be changed, and then you can rename your custom Info.plist file inside the Ti Project to something else.
