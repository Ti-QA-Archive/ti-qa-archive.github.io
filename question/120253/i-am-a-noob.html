  • 1) do you mean open the web browser? or open a webview? or do you just mean open another window like you would in web development?

    2) absolutely!

    3) 100%

    4) using http posts with a REST API on the server (android and iOS dont support MySQL natively, they do support native SQLite

    I recommend downloading our kitchen sink example app, it will demo all of the functionality you can have with titanium mobile, and also downloading tweetanium mobile as it is a good example of how to structure your apps code.

    We recommend NOT structuring your app like kitchen sink as opening windows using the URL is not the best idea all the time (even though it feels more natural to web developers) what opening a window via a url does is threads your application, and eats up memory, but it also creates a new scope making it hard to access thing in a part of your app outside your current window.

    The links to the two apps I recommended are:
    Tweetanium - https://github.com/kwhinnery/Tweetanium

    Kitchen sink - https://github.com/appcelerator/KitchenSink

    And welcome to Titanium! :)
