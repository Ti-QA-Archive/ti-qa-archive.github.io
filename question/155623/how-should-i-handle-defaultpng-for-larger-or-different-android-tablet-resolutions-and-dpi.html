    • Mine go in the following folders;

      • {appname}/platform/android/res/drawable-ldpi
      • {appname}/platform/android/res/drawable-mdpi
      • {appname}/platform/android/res/drawable-hdpi
      • {appname}/platform/android/res/drawable-xhdpi
      • {appname}/platform/android/res/drawable-xxhdpi

      I do the same, my iOS app splash is more detailed and falls off the edge, but the Android one is a central mass of imagery and solid colour on all edges - using the 9-patch to push out those "margins/borders" for the odd shapes.

    • Your main window has to be a heavyweight window (see here for the details or just add modal=false as a property of your window to make this so).

      Creating the 9-patch images correctly can be a bit of a challenge. Make sure you have just a single, contiguous area of black pixels on the top, left, right, and bottom. You can't mark a few pixels wide, skip a few pixels, and mark another section. Just one stretchable region marked on the top/left, one non-stretchable region marked on the right/bottom.
