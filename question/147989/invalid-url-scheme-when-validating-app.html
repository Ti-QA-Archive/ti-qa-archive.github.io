Invalid URL scheme when validating app

At some point since I last released a version of my app to Apple's iTunes service something has changed in either XCode or Titanium. I used to have no problems publishing my app, but now when doing so Titanium builds the app without a problem, but when I ask XCode to validate my it I get the following error.

The following URL schemes found in your app are not in the correct format: [cowes_co_uk]. URL schemes need to begin with an alphabetic character, and be comprised of alphanumeric characters, the period, the hyphen or the plus sign only. Please see RFC1738 for more detail.

My app is called cowes.co.uk and it seems something somewhere is converting my app name to cowes_co_uk and trying to use that as a URL somewhere. How do I configure/stop this?

I am a Windows/Visual Studio developer guy usually and all of this Apple stuff does my head in…

  • Ok, sometimes, depending on the name of your app, you will need to do a custom info.plist to correct any auto generated names from causing errors.

    — answered February 7th 2013 by Stephen Feather
    permalink

    • In version 3.0.0, we have also had extra CFBundleURLSchemes with garbage chars get inserted into our build info.plist files.

      You can take the info.plist from your build directory, copy it into the root of your project (along side {Resources}) and edit it there. This then gets copied over during the build process (be sure to clean your project)

      Keep in mind though, that some changes in your tiapp.xml may not be reflected later on if you make changes.

      — commented February 7th 2013 by Stephen Feather

    • In the info.plist you are looking at

      <key>CFBundleURLTypes</key>
<array>
    <dict>
        <key>CFBundleURLName</key>
        <string>com.mydomain.app</string>    
        <key>CFBundleURLSchemes</key>
        <array>
            <string>MyAppScheme</string>
        </array>
    </dict>
</array>

      Now, some of these can be placed in the <ios> section of the tiapp.xml, however, I can't guarantee that anyone particular key will be copied over correctly as there have been some issues with custom settings in tiapp.xml in the past.

  • In my case, my app name had (.) periods in it and they were being changed to (_) underscores, which is not valid.

    So…

    In tiapp.xml, I simply changed:

    <name>C.R.A.P.</name>

    to

    <name>CRAP</name>

    Problem solved, at least for me. I hope this helps.
