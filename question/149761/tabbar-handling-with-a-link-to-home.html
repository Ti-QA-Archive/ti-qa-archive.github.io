    Hi

    The reason for the clarification is that it would not be normal to use the tabGroup for this type of interface, as the tabGroup is designed to differentiate between main sections of the app and not a step-by-step style process. Your app "may" fall foul of Apples Human interface app submission rules.

    • iOS UI Element Usage Guidelines

    Typical ways of handling this interface style are;

    • Child windows of a single tab, each having a back button automatically displayed when the next one is shown.
    • A modal window with the same style as above, but in an easy to close window at any point
    • A series of views that are shown or hidden as each is currently required.
    • A series of views added to the scrollableView - each acting as a page in the step-by-step

    • I agree with Malcolm – having a tab navigate you back to a main, tabless window would be very unexpected from the user's perspective. I wouldn't try to go down that road.

      If you do continue down that path, and you do eventually want to support android, be advised that it is difficult to change your tab groups on android. I wrote an article on this subject that you may find helpful.

      IMHO it is a mistake to build for iOS only with "android eventually" – if you want cross-platform support, build it that way from the start. It will be much less painful for you, even if it seems like more work in the beginning. It's much easier to deal with the Titanium platform inconsistencies one at a time than to deal with 10 or 15 all at once. You won't know what is causing the bad behavior on android. Ignore this advice at your own peril.
