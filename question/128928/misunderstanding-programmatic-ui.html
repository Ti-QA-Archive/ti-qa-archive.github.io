(Mis)Understanding Programmatic UI

Yesterday I asked a question about using the forms module that was laid out in Forging Titanium Episode 10. The short story is that the form wasn't being displayed. I could debug the contents of the form object and everything is in place, but it simply didn't show up on the screen.

Today, I found a "fix". It shows up only if I explicitly set a height on the form object. The 'auto' height seems to equate to 0 even though the form has content and that content has height. I've bumped into this many times before and it just hasn't clicked that the container won't expand to fit its content.

When added to a window, as is done in the episode, it works just fine. I'm adding it to a ScrollView. I don't know why there would be a difference or what that difference might be or even whether this is the difference that matters, but it's the only obvious deviation that I can see between what I'm doing and what is happening in the standalone app.

Is there any resource or documentation or grand pearl of wisdom that will help me get past this aspect of the learning curve? I've spent far too many hours on these kinds of problems and would like to come to a better understanding of when I have to set the height and when 'auto' is good enough so that it's not always a matter of trial and error.

Thanks.

  • Auto dimensions are often times not calculated until a view is rendered, or a window is opened. This can throw off any sub-components that are also using auto dimensions. That's why it works when you add it to a window, rather than a sub component of that window.
