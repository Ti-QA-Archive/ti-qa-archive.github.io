    • I can't speak for Appcelerator either, but I suspect all decisions come down to where to allocate limited resources. The last [mobile OS market share analysis1 I saw showed webOS at only 2.4% of the market. That is 1/10 of the share of RIM, though RIM is falling faster than Palm.

      Also consider that even if webOS 3.x "is only a web technology packed into a pseudo native app", that most existing devices won't be on webOS 3.x anytime soon. Last I knew, here in the USA the original Pre and Pixi from Sprint and Verizon could not even be upgraded to webOS 2.x let alone 3.x

      So if a webOS branch of Mobile Web SDK needed 3.x as a minimum requirement (and I confess I do not know if it would), then the 2.4% market share of webOS would be greatly overstated as its potential user base. All of which brings us back to where to allocate limited resources.

      I think we can all agree we have our own pet peeve list of things we wished were already fixed in either the iOS or Android SDKs, so it isn't like they are dealing with unlimited resources here.

      Also, one beauty of open source is that you can fork your own branches. If you feel webOS 3.x support will be trivial to implement, why not do it yourself?
