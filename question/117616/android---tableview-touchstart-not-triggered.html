    • Aruna

      The issue described in ticket #3201 has not been resolved, which is why the ticket is marked "new" (not "resolved" or "fixed-in-qa") at the moment. Again, you don't need to use touchEnabled unless you need to disable click or touch events. Thus, in your case, simply do not use the touchEnabled property.

      touchEnabled - a boolean indicating if the view should receive touch events (true, default) or forward them to peers (false)

      As an example of the above explanation, if you position view1 above view2 and you set touchEnabled:false on view1, view1 will not respond to any click or touch events. Hence the following will not have any effect:

      view1.addEventListener('click',function(){
Ti.AP.info('action not performed');
});

      However, view2 will respond when the area of view1 is clicked, because the event is forwarded to piers:

      view2.addEventListener('click',function(){
Ti.AP.info('action is performed');
});

      Hope this clarifies it.

      Cheers
