  • Hi

    Check out the events;

    • touchstart
    • touchmove
    • touchend
    • touchcancel

    These will help you solve your problem. You detect the touchStart, log that state in memory, determine the touchEnd and then work out if that was above the space occupied by one of the things you are looking for. You could also check for the touchMove of those things you are sliding over after the button touchStart starts.

    Remember to keep in mind the user may not understand your UI needs this process as most will be used to press to see choice, press to select specific choice.
