    Hi

    You are correct about Android - everything now is Google Maps v2 for Android using the new module and v2 API keys.

    For iOS, the answer is not as easy. For iOS 6 or higher the maps are Apple Maps, for iOS everlier than iOS 6 then Google Maps are used.

    Apple does not have a method for returning routing information so people currently add the Google route information to the iOS map, which depending on the iOS version will be Apple or Google.

    HOWEVER; Google licence says that their map information cannot be used without using their map to place the route information on - so in reality adding the map route information onto the standard default map in iOS 6 is a breach of the Google Licence.

    Keeping up? Very strange I know.

    In order to remain compliant with Google terms of use for Google Maps Routing information you should not place it on the default map. There is a good module in the marketplace for $30 that provides Google Maps for iOS - adding it here is fine for all licences.
