  • This isn't a direct answer to your question, but I thought I would provide some insight, since I work at a company that has numerous clients who use various barcode formats.

    Code-39 is probably the simplest barcode format in existence. That can be good in some ways, but it does have many downfalls. The main issue is the lack of the error handling that is present in many other barcode formats. This can cause partial reads or misreads if the barcode is smudged, covered by a thumb, or not properly within the scan window.

    I would suggest either Code-128A or Code-128B, depending on if you require just capital letters or capital and lowercase letters. Code-128 is the de facto standard, since it is ubiquitous in UPC, ISBN, and many other arenas. Code-128 also contains a checksum value to help prevent erroneous scans and has values that are based on the ASCII table.

    I believe there is a barcode module available for Titanium, but last time I checked, it had a price above a certain number of scans. I am not sure if it supports Code-39, but it would be highly doubtful that Code-128 would NOT be supported.

    I hope this answer helps to at least point you in a helpful direction, even if I may have not directed answered it for you.
