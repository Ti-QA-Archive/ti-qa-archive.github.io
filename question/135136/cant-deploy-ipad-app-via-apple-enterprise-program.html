Can't deploy iPad app via Apple Enterprise program

My client has an Apple Enterprise license so they can deploy iOS apps in-house (wirelessly) and not through the App Store. Initially I configured the provisioning profiles linked to 2 specific UDIDs (device ID) so that me and my client could both deploy the app to our ipads for testing. Now we're done testing and ready to go live. I logged into developer.apple.com and redid the provisioning profiles so that unrestricted in-house deployments could be done, not tied to a specific device ID. But it just won't work. I can still deploy to these 2 same devices, but whenever we try to deploy to another device, it starts to install, then pops up the error "Unable to Download Application. 'Daily Report' could not be installed at this time.'"

I have no idea what else to try. I deleted and re-created my App ID, provisoning profiles, and the other stuff, both on Apple site and locally. I don't know what else to try to open this up for general in-house distribution. Once again, we have an Apple Enterprise license. I tried uploading the ipa file to TestFlight, and it shows that only the 2 devices are allowed. But that shouldn't be the case.

Help.

  • Accepted Answer

    You are correct in that you need to use your distribution profile and not your development profile.

    In titanium you can choose to distribute for apple app store. While this may be confusing as you do not intend to actually distribute to the app store, you should click this, select your distribution profile, and after compiling it should show up in Xcode archives for use

    • Andrew, thanks for all your help. It finally worked. It turns out my Keychain Access was jacked up and I had a duplicated entry. I had to clean that up and redo all the keys/certs/profiles, then it worked. It also helped to create a custom Keychain and move everything related to my app into that keychain. THEN I had to make that keychain my Default. It turns out that Titanium (or maybe Xcode) only looks at the default keychain, nothing else. This is in fact what Apple developer support suggested.
