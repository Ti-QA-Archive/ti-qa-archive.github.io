  • You could try your hand at one of the many freelancer focused sites.

    Let me tell you where I come from, as one who hires developers.

    Your account on the Q&A is just over a year old.

    That doesn't scream 1 year of experience, that says one year of exposure to Titanium. Your other known languages (C#, asp, php) are server/desktop centric and not mobile in nature. So there is an experiential disconnect between your background/education and the environment you are attempting to enter and you have to overcome this.

    You hold no Appcelerator Certifications

    This is a bit disconcerting, in that while you have a background in computer engineering, and have studied such in Istanbul, it shows no effort on your part to demonstrate current marketable credentials. Again, a degree from the 80s/90s (heck, even the early 2000's) are so 'old' in terms of comsci, that other than an 'atta boy' for completion, the knowledge learned then often isn't applicable to today.

    You are an unknown in the Titanium community

    With the lack of experience, and the lack of documentation, the third of the three things an employer would look at is 'are you known'. This carries weight only in regards to a lack of formal education, in that you may have community peers who will vouch for your knowledge and understanding. In your case, the answer is, 'no not really'.

    So where do you go next?

    You need to show a body of work

    You need to demonstrate your 'experience'.

    You need to document your credentials

    This means take the Appcelerator exams. Demonstrate that you understand the product and how it operates.

    You need to market yourself

    Generate QUALITY content that benefits the community.
