Force Orientation on Android Crashing

The Problem

I have checked the other questions and have not found a solution.

I have an app that i want to run in landscape mode only. When my app starts it boots in portrait then after the load company splash screen (usually the TI Appcelerator Default.png graphic) the app switches to landscape then crashes immediately. If I open the app and I'm holding the phone sideways landscape works. I can hit back to the home screen and hold my phone upright in portrait, start the app and it crashes again.

Use Case

  1. Hold phone upright in portrait mode.
  2. Start app.
  3. Company splash loads
  4. App attempts to switch to forced landscape mode and crashes
    >12-07 01:02:11.957: E/TiUncaughtHandler(14801): (main) [118,641] Sending event: exception on thread: main msg:java.lang.RuntimeException: Unable to start activity ComponentInfo{com.mycompany.app/org.appcelerator.titanium.TiActivity}: android.content.res.Resources$NotFoundException: Resource ID #0x7f02000f; Titanium 1.7.5,2011/11/02 17:00,ab20af7
  5. Click the "Force Close" button to be returned to the home screen.
  6. Turn the phone sideways for landscape. Start the app. All is fine.
  7. Return to the home screen. Hold the phone in landscape and the app will crash again.

My Config

  • OS X Lion
  • Titanium Studio 1.0.6.201110251616
  • Titanium SDK 1.7.5
  • Android SDK r15
  • Device - Droid 2 running 2.3.3

    In my experience, crash-bugs are often not the fault of the view or what you do to it, meaning: The functionality is not necessarily broken - often it has to do with references. Eg. y some properties cannot be assigned to under certain circumstances, or passing a view as a reference can be rather dangerous (if possible, I'm not sure if I did it).

    Make a dummy app that replicates your scenario in regards to windows, but without any other code whatsoever. If it crashes you know you are looking in the right place - if it doesn't, well … you'll have to take a look on your other code.
