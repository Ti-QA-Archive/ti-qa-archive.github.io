    Ken, Kitchen Sink works with multiple densities because they have

    <supports-screens android:anyDensity="false"/>

    in tiapp.xml. Not the best way, IMO.

    — answered January 5th 2012 by Shawn Lipscomb

    • why not the best way??? shawn please elaborate??

      — commented January 5th 2012 by Ken Crucillo

    • Setting anyDensity to false is considered "old, auto-scaling behavior" by Appcelerator. It's better to "dp" numbers that you want to be auto-scaled, and just use raw numbers when you don't want any auto-scaling. So you end up with things like:

      MyLabel.top='50dp';

      instead of

      MyLabel.top=50;

      More information is available here.

      — commented January 5th 2012 by Shawn Lipscomb

    • that's what im curious about… why is that in kitchen sink.. they dont use dp?? but the scaling is good.. hmm?

      — commented January 5th 2012 by Ken Crucillo

    • In Kitchen Sink, they went with the legacy method of setting anyDensity to false, because Kitchen Sink has been around for a long time (relatively)…from before they made the auto-scaling change. I'm sure they didn't see any benefit to refactoring all of the code in the entire project. Kitchen Sink is not necessarily a "best practice" showcase.

      — commented January 5th 2012 by Shawn Lipscomb

    • Like what i said i dont use kitchensink structure, im just curious about it, so you think if its more better if anyDensity is true?? ..

      — commented January 5th 2012 by Ken Crucillo

    • Yes, IMO, not setting anyDensity at all (letting it default to true) is a better approach, because it gives the programmer better control of what is scaled and what is not scaled. Then use "dp" on your numbers for things that you want scaled.
