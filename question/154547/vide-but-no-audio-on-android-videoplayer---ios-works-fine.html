Vide, but No Audio on Android VideoPlayer - iOS works fine

Hello all,

I'm testing the video player in Titanium and I'm running into an issue. iOS video player works just fine with audio and video. The android video player shows the video fine, but without audio. I've made sure that my media volume is up on my Galaxy nexus, so that's not the issue. Everything is up to date in Titanium and on my Nexus.

Can anyone help? Code pasted below. Thanks!

var win = Titanium.UI.currentWindow;

// vURL is set below. vURL is the remote video url, which is a h.264 mp4 file

var videoPlayer = Titanium.Media.createVideoPlayer({
top : 2,
autoplay : true,
fullscreen : true,
backgroundColor : 'black',
height : 200,
width : 300,
mediaControlStyle : Titanium.Media.VIDEO_CONTROL_DEFAULT,
scalingMode : Titanium.Media.VIDEO_SCALING_ASPECT_FIT
});

videoPlayer.url = win.vURL;
win.add(videoPlayer);
win.open();

    Hi

    Can you confirm the video plays correctly on the device when you do not use the app you are creating?

    Video and Audio have mixed support cross platform and a common issue with partially supported formats is the lack of audio on videos files - this commonly means the audio track embedded in the video is compressed/encoded in a sub-format not supported on the actual device.

    So to determine if this is the case for your problem - type the url in the Chrome browser on your android device. If this video plays here with audio then you will have determine two things;

    • The URL is accessible on the android device (which it should be if you have video)
    • The audio problem is not the device playback ability.

    I am assuming your Android is running Android 3.x or higher as you said it is a Galaxy Nexus.

    Report back.

    • Malcolm,

      Here's what I have found thus far. My Galaxy Nexus (stock 4.2.2) won't play a MP4 with MP3-encoded audio. A MP4 with AAC-encoded audio works fine. I tried the MP4/MP3 combo on a HTC One and it worked, both in Chrome and when opened as an Intent.

      The android docs say that MP3-encoded audio is only supported as file contained as .MP3. MP4/AAC is supported, but not MP4/MP3.

      http://developer.android.com/guide/appendix/media-formats.html

      So ultimately I think its the encoding and not the ao. Thanks for the help.
