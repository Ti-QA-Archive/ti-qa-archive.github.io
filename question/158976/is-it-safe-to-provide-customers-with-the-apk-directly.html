Is it safe to provide customers with the APK directly?

I'm using Titanium to make an app that is available on Google Play. But I have a customer who is interested in paying me for making a version of the app that is tailored specifically to their needs and requesting that they can make the app freely available to their employees. Hence it would result in me providing the compiled APK file directly to them, rather than through Google Play.

But if I do that my concern is now: how easy is it to decompile the APK generated by Titanium? I know everything is decompilable, but the question is how hard is it? Would it take quite a bit of effort to retrieve usable source code, or am I jeopardizing my business by making it relatively simple for someone to make a similar app?

    As easy as any other apk downloaded by the Google Play app store. There is no difference.

    So you better get your paranoia high horse right now and realise that no matter how smart and clever your code may be. Most people won't care.

    and if by chance they were interested in copying some features of your app, they can retro-engineer it as they will. Pretty much like every copycat application out there.
