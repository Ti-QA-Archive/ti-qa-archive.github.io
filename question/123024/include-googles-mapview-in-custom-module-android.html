Include Googles MapView in custom module (Android)

Hi!

I'm working on a custom module for my company's Android app and I'd like to include a simple map displaying a location and some annotations.

What actions do I need to take? Do I need to link/include it somehow? Looking at Google's example, they extend MapActivity. How does this relate to developing a Titanium module?

I have no previous experience in Android development except working with it through Titanium. All I know about activities is that they sort of represent a window.

  • If this is still required …

    You may set it in the manifest section of timodule.xml.

    As per Android docs (i.e. http://developer.android.com/guide/tutorials/views/hello-mapview.html) setting up correct permissions & enable Google maps lib it shall be the <application> tag as a child element.

    • File: timodule.xml
    <android xmlns:android="http://schemas.android.com/apk/res/android">
  <manifest>

    <application>

        <uses-library android:name="com.google.android.maps" />

        <activity
            android:name="ti.modules.titanium.map.TiMapActivity"
            android:configChanges="keyboardHidden|orientation"
            android:launchMode="singleTask"
        />

    </application>

    <uses-permission android:name="android.permission.INTERNET" /> 

  </manifest>
</android>

    That and further info might be found here: tiapp.xml & timodule.xml
