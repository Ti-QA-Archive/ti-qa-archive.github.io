Answer - iPhone - Invalid method passed to UIModule

I've seen this question a lot. I thought I would post a general answer after it took me 2 days to work it out.

When building for a device - Ti works out what modules you need - includes them, and disregards the rest. On the simulator it includes all modules.
In a nutsell - the module you are calling has not been included.

The first thing to try is doing a full rebuild (heaps of post on this around).

If you're like me - and that doesn't work:
For some reason Ti hasn't included the needed module.

The fix (until Appcelerator fix the bug):
In you app.js at the top - add dummy components of your problematic calls to the global namespace, so they can be detected easily by the build script.

preWin = Ti.UI.createWindow({});

preView = Ti.UI.createView({});
preWin.add(preView);

preTableView = Ti.UI.createTableView({
  style:Ti.UI.iPhone.TableViewStyle.GROUPED,
  selectionStyle:Titanium.UI.iPhone.TableViewCellSelectionStyle.NONE,
  separatorStyle:Ti.UI.iPhone.TableViewSeparatorStyle.NONE,
});
preWin.add(preTableView);

preLabel = Ti.UI.createLabel({});
preWin.add(preLabel);

//etc etc etc

For those with just a blank screen, or stuck at startup - you can debug your device with Xcode:
with your iPhone still plugged in - Open Xcode
Go to Window > Organiser
Select your device from the left
From the drop down list - Select 'Console'.
Here you'll get the debugging info you need.

Hope this is helpful!
