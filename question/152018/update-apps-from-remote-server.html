  • Accepted Answer

    I'm afraid this won't fly with Apple's standards. Since all application updates must go through the App Store.

    When you think of it, it kind of makes sense. Otherwise, you could have an application that complies with all the rules for the app store and it would be published. After that, (without any knowledge from Apple) someone could update the app and engage in illicit behavior for example. Hence the reason behind this limitation.

    Hope this clears this up for you.

    • Even google has moved to frowning upon outside updates to app obtained through Play.

      You could have an update system in place that feeds your app configuration changes.

      We do that in a number of our apps. App checks our servers, and if a new config file is available, it downloads it and uses it instead of the one it shipped with.

      Text, images, etc. Theoretically you could change the facebook access_token, or required permissions.

      You will want to be wary of how/what you change, as a man in the middle attack could change all your client apps in the wild.
