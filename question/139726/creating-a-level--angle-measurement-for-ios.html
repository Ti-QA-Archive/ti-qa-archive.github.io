Creating a level / angle measurement for iOS

I need to create a feature in my app that can tell me the angle that the device is tilted, preferably over a camera live view (so the user can align the tilt to match something in the real world), and get a "reading".

I looked at the Accelerometer example in Kitchen Sink, but it's a bit confusing as it's a continous readout of x, y, z numbers.

I am guessing that the rotation of the device while you are holding it vertically in front of you and looking through the camera is the X axis? What exactly do the readout numbers mean, degrees from what?

Are there any examples of a level or something similar?

Also, any examples of superimposing items over a live view?

SDK 2.1ga, iOS 5.1

  • The Accelerometer values can be a bit confusing. But it's basically a value between -1.0 and 1.0 - where 0.0 to 1.0 is 90 degrees. and so on.

    eg: In the KitchenSink example, hold your device absolutely upright for the z value to be 0.0. Tilt it on its back and it will be -1 when flat. Tilt it forward and it will go to (plus) 1.0 when level.
