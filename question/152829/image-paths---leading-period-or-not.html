Image paths - leading period or not?

I've just been porting an iPhone app to android, and not one of the images would display, until I removed the leading period the previous developer had put before every image path.

I've seen this in a few people's code, so am wondering what the leading period is doing and is it really required? Am I going to regret removing it throughout the app?

This works on iPhone but no images display on Android

Ti.UI.createView({
     backgroundImage: './graphics/fileName.png'
});

This works on both iPhone and Android

Ti.UI.createView({
     backgroundImage: '/graphics/fileName.png'
});

I also tried without the leading slash but that didnt work. So:

.&#x2F;graphic&#x2F;fileName.png - Doesn't work on Android

graphic&#x2F;fileName.png - Doesn't work on Android

&#x2F;graphic&#x2F;fileName.png - Works on Android

    Hi

    This question is very easy and very hard at the same time.

    The easy bit;

    /folder/filename.png

    This is how you should reference your images to be safe in all your scripts. This way it will resolve the path to the root of your resources folder. Do not use the . or ./, it just complicates things.

    Now for the hard part;

    Folder can be physical (they are where they say they are) or virtual (they change depending on the platform and resolution. This can be VERY good as you can provide imagery that perfectly matches the specific properties on the device the app is running on.

    To help you understand this, I wrote a very simple to understand answer a while back on another question and included many examples of how this works. The examples or for both Android and iPhone/iPad so should answer all of the questions you have including the small tweaks you can make to your tiapp.xml
