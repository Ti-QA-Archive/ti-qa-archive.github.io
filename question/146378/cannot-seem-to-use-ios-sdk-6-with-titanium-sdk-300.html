    Hi

    You have not said where you cannot use iOS 6; does not appear on a menu, your simulator does not show up with 6.0 option? Can you provide a little more detail please.

    Just in case though it may be that as you upgraded and you are more likely to have an existing project - you may simply not have swapped over the choice.

    In the test drop down (same one you use to run in the simulator), use the bottom menu choice to change settings (no idea of actual caption - not near a dev machine).

    From here use or select the iOS choices and there will be a drop down for iOS version, this may (on yours) have two options, with the current one being 5.0 or similar. Change to 6.

    If this does not show then Titanium does not know you have the new Xcode and you may need to convince it.

    See how the setting change goes and report back.

    • Have you had Xcode in a location other than the default?

      Try this in terminal;

      sudo xcode-select -switch /Applications/Xcode.app/Contents/Developer/

      This "should" convince your mac where the Xcode install is - provided it is in the same location as this sudo path.

      Re-start studio afterwards and try again.
