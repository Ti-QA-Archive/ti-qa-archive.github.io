512x512 iTunes icon - is this for marketing

Hi,
When uploading your icons/screenshots to iTunesConnect.com -> it asks:

>A large version of your app icon that will be used on the App Store. It must be at least 72 DPI and a minimum of 512x512 pixels (it cannot be scaled up). It must be flat artwork without rounded corners.>

That means I can submit something larger? What's meant by flat artwork?
Is this meant to be an oversize png file of your appicon or can it be something more useful with a bit more information?

Thanks

  • Accepted Answer

    A PNG version of your app icon will suffice. You need to create a 512x512 version in Photoshop (or Pixelmator) and save as PNG or JPG. There are also several apps in the Mac App Store that let you drag/drop a PSD file and it creates all of the required versions for you…
