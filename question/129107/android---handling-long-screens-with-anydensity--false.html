Android - handling 'long' screens with anyDensity = false?

All, I know that by setting anyDensity = true, and using dps, a fair amount of sizing and positioning issues can be handled manually. However, I am working on an app where most everything already sizes perfectly with anyDensity set to 'false' (i.e., 'the old way' before 1.7). The only big issue is for 'long' screens (e.g., HQVGA, etc.) where the additional screen height leaves some empty space above the tab bar and some entry controls like searchBars have their heights doubled.

Did anyone develop an approach to handling this type of situation?

Thanks.

  • Hi Brian I use a simple approach. Take the overall height and density and then set a value starting at 1 then for say a 480 wide screen = 2, then divide your dimensions by this value. This resizes high res images down for low res screens and positions everything as it should. I also almost always use height settings for full size screens and views. This approach also works on IOS saving having to have 2 sets of images and bar heights on low res screens which are too big etc. Oh it also works for setting font sizes and positioning.

    Hope this helps

    T.
