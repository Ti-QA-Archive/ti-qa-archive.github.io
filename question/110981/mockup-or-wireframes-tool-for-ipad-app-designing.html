  • I find the best UI mockup tool is Apple's Keynote (part of iWork). There's plenty of pre-made kits available for use right away in your mockup. Furthermore, you can make advanced graphics in Keynote, then once you are done, you can even copy&paste the graphics into a vector program and produce pixel accurate graphics for your app.

    You can even do mockup animations, what else can I say? It's the best.

  • I've gone through quite some of those 21 wireframe tools, but the only one that really got my attention was BluePrint for the iPad. If you have an iPad, you should give it a look. IMHO it is the ideal tool to work together with other (non-programming) participants of a project, or even your customer. The best thing about it, is that you can quickly set up clickable links, that gives a good idea of the user experience. The viewer part of the app is free and the lite version for trying out is free as well.

    Full version
    http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/blueprint/id405203705?mt=8

    Lite version
    http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/blueprint-lite/id407188253?mt=8

    Viewer version
    http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/blueprint-viewer/id411622447?mt=8

    I'm in no affilated with the developer, I just had a lot of fun (and success) with it the last days

    • I'll throw in a second recommendation for BluePrint. I didn't need the xcode export functionality that the others offered. Blueprint is nice and fast, easy to use, and it is very easy to export either the project for viewing on the iphone (free app required) or to export images for a client to look at. It only creates iPhone scaled designs for now, but since that is 90% of what we do, its a great little help.

      Can meet a client, and while one of our guys is chatting about details, the other is throwing together a mockup. Was well worth the price.
