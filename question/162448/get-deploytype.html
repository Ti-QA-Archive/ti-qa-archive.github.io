  • As you use Alloy, you can also use the Alloy constants:

    ENV_DEVELOPMENT
ENV_TEST
ENV_PRODUCTION

    These are booleans.

    To determine the deployType there is also a constant

    deployType

    wich returns the same as Ti.App.getDeployType()

      @Preety, when I read your question again another better way of distinguishing between production and development URLs comes to my mind. You could just add them to the config.jsonof your Alloy project:

      "env:development": {
   "BASE_URL": "http://your-dev-environment.com/",
   "API": "http://api.your-dev-environment.com/"
},
"env:production": {
   "BASE_URL": "http://your-live-environment.com/",
   "API": "http://api.your-live-environment.com/"
}

      In your code you can easily access the URL with:

      Alloy.CFG.API

      It will automatically pick the webservice-URL of your according environment.
