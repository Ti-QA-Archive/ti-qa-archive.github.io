Why are the size of Titanium Showcase apps so small?

All the apps we develop using Titanium are at least 5.5MB in size. I understand this is because of the Ti SDK. However, if I take a look at the size of these Showcase Apps in their market/store they are so small. For example, dialfaster (1.2MB), Oil Reporter (1.64MB), Tradui (1.52MB) on the Android Market.

Does this mean they have abandoned Titanium and gone native development environment?

  • I believe it is because they manually remove the libraries not needed.
    e.g. when you build with xcode, you could look into all the frameworks added and the third party libraries in the resource folder.
    Removing all that will reduce the app size quite drastic.

    • I have only done this with xCode, but I could imagine that you would take a duplicate of your current SDK folder (1.8.2), and stripping the android side down. And clean build with that.
      When you do a clean build, Titanium uses a python script to include a bunch of .jar files:
      Library/Application Support/Titanium/mobilesdk/osx/1.8.2/android/modules/

      Titanium is working on their API will be manually required by javascript in the future, but for now everything is included.
