Success story and frustration with Appcelerator

I have got my first app approved for the AppStore. The Android version is finished and is about to be published on the Android Market.

It has all been done in about 6 weeks (including review time in the AppStore). I knew when I started out that I was on a tight schedule. The choice fell on Titanium for being cross platform and what looked like easy to get up to speed for someone that never worked with iOS or Andriod development.

It worked out and the apps were done in time. Success!

What didn't work out was the cross platform part. We started out the iOS version as that was most important. Half way through we almost got the Android version up to par (only some messaging was not perfect). When the iOS version were finished we realized that we couldn't get the Android version to work reliably with events between threads. To save the project we ended up doing a fork and rewriting the whole structure. We were out of time to make big changes in the iOS version and we now have to maintain two projects…

The biggest disappointment is however with Appcelerator as a company. As we were pressed for time I sent an inquiry for premium support as soon as we got started. My initial message included a question about available plans and the pricing. After about two weeks I received this:

Hi Peter,
My name is Jeaninne Moya and I am contacting you for two reasons;
first to introduce myself.  I am part of your account team.  
Secondly, to follow up with you regarding the additional information you 
requested about Titanium. 
How we can support you?

Best Regards,
Jeaninne Moya
Account Manager
Appcelerator, Inc.
650-318-5134

I replied a few hours later with the same question as in my initial contact:

Thank you for your email.

I’m interested in the premium support plans.
How do you sign up for these and what is the pricing?

This was on March 25th. After that I haven't heard anything at all. I can live with some bugs in the software, but having to see info about premium support every time I compile from a company that doesn't even respond (at least not within 4 weeks) with how much it costs. If it is too expensive for small companies why not post a price indication on the web site?
