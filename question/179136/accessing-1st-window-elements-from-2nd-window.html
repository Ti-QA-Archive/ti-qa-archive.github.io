  • In short, no you can't have windows as children of views, except in the case of TabGroups.

    If you must use a custom tab solution and the content of your tabs is anything orther than trivial, then you're going to need to manage memory very carefully, particularly on Android.

    When off-screen, consider removing any listeners, clearing table / list data, removing maps, unloading images etc. and reinstating them when the tab is selected and visible again. It's clunky and not much fun to debug.

    Honestly though, the native android tabs component does a much better job of this and unless there some really compelling reasons not to, I'd use it.
