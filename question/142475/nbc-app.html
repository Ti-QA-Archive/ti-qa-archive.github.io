  • I assume that you mean the NBC app throughout the post even though you refer to it as ABC. The NBC app is not open source, so one can only speculate as to how its built, but lets tackle your questions one at a time.

    1) The tabs along the bottom look like the TabGroup on iOS. It is either a TabGroup with a custom AndroidManifest.xml that moves the tabs from top to bottom, or it is a simple custom made navigation bar made up of views and buttons that mimic the iOS TabGroup.

    2) It looks like a window yes. When you open a new window within the current TabGroup, the tab that the window was opened in stays highlighted.

    3) Their app is entire native. Not webviews or web UI. Every screen is composed of Ti.UI components.

    4) No, they created a loading screen along with loading indicators for smaller content areas.

    All your questions will always be better answered by firing up the IDE and writing some code. Find some features that you'd like to create/re-create and focus on doing it. Only then will you truly understand what can and can't be done.

    • Android does remove the tabs when a new window is opened. So, that would lead one to believe that they have either created a custom implementation of the TabGroup, or they are opening windows that are not the full height of the screen, leaving the bottom visible.

      As for images, you can pull images from anywhere, they don't have to be within the app.

      var img = Ti.UI.createImageView({
    image:'http://mywebsite.com/images/image.png'
});

      Their entire app is actually powered by their own API. That is how they grab updated shows and schedules. They also have a proprietary video streaming server that streams the video into their app. The ability to talk with services via APIs is what makes most of the best apps on the market so great.
