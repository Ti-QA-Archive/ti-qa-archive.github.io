    Hi Richard. Embrace yourself! What you are trying to acomplish is a nightmare's tale: Working hand to hand with iOS and android is frustrating enough… leaving it for last will be a journey of its own. You will come to understand the motto "Code once, debug everywhere"

    That being said: some pointers:

    • Try to change all of your object.getProperty() for object.property. Titanium creates the methods for the object upon creation depending on the os, so you won't view.getAnchorPoint in Android for example.

    • Try to move all your object.setIphoneOnlyProperty({}) to the constructor if possible. If not, prepare your self to fill your way with if ( 'iphone' === Ti.Platform.name ) object.setIphoneOnlyProperty()

    • If you have some things in JSS, move them back into the app itself. Some objects just DON'T RENDER if they are fed with a JSS property they don't support (ex. left property Ti.UI.TextField object on Android << this one kept me insane for a whole week!!! )

    • Look for os specific folders workarounds (ex. Android has externalStorage)

    • android emulator doesn't support geolocation, so have an android device at hand

    • android needs heavyweight windows to properly work with the back button

    • Don't expect the same look and feel! remember that they are Different devices, with different philosophies. Honor them

    • PickerView, DashboardView, CoverflowView, ScrollableView are some example of iOS specific components. Abandon them on android or be prepared to do the component manually yourself!

  • This will all depend on any iOS specific UI elements you might be using on your app.

    Also, some elements act slightly differently.

    First, check for Ti.UI elements which have 'iPhone' in the name, them work back from there.

    Some people like to have a single codebase for iOS and Android and use the API to determine which functions to use. I prefer to make a separate project and do it that way.

    Really, it all depends on the complexity of your app interface.
