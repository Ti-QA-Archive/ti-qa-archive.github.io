Determine orientation of recorded video

Hi. I'm working on an app which allows users to upload videos they've taken. All the functionality works, except that videos taken in portrait format are converted, via FFmpeg and rotated 90 degrees.

There's a workaround for this bug when taking photos, but is there a way to sort this for videos? The built-in video recording app works fine, but I'm not sure if that's at the app or the upload level (ie: videos uploaded to YouTube have the correct orientation).

Any help would be massively appreciated.

  • Apple's tools (video players, clients, etc.) all know how to interpret their own orientation status. Other clients, obviously, do not - as you have learned. The best way to handle this is to determine if a movie is rotated and then use a tool like ffmpeg or mplayer to rotate. Here's a script to show you the rotation setting inside a movie: https://github.com/danielgtaylor/qtrotate. Using ffmpeg's filters (-vf) can make short work of the rotation and scaling issues.
