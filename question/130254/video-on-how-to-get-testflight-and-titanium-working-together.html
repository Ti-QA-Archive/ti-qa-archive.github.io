  • Rio, thank you for your time in throwing this together. The Adhoc system from Apple can be confusing at times (ok, the entire signing, provisioning system can get messy. I manage a large number of apple accounts with a multitude of titles under each of them. I'm covered over with .prov files in case Titanium forgets one)

    Testflight is so much more than simply a distribution platform for adhoc builds, though. Integrating their API (whether directly in xCode, or via the TestFlight module in titanium allows developers to set checkpoints, giving them knowledge about the actual adhoc usage/testing. The API also allows developers to query users at those checkpoints to gather immediate feedback. If you are going to the trouble/time of setting up Testflight and a testing group, its worth the $9 to buy the module from the market and do your testing properly.

    Testflight (w/ the api via module) allowed us to see that nearly half our "testers" only ever hit maybe 5% of the functionality of our apps. A large portion of those only ever installed and launched the app, never actually doing anything other than trying to get props for being a "tester". (yeah, no longer testing)
