I'm new to Titanium and I want to start developing an app for both Android and iOS. Is there any downside of just jumping straight onto 3.2.0? The only thing I've noticed so far is that the Alloy examples were designed for 3.1.2

    Alloy framework is designed to support MVC architecture in Titanium and it was started with 3.x. There is no downside of jumping straight to 3.2 and its the latest General Availability got released just this week and you are good to go with it. If it says version number it doesn't mean that it works only to that version it mean it works for that version and higher of that.

    here is the doucmentation for Alloy incase you need it http://docs.appcelerator.com/titanium/3.0/#!/guide/Alloy_Framework.
