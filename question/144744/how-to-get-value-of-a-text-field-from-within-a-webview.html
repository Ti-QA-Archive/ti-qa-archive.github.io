  • Once you have the OAuth tokens required after authentication, it's far easier to use the Twitter API to get the actual data about the user (including avatar and cover image) and construct some real views and labels to display it how you like.

    If you really must do it the way you are suggesting, please look at the evalJS function for webViews to manipulate the HTML of the returned webView html. (This must be called after the 'load' event of the webView).

    I do not recommend your approach, as your app will break as soon as Twitter decides to chnage any of their HTML. (As they often do.)
