  • Group your right buttons in a View.
    tested on 2.1.4GA

        var view = Ti.UI.createView({
        backgroundColor:'yellow',
        width:'100%',
        height:'100%',
    });
    self.add(view);

    var rightButtonBar = Ti.UI.createView({
        backgroundColor:'green',
        width:Ti.UI.SIZE,
        height:Ti.UI.SIZE,
        top:0,
        right:0,
        layout:'horizontal'
    });
    view.add(rightButtonBar);

    var leftButtonBar = Ti.UI.createView({
        backgroundColor:'blue',
        width:Ti.UI.SIZE,
        height:Ti.UI.SIZE,
        top:0,
        left:0,
        layout:'horizontal'
    });
    view.add(leftButtonBar);

    var btn0 = Ti.UI.createButton({
        title:'button0',
        whichObj:'button0',
    });
    leftButtonBar.add(btn0);

    var btn1 = Ti.UI.createButton({
        title:'button1',
        whichObj:'button1',
    });
    rightButtonBar.add(btn1);

    var btn2 = Ti.UI.createButton({
        title:'button2',
        whichObj:'button2',
    });
    rightButtonBar.add(btn2);

    • From what I see in the example above, the buttons arent actually floating right, but because the width of the view is overridden with Ti.UI.SIZE (instead of Ti.UI.FILL) the view will grow to the size of its content. Since it is positioned with right:0 it will have the appearance (and effect) of them being right aligned (The buttons are left aligned in the container that grows to the width of its content and is positioned by its right anchor). Your issue is that you dont have width set to Ti.UI.SIZE (it defaults to Ti.UI.FILL) and the buttons are doing as they should, being placed horizontally starting at their container's left side (which is effectively at left:0).
