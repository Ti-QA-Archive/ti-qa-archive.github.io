    • Doug

      Presumably, if someone creates modules to provide the respective functionality of the TiPlus modules, and offers them to the community as open source projects, then everyone can add to them and benefit from them. Just as with any open source project, this does not necessarily mean that it will be integrated into the core product. Those projects can, and usually do, exist independently. There are plenty of examples with a multitude of open source projects where this is the case.

      Inevitably, the functionality and aims of some of those projects will overlap. This is typical; the community will decide, by a process of natural selection, which project should succeed in the longterm.

      "because the current SDK cannot do it, will at times become obsolete if the feature(s) exposed get added to the base SDK"

      Yes, this is also quite typical of these types of projects - without commercial support by the vendor, the underlying API can change unexpectedly. That is an issue for the 3rd party open source project to overcome and manage.

  • We will be officially launching Titanium+Plus in February, when we'll have all the details on our roadmap going forward. That said, here are the basics:

    • The Apcelerator Titanium SDK will continue to be available as it always has, freely available and licensed under the Apache 2 license. In almost every case, you can think of the features in Titanium as equivalent to the features contained in the iOS or Android SDKs. I say 'almost' as there are a select few modules, like Facebook, that will remain in the core Titanium SDK.

    • Titanium+Plus is a subscription to premium modules that add value to your applications. These modules are supported, updated, and maintained by the Appcelerator team as part of a Titanium Professional or Enterprise subscription.

    • We will continue to add modules to this subscription. Think of it as something akin to Netflix. You get access to a full library of modules as part of the subscription. You do not need to purchase modules separately. We have a roadmap of dozens, if not hundreds of these modules coming down the pike.

    More information will be coming over the next couple weeks, but hopefully this answers some the questions above. I'll also publish an FAQ later this afternoon.
