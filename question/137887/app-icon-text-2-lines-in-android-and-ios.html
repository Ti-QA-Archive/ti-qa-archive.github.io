App icon text 2 lines in Android and iOS?

I would like to set my app's icon label to be on two lines so that the entire app's name is shown. According to Android docs, you can use \n in the text of the <name> property of AndroidManifest.xml, but when I put \n in the <name> property of tiapp.xml, the \n becomes put of the text. Example:

<name>My \n App</name> becomes My\nApp on my device

Where do I change the name so that the AndroidManifest has the correct value to create the correct text?

Also, can you create two line app names for iOS? If so, how?

  • One way to achieve this is to include part of your AndroidManifest.xml in the android section of your tiapp.xml.

    Here is an example of what it would look like:

    <android xmlns:android="http://schemas.android.com/apk/res/android">
        <manifest android:versionCode="1" android:versionName="1.1">
            <application android:debuggable="false"
                android:icon="@drawable/appicon"
                android:label="My\nApp" android:name="MyandroidApplication">
                <activity android:configChanges="keyboardHidden"
                    android:label="My\App"
                    android:name=".MyandroidActivity" 
                    android:theme="@style/Theme.Titanium">
                    <intent-filter>
                        <action android:name="android.intent.action.MAIN"/>
                        <category android:name="android.intent.category.LAUNCHER"/>
                    </intent-filter>
                </activity>
            </application>
            <supports-screens android:anyDensity="true"
            android:largeScreens="true" 
            android:normalScreens="true" 
            android:smallScreens="false"/>
        </manifest>
    </android>

    Notice the MyandroidApplication and MyandroidActivity names, these will be different for every single application. The way to retrieve those for your own app is to build (successfully) your application. Once this is done, simply go into your &#x2F;build&#x2F;andriod directory and edit your AndroidManifest.xml file. The identifiers are in this file.

    As for iOS, it is not feasible unfortunately. (Even using native Xcode)
