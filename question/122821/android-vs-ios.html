Android vs. iOS

I'm curious what the "policy" at appcelerator is in regards to supporting both the iPhone as well as Android devices.

It seems as if iOS is the preferred platform (and I don't blame you since I prefer that platform as well), but I'd like to make sure that, going forward, Android doesn't fall by the wayside.

I recently released the first version of our app for the iPhone, and I have since started the process of porting it over to Android, only to learn that there are a heap of issues. Several things that worked on iOS just don't work on Android - and I'm not talking about functionality that is only available on the iPhone obviously.

So I'm left wondering whether appcelerator is committed to providing the same quality of support to Android as it does to iOS?

Thanks.

  • Accepted Answer

    iOS and Android are currently and will continue to remain first class citizens. This will not change. Some people think that we prefer iOS, but the truth is iOS prefers us (figuratively speaking). With iOS the incidentals are always the same. Hardware, firmware, screen size, etc etc. It is not as hard to maintain as a system with hundreds of variants in hardware requirements, software, and screen sizes like Android. We put forth full effort into covering all these bases, but the fact of the matter is, there are tons of them. But you can rest assured that Android and iOS will continue to be full priorities.
