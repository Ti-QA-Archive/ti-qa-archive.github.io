Why is Aptana development is slow moving ?

I was a solid fan once for Aptana studio in my Rails 2.x days and I had really loved it as an Editor. For the past year and a half I was into different technologies and now landing back to Rails, well this time its version 3.2. When I thought of turning back to my favorite editor, I see it has lot open issues (around 1500+). They all are in the JIRA pipeline to be resolved.

Logically & in layman's terms, this decides the image of Appcelerator as a company, and having 1500+ open issues on one of their own product/editor is not nice. Please Appcelerator team, its high time to sort things out with Aptana and fast or bring it down totally.

Question: What is the rationale behind all this ?

  • Thank you for your feedback. Two thoughts:

    1) Open issues is not the same as open bugs. Many of the issues that people report are improvements or feature enhancements. I would actually be concerned if people weren't suggesting new items. Taking a brief look at other popular open source projects, many have large, vibrant bug databases.

    2) For a reasonable amount of time the bug base was not being well managed, so there are still some amount of old, invalid or duplicate bugs still in the pile. We've fixed more than 500 bugs this year alone on Aptana Studio, so we are actively bringing that number in line. Since being acquired by Appcelerator, we've addressed over 2000 features, improvements and bugs across Titanium Studio and Aptana Studio, and we've had monthly releases of both products.

    All that said, we can't work on every issue with equal priority. Is there a bug in particular that you are interested in?
