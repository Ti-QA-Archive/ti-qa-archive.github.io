    • in Android you have the 9-patch system, where any file named .9.png is preferred over it's normally-named counterpart: test.9.png should be chosen before test.png.

      Ti does the opposite

      You can also put a generic image, a platform specific image and a platform/resolution specific image in your folder structure. Ti will always take the generic first, then the platform-specific and lastly the platform/resolution specific. This means that if you want specific images for one or two resolutions, you have to do ALL resolutions, totally counter-productive and wiping out 95% of the advantages of the native Android image system.
