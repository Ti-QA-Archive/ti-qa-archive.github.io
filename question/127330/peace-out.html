  • Hi Jason.

    You're the second one in short time leaving Titanium for similar reasons.

    While I agree that Titanium has a lot of basic core issues that can make new developers frustrated, I don't agree that help from community is poor. You asked two questions, one was asked 12 hours ago, second a day ago. Both were badly formed … you need to understand that people can't fix anything if there is no enough information and in many cases if they can't reproduce bad behavior. We all have everyday work and it's our good will to help you or other community members. That said, you need to understand that questions like "this doesn't work" or "help me with this" would take a lot of time and effort to get to some solution. To get good answer, the question must be asked in a proper way: http://catb.org/~esr/faqs/smart-questions.html

    On the other hand, there are guys from Appcelerator that are hired for answering question on Q/A (pretty cool, right) among other things, so they care about problems people from community are facing. Their responses are OK (could be better in some cases, though), but even they can't help you if you provide limited information about your issues. You are not the only one here asking for help, there are a lot of other developers asking question and providing better information about their issues. So, if they have limited time to answer questions, I think it's reasonable that they won't spend much of their time on questions that would take a lot of time and effort to get resolved (it's better to answer to 10 well formed question than to 1 badly formed - don't look at things from personal standpoint).

    So I think it's a bit frivolous from you to leave, I think you should be more patient. Titanium is getting better. I've been using it since 1.5 and I had moments like you have now (I regretted why I didn't choose PhoneGap, or even better - why I didn't develop native application), but I looked to the future (and managed to solve issues that I had) and I saw positive steps in Titanium.
    My application works pretty well at the moment, I'm working on application that will be used by field workers in the company I work for, for the input of large data sets that will be transferred over the net and be synchronized with our online database. At the moment, I have 850 files (each file is one component) and have developed complex forms and layouts that works well. So, complex applications can be developed with Titanium, that's for sure.

    I'm happy to work with Titanium, I love to program in JavaScript and I don't want to return to PHP (which is my first programming language love). What I don't like about Titanium is that a lot of their core functionality don't work and that is not being fixed quick enough. From engineering point of view, that is unacceptable (for example, Chromium dev team fixed core issue in 1 day, although it was not top priority and the fact that they have around 30 000 issues opened). The thing is, guys from Appcelerator listen and response in a right way. I think they understand existing issues in Titanium and I think and hope that in the future we will see improvements in Titanium core functionality. :)

    • Cloud building seems like a two-edged sword. On the one hand, it has the potential to mean you can create targets your own development machine cannot. Such as build an iOS target from a Windows machine, or a Blackberry build from an OSX machine.

      On the other hand, not everyone will be comfortable with their source being shipped off somewhere else and the implications it has. Also, even if you can build for other targets you are faced with harder debugging.

      The only way I can think of to "debug" an iOS app under Windows would be to litter the code with info() and debug() statements to the console (as we did in Ti Developer days) then run the Windows version of the iPhone Configuration Utility, hook up the device using USB, and use the console tab to view the messages displayed.
