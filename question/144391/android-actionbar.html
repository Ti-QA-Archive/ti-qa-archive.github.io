    • Couple of thoughts:

      1. The action bar was released with honeycomb and at the time, the idea from Google was that it was to be a tablet only design. Then magically it is to be the new UI direction on all devices. So a form factor only solution to a platform solution.

      2. Actionbar is a lot more difficult to implement with Titanium than you might be aware. Titanium was designed to be built upon an established theme and structure, and upon it we add our native elements. Actionbar must be declared before that, which means the way Titanium generates its app foundation had to change.

      3. Backwards compatibility is a problem as old android versions must be supported and as such their theme/foundation is considerably different than the way an Actionbar is implemented.

      4. If you take the time to go through the JIRA, you will find that most of these concerns HAVE been discussed in the comments and with Appc engineers no less.

      5. Google is again looking to change the way its UI works, and this means another modification.
