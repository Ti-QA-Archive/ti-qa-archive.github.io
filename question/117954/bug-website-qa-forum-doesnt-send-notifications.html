    • It's not that you bringing up the issue is not appreciated - in fact, your input is essential. However, it's always best to discuss it first to see what other peoples' experiences are. One ticket raised in error may be a trivial issue in your opinion, but the management involved for 100s of tickets that are either incorrect or duplicated or poorly-described or contain paragraphs of opinions rather than facts, unnecessarily consume peoples' time. This diverts resources away from what we all want the core team to be doing - writing code.

      If people abuse the ticketing system, by which I mean they do not follow the requested procedure, then it will become a closed system. I really, really, do not want that to happen, as then we would lose the many tickets raised by the community that are of a very high quality.

      Note that the Q&A posts you reference are not really relevant - they are over 5 months old, and the system certainly has been working since then. Hence, this is a new issue. Also, you raised your ticket before you posted to the Q&A. This is not the best approach, as is explained in the Contributing to Titanium guide.

      I hope you can appreciate this point of view, which aims to make the best use of everyone's time, even if it is not how it could ideally work if there were unlimited resources.

      Thanks for your understanding.
