      — commented September 11th 2014 by Nick Milner

    • That documentation is just saying that properties occupy RAM as soon as the application starts up and the entire time it is running. So don't put 20MB of data into properties, because you will probably slow down your startup, and you will definitely increase the memory footprint of your application.

      Based on your question title, you only want to store some small variables. Properties are perfect for this. I would think that you could store up to a couple hundred small strings, numbers, or boolean flags before you would even have to think about performance issues.

      The properties are definitely persistent. The only time they go away is when the app is uninstalled from the device.
