      — commented November 27th 2013 by Stephen Feather

    • Apps have a GUID.
      Modules have a GUID.
      You have an account.

      When you purchase a module, module is associated with your account.
      In theory, all the apps associated with your account should be able to use all the modules associated with your account. Unless, for whatever reason, the winds blew wrong, the moon was full, an app created before you 'bought' a module doesnt get the permissions. So, you change the GUID of the app, appc sees it as a 'new' app, and the module works.

      Module is sold through the marketplace. Then it is pulled, author throws it on github. If he uses the same GUID as what was in the marketplace, then when TiVerify runs, it sees the module as the one from the marketplace, and you may or may not receive the warning.

      If the author changes the guid of the module he releases, then TiVerify sees it as an unknown, and untracked module, you are not prevented from using it, and all works well.

      If you have the source code to a module the author did not change the guid in, you can get the source code, change the guid, and build it out yourself, and all works well.
