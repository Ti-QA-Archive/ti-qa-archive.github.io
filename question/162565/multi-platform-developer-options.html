Multi-Platform developer options

I think I already know the answer to this question, but…

As multi-platform developers, we are confronted with the burden of owning several platforms to support the products we develop

Already reluctant to drop several hundred on an iPhone, I just found out HTC has no plans to support Android versions greater than JellyBean (4.1), which means I will never have support for BLE, which means I now have to drop another few hundred on an Android

So, the question is, are there any options/programs available to developers to help lessen this burden, or are we all DOOMED!

— asked February 13th 2014 by David Glass

  • Accepted Answer

    Unfortunately, having a large pile of toys is the bane of mobile developers everywhere. If you're a consultant, I suggest you build in the cost of maintaining a library of devices into your contracts. You could pair with other local developers to share the burden. You might have an Open Device Lab near you or maybe you could set one up. Find a local meetup group related to mobile development and ask members there if they'd be willing to share devices, test apps for you, etc. Keep an eye on craigslist and ebay for cheap older devices, or buy them off friends or relatives (for as little as you can get them to take!).

    There are online testing service that will test for you – upload your apk/ipa and they'll test it on their devices. It probably works great. I think I'd want to have the device in hand myself to trust the test. But that's probably just me.
