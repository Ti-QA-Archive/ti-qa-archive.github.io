  • If you don't want to write html, open your ftp program, upload your binaries to the web server like Rainer suggests:

    You can also look into using a free service like TestFlightApp.com that helps to manage your different builds without writing your own html. You simply upload the appropriately provisioned binary to their site, your testers log in, register their devices with the service, and are present with ONLY the apps they can run on their devices (based upon provisioning).

    If a user registers whose device isn't permitted, you can pull the device id and then through apples portal update the provisioning profiles.

    In addition to the distribution, testflight also has an sdk that you can load into your application that permits checkpoints, so you can see if your testers are actually running through the app. You can have it pop up questions regarding certain events as well.

    There is a module in the marketplace thats about $10 that helps to pull the testflight sdk into your titanium app.

    Additionally, the next version of Titanium Studio (currently in testing) has Testflight distribution built in so you can publish directly from within TiStudio.
