    • Max,

      My first app 4 years ago used url:, because that was what we had. Because of scope issues, the next stage of best practice was a giant common namespace, and we used ti.include to pull everything together. This reduced the scope issues, but created a loading nightmare because like 80% of your code was all preloaded at startup. commonJs was introduced, which has the potential for scope issues, but it is much easier to pass params/variables in and out of your commonJs module than you could with javascript files loaded from url:. commonJS blends nicely with Alloy, allowing you to have the best of both worlds.

      Now that I know where this url: stuff is rearing its ugly head from, can focus on getting that resolved so no other new users such as yourself fall into the trap of using old practices in modern times.

      — commented January 5th 2014 by Stephen Feather

    • For the longest of times, the Kitchen Sink used url: to load each section of the app, but it was a unique beast, only used to demonstrate parts individually, and not to show the parts working as a piece of the whole.

      2 years ago, Kitchen Sink was moved to a commonJS format by Anthony Decena. I would still not use it as a complete app pattern though, as its roll is still to just demonstrate/test individual components, but as a reference, good to keep shortcutted.
