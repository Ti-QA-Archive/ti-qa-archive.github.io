What's a good nav bar icon button size for Android/iPhone/iPad apps?

I know, it's kind of a broad question.

I'm thinking of placing some small to medium sized roundy square buttons in a top nav bar that never disappear (kind of like tabs), with the exception of one that will toggle from 'map' to 'list' view. I'll have 4-5 icon buttons max.

Should I design at different sizes and swap out depending on screen size?

I was thinking about optimizing for iPhone screen size and sticking with that, but what about Android devices that have smaller screen resolutions? I guess I'm just not all that familiar with mobile design practices yet while comfortable with designing for larger displays.

Any suggestions on where to start?

    This is a multiple tiered questions here, but you may just want to start with the Human Interface Guidelines for each platform. Here is iOS for instance:

    http://developer.apple.com/library/ios/#documentation/UserExperience/Conceptual/MobileHIG/Introduction/Introduction.html

    The Android one might be a bit more in-depth based on the number of screen sizes, but I'm sure you can find a happy medium between what you read. I'm going to refrain from giving any specific suggestions as it could open a huge can of worms, but the docs for each platform should be where you get your answers.
