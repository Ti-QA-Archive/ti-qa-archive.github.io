    • I also would avoid splash screens and I completely understand why developers want to remove it. And here is why:

      From the Android Design Guidelines you'll get the advice not to use splash screens. I also remember this talk from the Android Design guys where they explicitly declaring splash-screens as an UX anti pattern.

      Splash screens are actually iOS specific but also Apple advices in their iOS UI guidelines "As much as possible, avoid displaying a splash screen or other startup experience. It’s best when users can begin using your app immediately."

      The problem I see with splash screens is that developers use it to load too much stuff in the background. Why can't I just get into the app? It is not a problem if the app needs to load content and stuff but it should be able to show me the basic UI without a 5 seconds splash screen gap. This gives users the impression of a bad performance of your app.

      So, in general, the answer to this question is that it is unfortunately not possible in Titanium to remove the splash-screen. On iOS it is part of the App, on Android it is imitated within the launch activity to initialize the "titanium stuff" and you can not change it (for now) or I should better say that I didn't accomplish it.

    • The splash screen used by Titanium is a static image, whilst Android itself does support a structured splash screen - this is not yet available for Titanium.

      For those who do not know - the structured splash screen (forgotten its correct name) allows the developer to create several sections and image images and text within each. Each section can be anchored to a fixed location or offset. This is used to counter the device ratio and size complexities and shear number of variations.

      However you can achieve similar results with path 9 splash screens. I wrote a post about it a while ago;

      • Android Splash Screens – Using 9-Patch Images

      Splash screens do not provide any interaction ability - they show they they go.
