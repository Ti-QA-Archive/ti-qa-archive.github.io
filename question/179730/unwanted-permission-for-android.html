Unwanted permission for Android

I've seen that android manifest xml specifies permissions on contacts (read AND write) but the tiapp.xml doesn't contains those permissions.

Is there any reason why I have this permissions enabled?

3.4.0GA (but with an old tiapp.xml)

Thank you

    The Titanium compiler scans your codebase looking for any use of APIs that would require special permissions. These are then used to populate the AndroidManifest uses-permissions tags. Are you sure you're not accessing contacts, firing an intent that reads/writes a contact, or are you including any modules that might do so? For example, the Ti.Barcode module can scan and interpret a QR code that encodes a person's contact info and then offers the user the ability to save that person to the address book. Because of that, even if you don't intend to use that part of the module's functionality, the app could in theory do it and therefore the permission must be added to your manifest.
