iOS 6 App - Orientation switch to show different app screens - What is the best method?

I have a client whose iOS 6 app needs to use the orientation switch documented here:
Handling Device Orientation
to show two completely different app screens. Landscape will be used to enter a table of user data, portrait will be used view a collective list of global user data.

My question is whether it would be better performance wise to:

  • Recreate controllers and views each time the orientation is switched
  • Just hide and show each of the screens on top of each other

Or whether there are any better ways of doing this?
Also, any extra advice on problems I could encounter using this method would be great.

  • Hi

    If they are different sets of child views, create two parent views, one for portrait, one for landscape. Then in the listener, show and hide as required.

    If you have LOTS of child views in one or both then you may consider removing and recreating as memory requires. But it would need to be a lot on iOS to force this sort of approach.
