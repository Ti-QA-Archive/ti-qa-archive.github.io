    • Just did this so here's a bit of help:

      1. Instead of the vi editor, you might prefer the OSX built-in 'pico' editor. No learning curve.

      2 Type:

      sudo pico ~/.bash_profile

      . . . hit Return and enter your password.
      I suggest 'sudo' in case the file is not owned by the currently logged account.

      1. If the file is blank add at the top:
        #! /bin/sh
        . . . and then add the "alias" line.

      Hope that clarifies things.
