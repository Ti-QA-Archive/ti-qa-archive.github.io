Responsive graphics in a Ti project for iOS and Android.

Hello.
I'm a Production Manager that's been given a job of handling an app that is designed for iOS and Android smartphones - so please accept the fact that I am not someone who understands code..
That said, I have a challenge that I hope someone in this community can confirm or deny for me an issue, because it's causing real problems…
Using Ti 2.1.3GA, nodejs and Python 2.7 my app has a page whereby there are several pictures in a scrolling view with captions underneath. The pictures are landscape and portrait and all saved in Photoshop to 720px wide - the idea being that the device would scale them proportionately and place the captions underneath one after the other.
This has been a real bane of the job. It would appear (and remember that I don't know what I'm talking about here, I merely attempt to parrot what I've been told by people brighter than me ) that there are "inconsistencies" in Android/iOS and in the way Ti handles sizing commands. ("don't use 'auto' for example…?)
I have had the text in the right place, but the resolution of the pictures plainly too low
I have had the pictures at the right scale, but then the captions are too close - or too far away from the botom of the proceeding picture.
I have had perfect text placement, but the pictures are squashed out of proportion so it just looks silly…

Now I accept that what I'm dealing with here is not a responsive HTML page - but that is, in effect what I'm after and I'm terribly confused that what would appear , to the layman so easy and 'standard', is proving so difficult…

So, after all that my question.
What am I looking for here? Is there an alternative method of dealing with the page that hasn't been considered and is easier to implement?
All rather difficult without seeing the offending project, but maybe, just maybe, someone can point out the obvious?

Many thanks

  • Accepted Answer

    If you want your images to stretch to the outside edges of your scrollview, try setting width: TI.UI.FILL on the imageviews, don't set the height, and height should then be proportional to the width.

    In the scrollview, set the layout: vertical and as you add the object to the scrollview, they will be stacked vertically by order of addition.

    And yes, difficult to guess at unexposed code.
