    • Matt is correct in the statement that SSL (HTTPS) requires a CCATS review. The ongoing argument has been whether individual apps, that utilizes the iOS built in SSL are required to have a SECOND set of CCATS documentation. If the apps use code that is already covered by Apple's CCATs paperwork, then in theory, the app themselves should not require additional registration. However, as the USG is terrible at making anything they touch very clear, the rule of thumb has been to register each application on its own to ensure one has covered their one back side.

      As Apple now asks very specific questions regarding the type of encryption in your application, it appears as though they (apple) are leaning towards apps being covered by the OS' broader registration.

      Stefan's question still remains regarding his 'technical query' rather than the 'legal answer(s)' he received. (that includes my answer as well)
