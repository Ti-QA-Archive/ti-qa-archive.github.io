  • It's pretty simple actually.

    ###Titanium
    Is completely free and open-sourced under the Apache 2 Licence. You can use use it to develop your applications without paying a single cent to anyone. (Even if you charge for those apps) And this gives you access to the complete SDK, the command line interface (CLI) as well as the Alloy MVC Framework.

    You can also use their IDE called Titanium Studio (based on Eclipse) for free. But the source code for this IDE is not open-sourced. It is owed by Appcelerator.

    If you ever need support, you can always rely on a great community to help you out on the Q&A.

    ###Appcelerator
    Is the company that develops Titanium and gives it out to the community. Of course, like any business, they make a profit by offering paid support plans as well as enterprise features for bigger projects.

    To better distinguish the two better, they named their paid- version of Titanium, the Appcelerator Platform.

    Hope this clears it a little better for you.
