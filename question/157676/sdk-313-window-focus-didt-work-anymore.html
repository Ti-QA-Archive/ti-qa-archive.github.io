    • i tested it a little further. the blur and focus events fire normally in the NavigationWindow if there is not another window rendered. So if you render a window over the navigation window, then it will not fire the blur and focus events. They seem to work fine if the navigation window is the only open window.

  • Hi Gabriel, Filipe, Alex, Marvin, I also had this problem for my app. I also retooled the navigationGroup into the new navigationWindow and since I also had a so-called 'Toast-notification'-module running in the app (showing those nifty temporarily messages to a user), which opened with a Ti.UI.createWindow(...),open() - method (and after a few (1.5) seconds closed automatically) suddenly all the blur/focus events didn't call anymore, breaking the app.

    So this thread actually helped me looking into the right direction. Thank you. Here is my workaround:

    What I did was replacing the second createWindow, (The message window (HUD-module)), with a View. And adding this view to the NavigationWindow, so it would always be on top of the navigationWindow, but as a view, not a Window.

    Maybe this is something you guys can also implement as a workaround for your hamburger menu and other multiple window shizzle. Just adding the sidebar as a view instead of a seperate window.

    cheers!
