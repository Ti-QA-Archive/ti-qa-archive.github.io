  • You can deal with different densities onscreen by using percentages rather than points to position elements. You can create images for different densities and resolutions and store them in platform specific folders. This is answered quite extensively both in the docs and on this forum. Here is the link for Android density specific resources docs: http://docs.appcelerator.com/titanium/2.1/index.html#!/guide/Using_density-specific_resources_on_Android

    • Every app is going to be different, and your app will never look exactly the same on every device, there are just too many form factors. Just because I give you an example, doesn't mean that your app and images are going to work the same way. Did you add the anyDensity=false to your tiapp.xml??

      

<android xmlns:android="http://schemas.android.com/apk/res/android">
    <manifest>
        <supports-screens android:anyDensity="false"/>
    </manifest>
</android>
