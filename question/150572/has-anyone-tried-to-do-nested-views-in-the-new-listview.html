Has anyone tried to do nested views in the new listView?

With custom tableviews I could basicly add one view to another view as its container in a nested fashion. However with all the examples I've seen of the new listview, it seems all the views are basicly stacked one after another in order. Has anyone tried to somehow nest the views? I cant think of how I would do that, but hopefuly someone smarter then me has figured out a way?

    Just implement childTemplates in childTemplates:

    var myTemplate = {
    childTemplates: [
        {
            type: 'Ti.UI.View',
            bindId: 'view',
            properties: {
                left: 20,
                width: 100,
                height: 70
            },
            childTemplates: [
                {
                    type: 'Ti.UI.Label',
                    bindId: 'label',
                    properties: {
                        top: 10,
                        text: 'demo'
                },
            ],
        }
    ]
};

  • Hi

    The first thing to keep in mind is that listView and tableView are different solutions to the same problem - they will co-exist but you need to choose which one solves your specific needs.

    tableView

    The original way of displaying tabular data or at least rows or data/information. This started off with a very simply approach;

    • Title
    • Optional Left and Right Images
    • Choice of right hand side element; none, child, detail or tick

    This provided the ability to information in many ways - but not lots of ways. Then it gained the ability to include views or any sort in any layout and you could mix them up to suit your needs - perfect. This now gave everything you could ever need.

    However one drawback is that some times you want to focus on speed. As the tableView allowed so many combinations of views it can slow down the processing and rendering of those views - particularly on older devices. It also led to some badly written or thought out layouts that added to the performance issues.

    People who have been playing with the tableView for a long time know how to make the most out of it without the struggle of extra performance or memory issues. Me included - but I am old so - I have been here longer.

    Even for us old pros - they could sometimes be a better way to achieve the same goal, and increase the development process, the maintainability, the raw speed and decrease the memory requirements.

    So the listView was born.

    listView

    As I say tableView uses child views to render data, the big difference of listView is that is focuses on the data side of things. In order to do this the listView has a few standard layouts of the information you would normally use. These include many (but not all) of the ways information is traditionally displayed in apps.

    It will NOT replace tableView and will NOT allow every layout you could want - that is what the tabeView is for.

    However if you want the maximum speed opportunity then the promise of the listView is just that. Define the presentation of one of the standard layouts and then throw data at it.

    The listView then already has everything it needs to display the information and can more intelligently display the data when on screen and reduces the amount of off-screen caching that would be needed for the tableView.

    Choice

    • If you need raw speed and one of the currently available fixed layouts suits your needs - then go listView
    • If you have way more rows of information than a sensible person should display - then go tableView
    • If you need your rows to be displayed in ways not supported by the listView - then go tableView
    • If you want to change the layout on the fly - then go tableView

    Your app will likely have a mixture of both types of these views as one solution does not fit all.

    Note for others who read this listView is ONLY available from Ti SDK 3.1 or higher, not currently available out of beta at the time of writing this answer.

    It sounds like you still need to use tableView for your specific need mentioned above.
