I'm planning an app in which the user will enter their credit card details.
To simplify and speed up future purchases, I'm thinking about storing the credit card details on the phone. How secure is this? Can someone jailbreak and reverse engineer the app and access credit card details?
Should a db be used? Or Titanium.App.Properties.setString() ?
Or is there a better way of doing this?

Will Apple even allow this? Bypassing their in-app purchase-feature?

The app will be available both on the iOS and Android.

  • Accepted Answer

    I would advise against it. For many reasons that you listed in your post.

    • Storing user payment information in a database (or setting) could be easily exposed. This in not a Titanium specific issue. An Objective-C or Java application would be faces with the same problem.
    • Apple will not approve an application that has an in-app purchase mechanism that is does not go through official channel. As you mentioned, Apple wants it 30% and will prevent any developer to bypass this system.

    My best advice would be to use Paypal or the official apple Store in-app purchase (using a module)

    Hope this helps.

  • Regardless of Apple approval guidelines, storing credit card information in a mobile app is a VERY bad idea. At the very least you need to use as strong of encryption as possible. For iOS, you would want to use the iOS Keychain (for example, see here).

    If you don't understand the PCI legal requirements on credit card data security, you REALLY don't want to be trying to store data anywhere – let alone a mobile device. And if you did understand the PCI requirements and potential legal liabilities for data breeches, I don't think you'd be asking this question in the first place.
