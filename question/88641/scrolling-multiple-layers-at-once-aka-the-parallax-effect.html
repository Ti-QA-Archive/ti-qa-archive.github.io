Scrolling multiple layers at once (aka the parallax effect)?

Hey guys!

Just a quick question to see if it's even possible to do. :-)

I have an image that has two layers, one layer is an object in the front and the other layer is the background.

Now what I want is to scroll the layers with my finger, the layer in the front should scroll slightly faster to create the parallax effect.
As the movement of the finger controls the speed it would probably better to slow down the layer in the background instead.

Has this ever been done or is is simply impossible?

I know how to do it with jQuery and could solve it with a simple webview but I don't want to do that. :-)

  • It should be possible.

    Let's say you have 3 layers:front (F), middle(M), backward(B);

    You will have to move the layers by yourself registering the touchstart coordinates as initial stage and touchmove coords for moving, and the distance will be the difference between touchmove and touchstart.

    Each layer will move with a small difference.
    let's say you scrolled the finger with X pixels, then you move

    F with X*1.2, M with X and B with X/1.2.

    hope it makes sense

    • It's not running that great but at least it's running. ;-)

      'view' has a width of 340px (== device width) receives the touchmove event and three other views move around.
      Those three views are each 680px wide and have a left margin of -170px to center them in the 340px wide device.

      view.addEventListener('touchmove', function(e)
{
    var x = e.x;
    //var dragWidth = e.source.width;
    label.text = Math.round(x); //debug
    label2.text = Math.round(view2.left); // debug

    view2.left = Math.round(x - view2.width/2) * 0.3;
    view3.left = Math.round(x - view3.width/2) * 0.6;
    view4.left = Math.round(x - view4.width/2) * 0.8;
});

      As the three layers only move by +170px or -170px (the width of view / 2) I need to find a way to translate the movement so that moving the finger 100px to the right moves the images 200px.
