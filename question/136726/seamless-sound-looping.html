Seamless sound looping?

First question, new to Titanium. Developing for iOS using all the latest SDK/versions.

I have a sound file I'd like to loop continuously. When it repeats, you can hear a brief but noticeable pause when the sound starts over. (If I take the same sound in an audio editor and paste it end to end, there is no audible gap - the transition is seamless.) The sound is continuous with no silence (unlike the crickets where you can't hear this problem)

Is this a limitation of sound looping? I read that looping compressed files (mp3) may be part of the problem.

The only thought I have is to play two identical sounds simultaneously and try to fade them in/out on top of each as you get near the end of the sound?

  • MP3's by design will have a second or 2 of silence at their beginning.

    If I load a perfectly edited wav into Soundforge, when I save as mp3 it gets the silence added (though this could just be an idiosyncracy of Soundforge perhaps?)
