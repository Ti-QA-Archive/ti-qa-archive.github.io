  • I had this as well, in the end it turns out the problem was with a custom Info.plist file. I had used custom fonts in my app, and had copied a Info.plist file but instead of putting it in my root directory, had dumped it into Resources.

    Removing the plist file from Resources and putting it back into the root directory, then doing a clean and re-build on my project and it was fixed straight away!

    If you're using custom fonts or plist files then they would be well worth checking.

  • I had a similar issue and it took me several frustrating hours (and some hair pulling) to figure out what was going on. I hope this solution helps in your case.

    Being new to Appcelerator/Titanium, I thought I'd benefit from a book… and bought the Kindle version of "BUILDING MOBILE APPS with TITANIUM" by TIM POULSEN et al (I like it). I copied & pasted a few lines of JS code from the book and, as it turns out, that was the beginning of my problems. That code I copy-pasted had some NON-SPACE BLANK characters and the xcodebuild was choking without giving a useful message and without even pointing to the problem area. The problem was happening even when I commented out the code. After a few fruitless reinstall/restart/recreate cycles, an octal dump came to my rescue and showed me the offending characters.

    % od -c offender.js
0000000   \t  \t  \t  \t   T   i   .   G   e   o   l   o   c   a   t   i
0000020    o   n   .   p   u   r   p   o   s   e      **   =      **   "
0000040    G   e   t       C   u   r   r   e   n   t       L   o   c   a
0000060    t   i   o   n   "   ;  \n

    Notice the double stars! They are the NON-SPACE BLANK characters. When I replaced them with proper spaces, the problem went away.

    The moral of the story is, what you see in an ebook is not necessarily what you get. If you buy an ebook and you see some code that you want to copy/paste, do so from the code that comes WITH the ebook, not FROM the ebook. Most ebooks including the one that cost me a day comes with downloadable code.
