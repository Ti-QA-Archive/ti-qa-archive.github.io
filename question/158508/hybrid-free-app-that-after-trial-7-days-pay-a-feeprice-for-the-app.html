  • I don't really see where you are getting with these questions, but let's see if I can answer them anyway.

    Since paid apps and in-app purchases are enforced by the same body (Apple or Google depending on the platform), you cannot implement in-app purchases if you cannot charge for apps in the country you are in.

    This in NOT a Titanium issue, this is enforced at the platform level. Even if you developed your application using ObjectiveC or Java, you would still be faced with the same problem.

    In-app purchases are not tailored to trial version. In fact, that model is not very much used on the stores. What you usually see are "lite"version of an app and prompting the user to purchase the pro version on the app store.

    What in-app purchases are generally used for is to sell additional content (think extra levels on angry birds, music on iTubes or Magazines editions, etc)

    Your use case, where your users are redirected to your website in order to purchase the app in not permitted on the app stores. Because what you are trying to do here is to go around Apple and Google to make your sale. They do not allow that, every transaction had to go through their infrastructure and they will collect a 30% fee in the process.

    Also, I strongly suggest that you read some documentation on the matter, this would allow you to have a better grasp on some of the general concepts on in-app purchases in general. Thus facilitating your approach in the future.

    I hope I made things a little clearer for you
