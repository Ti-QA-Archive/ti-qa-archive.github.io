  • Hi see yew,

    i had to tweak your code a little to get it working.

    First of all, Titanium.Map is DEPRECATED since 3.2.0 and you have to use the ti.map add-on module Google Maps v2 for Android and iOS Map Kit for iOS.

    Please read this documentation carefully.

    So i changed all references for Titanium.Map to MapModule.

    var MapModule = require('ti.map');

    mapType: The STANDARD_TYPE constant is missing from the Google Maps v2 module. Use the NORMAL_TYPE constant instead.

    I changed your points from 

    points =[
        {latitude:1.306383.latitude, longitude:103.778421.longitude},
        {latitude:1.309844.latitude, longitude:103.777113.longitude}
    ];

    to 

    points = [
          {latitude : 1.306383, longitude : 103.778421}, 
          {latitude : 1.309844, longitude : 103.777113}
         ];

    because there where typos (additional ".latitude" and ".longitude".

    I added some check code, if Google Play Services are available on the emulator/device.

    Please try this code.

    var MapModule = require('ti.map');

// Check if Google Play Services are available on the emulator/device.
var rc = MapModule.isGooglePlayServicesAvailable();
switch (rc) {
case MapModule.SUCCESS:
    Ti.API.info('Google Play services is installed.');
    break;
case MapModule.SERVICE_MISSING:
    alert('Google Play services is missing. Please install Google Play services from the Google Play store.');
    break;
case MapModule.SERVICE_VERSION_UPDATE_REQUIRED:
    alert('Google Play services is out of date. Please update Google Play services.');
    break;
case MapModule.SERVICE_DISABLED:
    alert('Google Play services is disabled. Please enable Google Play services.');
    break;
case MapModule.SERVICE_INVALID:
    alert('Google Play services cannot be authenticated. Reinstall Google Play services.');
    break;
default:
    alert('Unknown error.');
    break;
}
