HowTo move the Android TabBar to the bottom

Hiya,
im currently developing my second cross-platform App with Titanium and i was intent to make both look as similar as possible…so i was wondering if its possible to move the Android-TabBar to the bottom of the my Titianium-Android-App.
After a couple of minute on google i found this article which explanes how to do it on a native android app : http://envyandroid.com/archives/326/align-tabhost-at-bottom

I figured out that this TabBar Navigation-XML is located under the Android "build"-directory of my current project "APP_PROJECT/build/android/res/layout/titanium_tabgroup.xml" but i couldn't just change it straight away because it got overwritten each time i did a rebuild-> of couse …its the app's build-folder;)

With a little trick ( changed the file-rights to read-only and let the compile fail) i figured out that the xml file is stored as an template in "/Library/Application Support/Titanium/mobilesdk/osx/1.8.0.1/android/modules/titanium-ui.res.zip" so i extracted the zip changed the xml to nearly what the tutorial on envyandroid.com said and zipped the file again!
After a couple of changing the xml-file and rezipping it i finally got my bottom align tabbar!! HURRAY!

…when i was done with being happy i recieved an appcelerator-ticket-mail which says i could just put the file into my "PROJECT"-Folder next to the "Resources"-Dir like "APP_PROJECT/platform/android/res/layout/titanium_tabgroup.xml" with the following content to get my wished look. -.-

<?xml version="1.0" encoding="utf-8"?>
<TabHost xmlns:android="http://schemas.android.com/apk/res/android"
    android:id="@android:id/tabhost"
    android:layout_width="fill_parent"
    android:layout_height="fill_parent">

    <LinearLayout
        android:orientation="vertical"
        android:layout_width="fill_parent"
        android:layout_height="fill_parent"
        android:padding="0dp">

        <FrameLayout
            android:id="@android:id/tabcontent"
            android:layout_width="fill_parent"
            android:layout_height="wrap_content"
            android:padding="0dp"
            android:layout_weight="1"/>

        <TabWidget
            android:id="@android:id/tabs"
            android:layout_width="fill_parent"
            android:layout_height="wrap_content"
            android:layout_weight="0"/>

    </LinearLayout>

</TabHost>

  • I saw this post was posted like 2 years ago, but still:
    creating your own tabbar is not hard. titanium may have some new features since two years ago but here's one way tot do it:
    i created a window with two views: one bigger one at the top (main screen), one smaller one at the bottom (tabbar container). The main screen contained a scrollable view, and in the tabbar container i placed the buttons as links to certain views inside the scrollable view.
    the buttons have an eventlistener that triggers a scrollTo in the scrollable view.

    This way you can slide between pages, and you can use the buttons. I even made it with a selected button view: the buttonlink to the page you're on looks different than the other buttons.

    You can even make it dynamic: i made it with an array. place the titles of the pages u need in an array and you can dynamically create the 'pages' and the buttons.
