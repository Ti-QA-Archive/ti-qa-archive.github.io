  • Hi Richard,

    I thought it would be worth to just let you know that I probably found a workaround to the problem with relative paths in ios and testing with jasmine-node wrt the comments to my other answer.

    First, require() implementation on iOS is completely buggy at the moment, as reported in several tickets in JIRA. Here's one for all: https://jira.appcelerator.org/browse/TIDOC-514.

    Now the workaround for this specific scenario consists in the following:

    • don't use relative paths in require in Titanium (but you are free to use them in your jasmine specs run through node)
    • Say you have module a.js in directory Resources&#x2F;app_modules&#x2F;mod_a&#x2F; and module b.js in Resources&#x2F;app_modules&#x2F;mod_b&#x2F;: all requires to a.js throughout your app should be done as require(&#39;app_modules&#x2F;mod_a&#x2F;a). The same for references to b.js
    • In your jasmine specs just use relative paths as usual
    • Now the trick is to set the NODE_PATH environment variable to point to the Resources directory of your Ti Project, just like: 
    export NODE_PATH="${PWD}/Resources"
jasmine-node Resources/spec

    supposing you are in the root directory of your project. This obviously could be encapsulated in a shell script or makefile.

    I hope you'll find the hint useful as it has been for me.
