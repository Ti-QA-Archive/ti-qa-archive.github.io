  • As a developer who also does native Blackberry development (but not yet used Ti Blackberry SDK), I can tell you that it would be highly unlikely for them to support 4.x devices. In fact, I'm glad to hear they support 5.0 – I have tried asking a few times in the past the minimum supported version and nobody ever replied. I feared it would be 6.0 as a minimum.

    In 5.0, RIM added lots of stuff to the OS such ass SQLite and direct file I/O which would make implementing Ti on the Blackberry much easier. Not to mention tons better multimedia support.

    I have some Blackberry clients still running 4.2, but am in the process of mothballing all 4.x clients at my current application software versions and saying all new features will only be available to OS5 or higher clients. I just got to the point where was not willing to live with the OS 4.x restrictions anymore. Anybody on one of them should be past carrier contract expiration dates, any should be able to get a better phone super cheap if they want to stay with BB.

    For app testing purposes, I just picked up an OS5 phone on ebay for $25 and an OS6 phone for under $80 – I just test with WiFi to avoid monthly charges. But my point is if a person didn't want to renew a term commitment with a carrier, there are still really cheap ways of moving to OS5 or better.
