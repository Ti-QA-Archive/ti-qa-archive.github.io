    I've used Titanium on XP just fine. In fact, for Android development I find it more reliable than using Titanium on my Mac. I can't hardly ever get the emulator to load on the Mac!

    Sometimes adb hangs and you have to kill it. You could restart. But instead, press Ctrl + Shift + Esc. Go to the Processes tab. Find adb.exe and click End Process. Go back to Developer and try again. It will start a new instance of adb.

    I read somewhere a suggestion that when you upgrade the Android SDK or any of its components (SDKs for example), you should delete all your virtual devices. Open android-sdk/tools/android.bat and use it to delete all the virtual devices. Titanium will re-create them. Maybe that would help you here, too?

    Also, keep in mind that the emulator, adb, etc. are Google's tools. Ti is simply calling out to them to compile, install, and run your app. Slowness in the emulator, it hanging, etc. aren't Appcelerator's fault. The iOS emulator is significantly faster. But again, that's not Appcelerator's doing but Apple's.
