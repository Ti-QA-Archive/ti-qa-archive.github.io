    OK, here's your answer. On Android ICS (4.0+), if you have android:anyDensity=false in your AndroidManifest.xml, or tiapp.xml in this case, AND you have forced GPU rendering enabled on your ICS phone, you'll get that problem shown in your screenshot. This is a problem for native Android apps as well.

    Your options are to either not use the android:anyDensity=false options, or to disable forced GPU rendering on the phone. Since you can't really control the latter on other people's phones, the former seems like the best solution. Remember to use the 'dp' suffix on all your dimensions then.
