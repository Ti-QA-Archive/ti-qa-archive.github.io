  • Javascript and Actionscript (at least 3.0) are more similar than they are different, both of them deriving from – albeit different versions of – ECMAScript.

    You may find there are some things you've used in AS that aren't available in JS. The Wikipedia entry is a good start to see what parts of ECMA-262 a given implementation supports, and there are some pretty good high-level summaries elsewhere on the net.

    As for speed, I don't have any personal experience or hard numbers on this, but I would expect the comparison to show AS3 to be much, much, much faster than Titanium's JS.
