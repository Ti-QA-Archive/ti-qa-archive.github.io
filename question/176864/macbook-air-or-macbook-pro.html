  • Hi Viraj - the MBA is fine for coding and building/signing, but for serious emulation the low-power i5 in the MBA could fall short.

    Unfortunately, all new Macbooks have the RAM fused to the motherboard - so you might wanna go back a few generations and pick up a used one with upgradeable ram!

    Of course you could deploy builds remotely to your MacMini and stream the emulation back to your MBA ;-)

  • Hi Viraj

    I take a different approach to the others, but it does depend on finances.

    Firstly the MBA's are absolutely fine for developing Titanium apps, I have a 2013 i7 8mg 11" MBA, which performs excellently.

    However, it does not have a retina display.

    The MBP's are also excellent, and although as said you can't upgrade the ram, you can buy them with 16mg and i7 retina display. Although portable they can be a bit heavy.

    MY advise here, is to go into an Apple shop, and decide on the machine that suits you best, then go onto the Apple web site and order the highest spec version of that. i.e. MBA 13" i7 etc … Hard drives, processors and ram are all customisable.( the stores do not have all options)

    And again it depends on if you want a DVD Drive, I have an external, but haven't used it for over 12 months.

    So rambling answer, but what suits you best … for Look and feel, portability, day to day usage.

    All the MBA's and MBP's will perform and do the task of mobile development.. What else do you want it to do.

    Also remember September is when Apple announce hardware upgrades ….. Rumour of a 12" MBA with retina display…

    Hope this helps

    T.
