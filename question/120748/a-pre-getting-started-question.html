A pre-getting started question...

I've never used Mac's and I understand that I need one to get started with Apple iOS development. At this point, I really only want to get my feet, and I'm looking for the most inexpensive route. If it is bearable, I would consider getting an older MacBook Pro with a Core 2 duo processor (2.4GHz'ish). I"m hoping some of you would share your experience using older Apple hardware and the performance of the different Titanium software packages. (e.g. Studio, Developer, etc.)

My concern is getting older hardware in the $600-$800 range and having time to go make a sandwich while I wait for the emulator or any compiling I need to complete, or just doubling the price point (or more) and getting an i5 machine and save the frustration….

Thoughts?

Thank you in advance.

PS. I realize that I could get my feet wet with desktop and android apps in a Win7 or linux environment, but I kinda want to jump in and get both feet wet at the same time.

    I would advise you to buy a mac that will be capable of runing Lion (At least a Core 2 Duo with 2-4 gigs of ram). So dont go for a very old mac.
