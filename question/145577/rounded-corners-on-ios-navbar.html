Rounded Corners on iOS Navbar

Hey guys,

This may be a simple answer, but just wanted to make sure that I had all of the ways figured out (or learn better ways if I don't).

My end goal is to have that really stylish looking "rounded navbar" that most apps have these days in a mobile iOS app. The goal is to have the top left and top right corners rounded while the bottom right and top right aren't.

The way I do it now is create a background image and set the navBar image to use that. Works great when you're keeping the device either horizontal or vertical (depending how the image is made) but for an app that allows you to rotate and switch orientation, does anyone know of a way to do it that allows a crisp rounded navbar to be used regardless of the orientation and/or width of the device?

It may just not be possible.

  • You could try creating a simple UI View with the same background colour as your navbar and set the borderRadius property to get your rounded corners.

    Should then be a simple case of setting the zIndex so it's stacked behind the navbar and dropping it down a little so just the top rounded corners of your View are visible above the navbar.

    Not tried it but should work.
