  • Accepted Answer

    There's no need to reinstall OS X, though you will need to upgrade. You need to upgrade to Xcode 5, as 4.6 is not supported. You'll need to get it from the Apple Developer web site, as the AppStore version will be 5.1 (or at least something newer than 5.0). I'm pretty sure Xcode 5.0 requires Mavericks. But that's a free download from the AppStore.

    In my testing, Xcode 5.1 will work but you won't see logging output on the console. There might be some other quirks as well. Those compatibility problems should be addressed in our next release.

    We're at Apple's mercy with OS and Xcode minimums. They have specified that all apps must be iOS 7 compatible. For that, you have to be using Xcode 5. Apple sets all those minimum requirements and we just have to play their game.
