Help me choose the best route for this idea (using alloy)

So I have an app written in classic. I started the app about 3 years ago. Last week I decided to rewrite the app from scratch using Alloy. Here is the scenario I am facing.

  1. The app has a window that plays a video
  2. Depending on what video the user selects, the video player behaves differently. For example, Video A you are allowed to drag the playhead at any time whereas Video B, you can drag the playhead backwards and forwards but only as far as you've watched (you cannot skip forward).

The video window shares all the same UI and quite a bit of functionality. In my old app using classic, I just did a Ti.include(&#39;path&#x2F;to&#x2F;certain&#x2F;playtype.js&#39;); in my window.

That worked for classic. In terms of Alloy, I want to be sure I do this the right way and not just redo what I did in classic. Can anyone offer up a suggestion as to how I do this?

The example I provided above was just the tip of the iceberg. There is a lot more functionality differences. Enough to where I wanted the files separate as it would be easier to read the code and edit.
