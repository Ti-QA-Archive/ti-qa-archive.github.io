    • That's reasonable. There's nothing about that on the plans and pricing.
      As far as the app - it's more about picking a platform for an "app to be named later." :)

      I don't think I'm alone in examining platforms with the idea of creating multiple apps in the future for general release and for clients. From that perspective, the appcelerator platform (NOT Titanium) appears challenging from a cost perspective. I'm sure there are many businesses it's easily justifiable for, I'm just a one man shop. But it's good to know there's an option, but there are no details.

      Like I mentioned before, I can use other third party solutions for things like push, storage, database, email, and analytics… it's just more work as there's no ACS to take advantage of.. as I understand it.

      So basically - If you're a one man shop, use the plethora of 3rd party providers and roll your own code to use their API and avoid ACS UNLESS you're confident you will generate enough revenue to justify the costs. It's a cost / benefit thing I guess to consider $500 a month.

      Too much app interaction could cap out the services too fast, depending on how the app is designed.

      Of note - I'm uncertain it's the best approach to expect a customer to have a specific application with completed workflow / communication design in hand for them to be able to determine if they should choose the Appcelerator Platform over its competitors.

      I completely understand there are costs - so I'm not criticizing.

      I'm sure many, many customers design their apps ui, and how it will work in the back end before contacting Appcelerator. I'm probably in the vast minority in that I'm examining the platform from a cost / benefit perspective; evaluating it from the perspective of the opportunity is presents to ME, as an app / app services entrepreneur.
