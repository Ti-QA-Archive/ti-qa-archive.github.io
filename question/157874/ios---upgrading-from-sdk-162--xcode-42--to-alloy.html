iOS - Upgrading from SDK 1.6.2 / Xcode 4.2 .. To alloy

We have a 1.6.2 iOS app (Xcode 4.2). We have been releasing updates without a problem on this platform for over 2 years. However, I am expecting our next upgrade to be rejected. Mainly because our platform versions don't support the iPhone 5 resolutions, that I believe became mandatory in May.

Is this rejection assumption correct. And if so, what sort of pain do we have to got through to upgrade to the latest Xcode & titanium platforms?

I seem to remember some of the v1.x controls are no longer supported in v3.x, so there will be some rewriting.

Anybody been through this and have any advice?

  • Hi Mel

    Without looking at the code base, I am going to have some guesses here.

    Version 1.6.2 is pre commonJS.

    So an assumption is that you have used a global namespace method.

    Although you don't have to use alloy, but its advantages maybe useful, you will need to implement commonJS.

    quite a lot of items have been deprecated between 1.6.2 and 3.1.3.

    The decision here is not if you have to upgrade but if you use alloy or just commonJS, either way you have a massive learning curve, and a potential application rewrite, depending on what you app does.

    Not a great deal of help, but you have reached the point of no option unfortunately.

    T.
