  • John,

    On the App Explorer tab, there is a little box (image) with a drop down. Choose "Distribute - App Store". The process is very similar for ad-hoc testing as it is for release. Create a provisioning profile. Attach the device to that profile. Sign the package. Put on device.

    • If distributing for the app store, on the "distribute" pane:

      How many Distribution Certificates are in the drop down?
      How many Provisioning Profiles are in that drop down?

      If running on a device, on the "run on device" pane:

      Is it the correct Development Certificate? Is it the correct Provisioning Profile (often Team Provisioning Profile)?

      Has your appid changed since the other profiles/certs were issued?

      Remember, in the Apple iOS Provisioning Portal you need the following:

      For Development:

      1. Your development certificate.
      2. A registered device
      3. A Development Provisioning Profile (tied to your dev cert and referencing your device)

      For Distribution:

      1. Your distribution certificate.
      2. (if ad-hoc) registered devices
      3. A Distribution Provisioning Profile (if ad-hoc, must reference devices)

      Yes, you may end up needing to remove all those certs/profiles (extreme method) from your keyring and starting fresh. (btw, this can happen with xCode too, so its not just a Ti anomaly)
