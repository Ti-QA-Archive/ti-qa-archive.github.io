    • ok, in all seriousness, though…

      in recording a stream, depending on the encoding, the stream will have control packets shoved down the pipe ever so often to tell the player where it is and to allow for corrections.

      For MP3 streams, you can often just save the stream straight to drive (even using wget in some cases) and they are playable, with a good player that will ignore packets until it gets to one it understands)

      For other streams, WMA, OGG, AAC, the file saved must start with control headers that you either created on the fly yourself and pre-pend to the saved stream, or you don't start saving until you have come across a control packet by parsing through your buffer to that point.
