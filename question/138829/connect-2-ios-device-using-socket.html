  • Hi,

    I don't think it will be possible without a middle-ware. (in this case, the server). It will act as a central host.
    If your concern is about running such a server which may be heavy on resources, then I'd like to draw your attention to socket.io which can be configured on nodejs.

    You'll be concerned only with the sever part of it. (I'm assuming you have the iOS part figured out).

    Another scenario:
    Now that I think of it, this might be possible. I don't have any references for you at the moment, but if I do find anything, I'll get back to you on it.

    Have you considered trying it out with the Bonjour service? But again, I guess that is limited to the local network. But you should be able to figure out something on those lines.

    In my opinion, having a middleware is good, as it can also be used to make sure that requests flowing in and out of the sockets are valid and authentic. You don't want your users to be in a risk situation.
