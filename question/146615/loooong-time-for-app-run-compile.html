  • Running an app on the Android Emulator does indeed take a long time, but 25 minutes is way too long. At this point you should cancel the process and restart it.

    If possible though, you should do all your android testing on device. It is much faster for Titanium to load your app onto an Android device and run it on there rather than on an emulator. The reason for this is that the Android emulator is a virtual process and does not directly use your computers CPU (unlike the iOS Simulator (note: simulator vs emulator) which does). So use an android device for testing whenever possible as it will drastically reduce your debugging time.

    Oh and as for the errors, warnings, and infos in the logs, yes everyone sees them, they are usually not serious enough to affect your installation.
