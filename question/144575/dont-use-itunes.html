  • Couple of things mate, just 'adding features' isnt as easy as you may think it is.

    Apple controls the way apps are permitted to be installed on their devices.
    There are essentially 2 ways:

    • developer provisioning profiles - these lets a developer put an IPA onto a device through itunes via a cable. This is the way that Apple has designated.

    • distribution provisioning profiles - (adhoc and itunes) itunes are, obviously, used to sign and provision your ipa for distribution only through the App Store. The second is Adhoc. Ad hoc lets a developer designate 99 devices a year on which they can install their apps without going through itunes and the app store.

    • (there is enterprise, but its like having your own app store)

    All of these are well documented by Apple in their developer documentation, which all real iOS app developers ought to be reading and are expected to be knowledgeable on, as these are the Bible as far as Apple is concerned an dit is the developer's responsibility and no one else's to understand this information.

    Now, it is possible to remotely install your apps using an AdHoc profile, using a service such as testflightapp.com or others like it. You will need the device id of the device you wish to install to, enter it into your itunes connect developer area, attach the device to a provisioning profile, sign your API using the then downloaded provisioning profile.
