Does using ACS mean my app uses cryptography?

Apple is asking if my app uses cryptography, I'm using it to store peoples information.

I'm asking question is in the title, as it's a legal thing I thought I'd ask if using ACS means my app uses cryptography? I've read the pages about it and it's covered in legal stuff that seems to tell me I'm in too deep!

I've found links about using https and SSL needs an ERN, I'm assuming that ACS uses something along these lines?

I've researched how to get a secure app onto the store, I'm just asking if ACS comes under the Apple rule of cryptography? Thanks for any help you can provide.

  • Simply put, if YOU believe that the APPLE documentation defines SSL connections within your app as using encryption, then you should jump through the hoops to have your app blessed by the US Federal Government.

    If however, you believe that the use of SSL in your app uses the native libraries that come with iOS, which have already been blessed by the US Federal Government, then you just push your app out the door.

    I am not a lawyer. I have never played one on TV.
