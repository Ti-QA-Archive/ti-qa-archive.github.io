  • The short answer is no. The webview can only access Ti.App.addEventListener() and Ti.App.fireEvent() (and Ti.Api.* logging functions).

    If you want to make things more direct, you might be able to do something with evalJS() and a setInterval() call on the Titanium side of the house.

    In other words, your WebView's JS could write to a variable in the WebView's namespace, and then you could periodically use evalJS() in your app's JS to check the value of that variable.

    I've used this before, and it doesn't seem to place a heavy burden on the mobile device. You could check every 50 ms or 100ms, so that the latency isn't really noticeable by the end user.
