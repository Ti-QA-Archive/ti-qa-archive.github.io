Data Privacy - Europe

Are titanium appcelerator apps legal in Europe? From my point of view they aren't, beacause of the privacy policy. It's not legal to collect user data without asking the user, especially when the data is personally identifiable and when it is sent to a third party. Please correct me, if I'm wrong.

  • Accepted Answer

    Appcelerator is not the issue at hand here. An application written in Objective-C or Java could just as easily retrieve the contact information and send it to a third party.

    It is the developers's responsibility to inform the end-user when any information is to be sent to a third party. Like any other software developed for the European Union, these rules must be enforced by the application. I'm sure you will agree, there is no specific way for Appcelerator to "detect" this. (if an end-user is in the EU or not)

    Added to that, on Android, the end-user must allow the permissions when installing the application on his device. If he refuses, the application cannot be installed at all. So no information can be retrieved without the user's knowledge (and consent).

    I hope this clears it a little more for you.
