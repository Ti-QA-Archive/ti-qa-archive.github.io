Get geolocation by from a button click event

Hi buddy,

I am doing an iphone app which would log the device's location when the user clicks a button. I've seen a lot of examples of geolocation implementation, most of them fire the getCurrentLocation when the app starts, then listen to the 'location' event for subsequent update.

What I want to do instead, is only calling getCurrentLocation when the user clicks a button. My app doesn't need location tracking and one-off shot is fine.

I tried that inside a button click listener but it failed, may I have any advice? Should I fire another event from the click procedure and let that listener to call getCurrentLocation instead?

Any help would be much appreciated.

Thanks and Regards,

  • The getCurrentLocation has 2 "problems"

    1. is asynchronous, so you won't get the reply inside the click function of your button, but in the callback, after the device did its stuff(start the gps, etc) that can take a lot sometimes.
    2. when you "cold start" the gps using getCurrentLocation you usually get a cached position from the last time the gps was used. This is how the device work.

    So the approach you saw in the other apps is legit :)
