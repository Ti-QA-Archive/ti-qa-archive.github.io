Flip animation behaving mysteriously

I have a flip animation that behaves strangely. It all works fine when I trigger it by tapping on the card in question. I flip between the front card and a barcode on the back side, and then back again.

The problem occurs when I trigger this event from outside the card. This happens two different places, either with a tap on the main menu, or from an external event.

Both of these events are supposed to flip the card back to the front. What happens is that the card immediately displays the front card, and then performs the flip. The flip ends up on the correct card.

This is the original flip:

flipButton.addEventListener('click', function() {

g.originatingCard = card.id;
g.cardContainer[card.id].animate({view:g.barcodeView,transition:Ti.UI.iPhone.AnimationStyle.FLIP_FROM_LEFT});

});

and this is the flip back:

// Take us back to the originating card

g.cardContainer[g.originatingCard].animate({view:g.cardView[g.originatingCard],transition:Ti.UI.iPhone.AnimationStyle.FLIP_FROM_RIGHT});
g.originatingCard = null;

(g is a global object to store data)

I have tried to do the the return call directly from the calling function, and I have also tried to just trigger the click event on the card, but both give the same result. For some reason the destination card flips into view immediately before performing the flip, although it works perfectly when I tap on the card itself.

What happens here?

Titanium 2.0.1.GA2, iOS
