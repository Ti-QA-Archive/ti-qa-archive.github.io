  • Accepted Answer

    Use the Ti.Paint module (https://github.com/appcelerator/titanium_modules/tree/master/paint/mobile/ios)

    This is what I did (more or less what you need):
    http://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=427894983975874&l=c969f42a76

    For your purpose, install ti.paint-iphone-1.2.zip (erase mode works better than 1.3)

    var Paint = require('ti.paint');

var paintView = Paint.createPaintView({
    strokeWidth: 60, 
    eraseMode: true, 
    width: '100%', height: '100%', 
    image: 'sample.png',
});

win.add(paintView);
