  • You will likely find that your background service doesn't just stop when the phone is restarted. See this note in the Ti.App.iOS.BackgroundService docs:

    Background Service Limitations

    A background service is subject to limitations imposed by the operating system, such as

    • The OS limits the total amount of time a background service can run for after the application is paused, typically to no more than 10 minutes.
    • The OS may terminate the background service at any point to reclaim resources.

    You may be able to use LocalNotifications to run some code at a specific interval. I have used LocalNotifications for something a little different; it did not involve location services. At any rate, I have some lengthy discussion of this technique in part 3 of my GrillTime tutorial.

  • Hello Andres,

    What you are trying to do is unfortunately not possible. The only way to run a background service on startup is through android (via a module). As you should know, apple does not allow anything to happen without user consent, this has always been their credo. Starting a background service or app always has to be done by the user, there is no way around this, this isn't a limit of Titanium, rather a limit that Apple puts on their devices and products.
