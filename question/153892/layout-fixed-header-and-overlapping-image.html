    Hi

    You just need to break the two main sections apart and then then place the header somewhere you may not expect.

    <Alloy>
    <Window backgroundColor="#fff">
        <!-- content wrapper -->
        <ScrollView id="content" width="Ti.UI.FILL" height="Ti.UI.SIZE" top="25dp" backgroundColor="green" layout="vertical">
            <View width="Ti.UI.FILL" height="4dp" backgroundColor="red"></View>
            <View height="Ti.UI.SIZE" width="Ti.UI.FILL">
                <!-- content  -->
            </View>
        </ScrollView>
        <!-- header wrapper -->
        <View id="header" backgroundColor="#000" layout="horizontal" height="30dp" width="Ti.UI.FILL" top="0">
            <ImageView image="/logo.png"></ImageView>   
            <Label color="#fff" width="Ti.UI.FILL">Test</Label>
        </View>
    </Window>
</Alloy>

    You remove the layout from the window and then create two main sections; content and header. I have made the content section a scrollView (you can change back if you want), but this allows the contents to extend the available space. The header is added last and is slightly taller than the space left for it in the content - you can adjust the actual overlap.

    I would suggest moving many of the display and position properties to the TSS when you are ready.

    This solution locks the header to the very top of the display, if the content section expands it will scroll within its available space as you requested.

      • header; take the image out of the header view and set height to your preferences, remove the layout property and do not set positioning to the label - the label will center to the space of the header
      <View id="header" backgroundColor="#000" layout="horizontal" height="30dp" width="Ti.UI.FILL" top="0">
    <Label color="#fff" height="Ti.UI.SIZE" width="Ti.UI.FILL">Test</Label>
</View>
<ImageView image="/logo.png" top="2" left="2"></ImageView>
      • image positioning, by doing the above you should have freedom to overlay the image as you need because it will not be clipped any more.
