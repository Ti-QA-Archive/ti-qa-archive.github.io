  • Hi

    I think you will find the fullscreen means cover any navigation or tabs within the iOS app and doe snot mean hide the status bar.

    There is a tiapp.xml property to hide the status bar'

    <statusbar-hidden>true</statusbar-hidden>

    But this turns off the status bar for the entire app and not just this window or the email dialog.

    Whilst there is a property for each window to choose how the status bar is displayed that only affect presentation and not the actual display.

    So in short there isn't a way to force the fullscreen to exceed the app available space to include covering the status bar unless you are happy for that to occur across your entire app.
