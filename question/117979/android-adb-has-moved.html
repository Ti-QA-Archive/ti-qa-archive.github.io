Android adb has moved!

I installed the latest Android, I installed the latest Titanium. I went to the 'profile' tab, and selected the location of my Android install.

Titanium rejected it because it couldn't find 'adb' in the 'tools' folder. I looked in the tools folder of my Android install, and found out that adb is now in 'platform-tools'.

So I can't hook up my Titanium 1.6.1 with my Android sdk. How soon will there be a version of Titanium that knows to look in 'platform-tools'?

  • Michael, don't worry, it's tough when you begin with Titanium, but it gets easier and eventually very enjoyable! ;)

    The workaround for adb is explained in the Getting Started guide, under Preparing for Android development and the Installing on Windows 7 guide under Workaround for a missing adb.

    If you are unable to create symbolic links on your system, you can simply copy adb.exe and AdbWinApi.dll from platform-tools into the tools directory.

    Hope this helps
