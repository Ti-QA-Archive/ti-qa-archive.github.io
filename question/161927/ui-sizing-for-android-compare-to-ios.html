Ui sizing for android compare to iOS

In iOS developing the ui is dead easy and supporting retina devices is straight forward enough just double the resolution of your graphics and append an @2x to the file name. However i'm pulling my hair out trying to get android to behave. It doesn't support @2x as thats an iOS thing so what it does instead is scale, stretch and warp my graphics to whatever the screen size is, and there are a lot of screen sizes.

So how can i rectify this, percentages seem logical but don't work when it comes to it because the screen resolutions differ so greatly.

Can someone please explain to me how best to do this?

    • It may not seem so, but Android actually has great support for multiple densities and screen sizes, better support than iOS has in it. As you mentioned, in iOS you have 2 densities, and 1 basic screen ratio. The tall phones came out and blew that, with honestly no way to account for vertical differences.

      Its the volume of different android devices that make it tough on a developer. But, if you use density specific graphics, use dp for your dimensions, and sp for your fonts, it will be a lot nicer AND you will put out a truly pro looking product.

    • It seems that you are now talking about the Logical Density Factor?

      I would make sure your system units are set to 'dp' inside your tiapp.xml

      <property name="ti.ui.defaultunit" type="string">dp</property>

      Then take a look at the documentation on logicalDensityFactor
