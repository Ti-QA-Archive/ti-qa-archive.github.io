    If you've had a good look around iPhone KitchenSink and had a go at using some of the examples there to try out iOS stuff, I would then advise you to look at the iPad KitchenSink, which doesn't have a lot going on, but but use that as a basic project to try out adding the things you've learned from the iPhone version.

    Really, the best way to learn is to try things out :) You'll be amazed at what's possible (I know I was!)

    One thing that's definitely worth nailing is handling orientations, since the iPad gets rotated a lot more than an iPhone, in general usage.

    Once you get the basics in your head, you can then try out some of the iPad specific things possible.

    Everything will all depend on what it is you're trying to achieve.

    We all had to start somewhere! :)

    Good luck! And don't forget, we're (usually) always here to help!
