  • Well, what strikes me the most in your statement is the part where it says: The designer wants the app on both ios and android looking almost identically.

    It makes little to no sense to have a native application that uses the native functionality and trying to shoehorn one way of doing thing into another platform. (for example, navigation on iOS is completely different that on Android) And making an Android behave like an iOS app will feel odd and out of place.

    Now don't get me wrong though, there are a lot of commons things you can reuse between the two. And you can also give them a common look and feel that clearly identifies the application.
