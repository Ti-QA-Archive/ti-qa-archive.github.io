    While I agree the docs have always been a bit thin on the ground, I find the KitchenSink exmaple application has nearly always been the place to find a basic sample of just about anything I have needed to build.

    In order to go 'beyond the basics', for me it's been a question of learning-while-doing, trying out ideas and expanding on the examples out there. There are also many developers out there putting out tutorials themselves in how to do more things.

    There is also a 'living' document growing over on the Wiki here : http://wiki.appcelerator.org/ which has a ton of ever-increasing information.

    Regarding your point '3': there are plenty of people here willing to help. :) Honest!

    Regarding point 5: there's plenty you can do with the elements which Titanium provides which go way beyond simple view/table/nav apps. The only limitation is imagination and willingness to try out ideas. :)

    Yes, the main documentation section here is awful, but I think they're working hard on ways to get all that up to scratch in a way that will be as useful to newcomers as it is to 'seasoned' Titanium developers.

    Don't give up. ;)
