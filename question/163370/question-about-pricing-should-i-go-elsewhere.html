    This is a recurring question, that has been discussed already too many times. I'd be really short:

    1. Free to do whatever you want, sell it, work for clients, set your computer on fire, etc. So FREE.
    2. There is a free tier that is suited to a lot of apps (if you have more than 5M api calls per month then you have already a hell of an app and you should go enterprise - so contact sales). So FREE under 5M calls.
    3. Yes, Parse,Amazon, own server, you name it.
    4. No hidden analytics. There is a setting in the tiapp.xml that disables them. The only call "home" is done to verify the modules. If you want to know how they know you are using ACS this is pretty clear to me - you make a call to Appc servers…

    Now the last part of your post is a little grumpy, don't take it so hard :).

    The answers to your specific questions in case that doe snot answer your needs.

    • What are the limitation to writing my app within your framework?

    None, the community version is free the paid for version is paid for.

    • Can I only be so profitable with the app?

    Earn nothing, earn a million or more - that is up to you. Nothing anywhere to stop you.

    • Can I freely transfer ownership of the app to someone else

    Yes

    • …and expect that no matter what you will not contact them or go after them for licensing fees. (Out side of your cloud services)

    No one is going after your clients for any sort of fees. Unless you choose to exceed the free level of cloud services then YOU will be asked to arrange a licence fee if YOU decide the client should continue to use the services - but the licence is with the client.

    • What is the pricing structure of your cloud service?

    Loads of free stuff, when you have used that up then you move the paid version of the solution. As the solution is tied to your actual needs the best thing to do is call sales now or in the future. Kep in mind this only affects Cloud services - do not use them - no worries - stay on the community version.

    • If you can not not tell me this I will not use it.

    Seriously - felt the need to repeat yet again?

    • Can I freely use other cloud services in my app instead of yours?

    Yes whatever you like - you are not tied to anything you do not want to be.

    • Thus not sure you cloud services at all.

    Correct your choice - any service, the ACS cloud service are very easy as they are built-in but again use what you like - I do.

    • How do you know when an app uses your services?

    Because you make API calls to the services, send a push it goes the server to make the server call, store something on the server - it is stored on the server. Nothing hidden, nothing spying on you - if it is in the cloud it must have gone up to the - well, cloud. This is not the NSA.

    • Do you have built in hidden analytics?

    Nope, there are analytics - you can access, you can add your own choice if you wish - or turn them off - your choice.

    • What are the limitations

    Very open question. I will assume you mean if you pay nothing what do you not get. You will not paid paid support services - you come here for help and no SLA as the solution is free. You can only use the free elements of the cloud services - 5 million pushes for example - sounds like a lot - because it is a lot. Titanium does not do what you need, find a custom module to colve the problem. Cannot find one - build one - that ability is built in.

    So in short - calm down, you asked questions and got answers, you got them quickly and you got them concisely. No one is out to get you or steal your money.

    If you end up making millions you do not have to pay Appcelerator a single penny. Then the question becomes - if you made millions from something you got for nothing - is that fair - you decide - but it is still free.

    Need the benefits of the EXTRAS that a paid account gives - cool get a paid account.

    But next come - come back without the attitude - we are here to help not get moaned at before we have even had chance to screw up anything.
