Not picking the right image files for Android Screensize

Hi all,

Android Ti mobile SDK 1.6.x from continuous build server (about 1 week ago 10th Jan) on Mac OSX. Building APIs 2.2 on Ti Dev 1.2.2

I've got different image sizes in my

Resources/android/images/high
and
Resources/android/images/medium

I have a "H" written over the image and a "M" written over the image so I can easily see which one is being displayed.

My problem is that for some reason even tho I toggle between HVGA and WVGA800 in the emulator it is only copying the high rez images into the build directory (res/drawable-hdpi) and displaying it.

Now whats even stranger is it actually worked once and I got the correct "M" medium images showing, but when I toggled the emulator to WVGA800 to test (it worked) and then back to HVGA it kept stuck on the H rez images…….

Very frustrating.

Now this comes up during the build

"[WARN] For your density-specific images (android/images/high|medium|low|res-*) to be effective, you should put a <supports-screens> element with anyDensity="true" in the <android><manifest> section of your tiapp.xml or in a custom AndroidManifest.xml"

But I'm unclear on the exact format that its meant to take in the tiapp.xml

I tried a few things and it still came up with this warning…

    <android xmlns:android="http://schemas.android.com/apk/res/android">
        <supports-screens 
           android:largeScreens="true"
           android:anyDensity="true"
           android:normalScreens="true"  android:smallScreens="false"/>
    </android>

Any help appreciated, since it worked once I think there's a bug, but perhaps I just got lucky..

  • Here's how to correctly format the tiapp.xml so the screen settings go through to your android manifest.

    <android xmlns:android="http://schemas.android.com/apk/res/android">
    <manifest>
        <supports-screens 
           android:largeScreens="true"
           android:anyDensity="true"
           android:normalScreens="true"  android:smallScreens="false"/>
    </manifest>
</android>

    Note that I am still having problems with it using the "hi-rez" even with HVGA show emulator (which should be the medium images..)
