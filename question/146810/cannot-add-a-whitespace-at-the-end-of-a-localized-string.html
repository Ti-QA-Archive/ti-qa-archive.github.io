Cannot add a whitespace at the end of a localized string

Hi,

it seems like that the whitespaces at the beginning and end of localizations strings are automatically trimmed out. How can I force use them?

Example of my en strings.xml:

<?xml version="1.0" encoding="UTF-8"?>
<resources>

    <string name="syncInfo"> You have not performed synchronization yet.</string>
    <string name="syncInfo1">Last synchronization was made </string>

</resources>

So, my L("syncInfo1") is equal to "Last synchronization was made" instead of "Last synchronization was made ".

Any workaround?

–
TiSDK 3.0.0.GA,
Titanium Studio 3.0.1.201212181159
Xcode 4.5.2 (4G2008a)
iOS 6.0

  • I found it after all. You have to type \U0020 if you wanna use whitespaces at the beginning or end of the string.

    — answered January 13th 2013 by Onoma Epitheto

      This works for me: 

      <string name="lastupdate">Last update:\U0020</string>

      If it doesn't work right away, clean your build folder and retry.
