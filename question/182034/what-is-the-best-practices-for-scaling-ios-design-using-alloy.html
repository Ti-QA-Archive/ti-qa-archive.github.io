  • Accepted Answer

    Amazed at the other answers. This is how best to do it so it works on all phones/platforms, regardless of their resolution or aspect ratio.

    "#demoView":{
//    original width was 310, "normal" iPhone width is 340. (340-310)/2=15
//    width:310,
    left:15,
    right:15,
    height:200,
    backgroundColor:"#ddd"
}

    demoView will always leave a density-independent 15 px on the left/right sides of the screen. If you just want to scale the height according to the phone (typically not done, but let's assume it's what you really want), you could use something like logicalDensityFactor to use as a multiplier.

    Also, the iPhone 6 is still a @2x device. The 6 Plus is the @3x device.

    • The reason Titanium isn't designed to do what you're trying to do is because it is a bad idea for maintainability. To be honest, you should be re-thinking your UI to expand onto different screen sizes & aspect ratios, rather than trying to force the phones to pretend they're an iPhone 5-sized screen.

And, sometime in the very near future, they're going to announce that your app needs to support the iPhone 6 sizes or they will reject the app/update. With history as your guide… Apple launched the iPhone 5 in September 2012. Apple announced your apps had to support that screen size in March 2013, and it took effect May 2013.
