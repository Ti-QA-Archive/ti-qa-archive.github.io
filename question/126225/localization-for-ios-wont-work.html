Localization for iOS won't work

Hi,

We are having a problem using Titanium localization for iOS. It seems that this built-in method "Ti.Locale.getString('string');" works only on android but not in iOS.

Is there any other way to make this work on iOS or any work around that we could do ? Thanks…

  • Hi.. Finally i found the solution.

    What I did was. I build the application thru titanium by selecting "Run > iOS Device" not "iPhone Simulator" This will copy and compile all your localization files from your root folder to iphone\build\Debug-iphoneos

    You will notice on Ti console that your localization files are being compiled and once that compilation is done your itunes will also detected you will see there your application is added (in my case I just don't need itunes yet so I just close that first)

    After that you will need to open again your xcode project and intall it on your device and your all done!

    Hope this will serve as a big help to others who has the same problem that I encountered.

  • After adding in your i18n resources to the project, be sure to do a Clean build of your project.

    We use the L('mytag') format and it has worked well in Android and in iOS. Single quotes, not doubles. Be sure the strings.xml has the correct header

    <?xml version="1.0" encoding="utf-8"?>

    title:L('doctrines')

    <string name="doctrines">Doutrinas</string>

    Here is an example from a project currently under development. The window.title and the tab.titles have been localized. The data itself is currently being translated by the client.
