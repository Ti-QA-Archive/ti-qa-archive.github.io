  • Hi

    The Android emulator has been always been something of a let down, it is a surprise that Google has allowed it to remain this way for so long. There may of course be some issues with how Titanium talks to it that do not help things - I do not know - but in short using it is nothing short of frustration at best.

    Personally I have given up on it and until recently simply connected physical devices - not always practical for a variety (despite having loads) as you cannot account for as many configuration variations as you would like.

    Not what you want to hear I am sure - as there is no magic answer…

    …there is a magic answer. A solution know as Genymotion - it is an emulator that is widely liked and by some loved. Reported to be very fast and generally and all round winner.

    I would suggest giving it a go, there is a free version that has no restrictions - paid versions with more features, the free one will do everything I imagine you need of an emulator - so your choice to buy a license. The only thing I will say is if you use it all the time - consider a licence.

    First off - genymotion has nothing to do with Titanium - but Titanium can connect to it and it works well. You just have to set a few things up before it will work - but it is easy enough and there are blog posts to help you along.

    • Genymotion – An Android emulator you can use
    • Using Genymotion with Titanium 3.2

    Some of this information was created before Ti SDK 3.2 was available so simply take that into account when setting things up - for example you are no longer working with a beta of 3.2.

    • Genymotion

    Hopefully this will get you back and
