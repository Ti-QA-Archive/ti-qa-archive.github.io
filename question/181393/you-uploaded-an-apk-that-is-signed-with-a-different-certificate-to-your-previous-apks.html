You uploaded an APK that is signed with a different certificate to your previous APKs.

Hi All,

I uploaded a previous version of my app directly from the android bin folder with the project without signing it properly when i was learning.

But i have added pushed to my app and they only work with my development push keys i need to have a proper signed version that work with production keys.

But after signing my app properly and then trying to upload to Google play i get the error.

You uploaded an APK that is signed with a different certificate to your previous APKs. You must use the same certificate. Your existing APKs are signed with the certificate(s) with fingerprint(s).

I would be happy to delete the app old and then re upload the production signed version but Google play does not allow you to delete the app.

say com.myapp which is annoying but i built this app cross platform so i dont really want to change the app id.

Can anyone suggest a way around this or a possible solution?

I really need my app to work for push notifications in production not development.

Thanks

  • You are out of luck, my friend. You cannot change your keystore after you upload to Google Play… that's the entire point of signing an app.

    The only course of action you have is to upload a new version of your app with a different appID. You can be smart about it, though, and perhaps upload an update to your "old" app that directs users to go download the new version.
