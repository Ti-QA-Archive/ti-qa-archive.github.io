Mapview click annotation is buggy to the hilt!

According to the docs:

On iOS, if the user clicks on the pin or annotation, the clicksource is one of: pin, annotation, leftButton, rightButton, leftView, rightView, title, or subtitle. If the user deselects the an notation by clicking elsewhere in the map view, clicksource is null.

This certainly is not what happens. See this ticket for further details:

https://jira.appcelerator.org/browse/TIMOB-15545?focusedCommentId=276551#comment-276551

Essentially: clicking on a pin displays the annotation. Clicking on the annotation does nothing unless it is a rightButton with disclosure. With the annotation open, clicking anywhere outside of the annotation fires the clicksource of the annotation and closes the annotation.

I cannot believe anyone else has not come across this before, there should be a way to get a click event from the rightView - even the title would be enough, but the only event that fires in my tests are the rightButton with systemButton.disclosure attached.

If anyone can supply a patch whilst Appcelerator devs take their time on the above ticket i'd be very appreciative. I have tried scouring the sdk to see why the click events behave in this bugged way and do not adhere to what the docs say, but came up with nothing from my limited understanding.
