Prevent iPad app from exiting/pausing

We've run across a few iPad apps (MenuPad, MailChimp, etc.) that intercept the home button so the app just restarts when it's pressed. We're developing an enterprise level app and need the same type of functionality. How can we achieve this with Titanium?

    It was my understanding that Apple was even more anal-retentive about intercepting the home button as they were the volume keys. So I am surprised if any apps in the app store are allowed to do this, even if they are designed for a specific purpose/environment like MenuPad (which I was not aware of previously).

    Thus I thought the only apps which would get by with it our in-house enterprise apps, which of course is what you are developing anyway. But my point is that this liokely isn't a common request because I always thought doing it would mean an automatic rejection from the app store.

    I suspect to truly intercept and usurp for your own purposes would require a custom module using objc code. However, you may be able to get by using a technique the kitchen sink demonstrates. Try running kitchen sink on your iOS device then put it in the background. After 3 seconds, you will see a popup asking if you want to relaunch it.

    The 3 second delay is simply because that is what kitchen sink is doing. You could make it immediate or a 100 milliseconds or whatever, then instead of asking the user just relaunch it. Take a look near the end of the kitchen sink app.js around line 342, and also at its bg.js background service handler for ideas.

    If you want to truly usurp the home button, try a web search to find the objc code to do it. If you can find that, post back here and I'll make a little module for you.
