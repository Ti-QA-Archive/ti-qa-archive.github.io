  • Accepted Answer

    Go into your developer portal and revoke ALL certificates and ALL provisioning profiles. We are going to be doing a clean re-certification.

    After that's done, go into xcode and open up the 'Organizer'.

    Go to the 'Devices' tab, and then the 'Provisioning Profiles' section.

    In the bottom right, click 'Refresh'

    After that, it will ask for a login and start working. As it goes, approve it creating and downloading certificates. It should automatically make a iOS Team Provisioning Profile for you. Xcode will do everything automatically; much easier than going into the Dev Site and risk messing up.

  • At first I too struggled with deploying iOS apps from Titanium. I soon learned that there really wasn't any Titanium was doing "wrong" and that is was because I hadn't properly set up my Apple stuff.

    I suggest you put Titanium Studio aside for a moment and get your Apple stuff working first. By that I mean, take a simple "Hello World"-type program and successfully deploy it using Xcode. Use the workflow information provided in Apple's developer site to download all the necessary keys, certs, profiles, etc. Then package up the app and deploy it. If you get stuck doing that, I suggest you look to Apples forums.

    Once you have that working, using Titanium Studio will be much much easier. You will also "know what right looks like" and it will be easier to troubleshoot your TiS specific problems.

    At this point, you best resources are Apple's site and their forums.

    Once you got that working, we here can help you get that same Hello World app deployed via TiS.
