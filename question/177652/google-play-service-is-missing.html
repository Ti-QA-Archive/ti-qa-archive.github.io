  • What I have found is that when I use modules that link against google play services, I get a TON of error messages that don't seem to negatively impact the program. But those messages are very distracting. I spent hours chasing those error messages instead of focusing on the real problem (putting a v2 ti.map view into a DrawerLayout, which I guess causes some sort of fragment-related problem â let me know if you want the gory details).

    So while those errors look bad, they may not be the root cause of your problems with push notifications.

    I'm also not sure about how you're removing one of two google play services JAR files. If you have to do that, there's probably a version conflict, and you may be masking the problem by removing one of the conflicting libraries â only to have some method call fail later because it doesn't exist in the version you kept.

    Those error messages are really annoying, and I wish that google and/or appcelerator would work to eliminate such pointless error messages like "The Google Play services resources were not found" and "dalvikvm: Could not find method gui.a, referenced from method gqh.a". Those are HORRIBLE messages â they look like legitimate errors, and could easily be the reason why the code is failing, so you chase after them, but in reality, they're meaningless, and somehow you're supposed to know that you should just ignore them. You'll learn this, of course, after about 4 hours of scouring google, Titanium forums, github commit messages, etc.

    I don't mean to bash the platform, because it really does enable devs to build hugely cross-platform codebases that adapt well to a variety of different devices. But after two years of using Titanium, it hasn't really gotten any more stable.

    The documentation is incomplete/inaccurate (and could they please just allow comments in the documentation like php.net â that would answer SO many questions and save so much wasted time), example code is terrible (I still have a bad taste in my mouth about KitchenSink not getting the CommonJS workover for 18 months after CommonJS was declared The Right Way), the Q&A has god-awful searching and filtering (try to find the most recently answered questions on a particular tag, for example; you can't do it), every release introduces new bugs to work around, rather than fix long-outstanding bugs, and the modules are often incompatible with one another, using conflicting libraries underneath.

    I do love developing with Ti, though, and it is better than developing in Java and Objective-C. But just barely so, and that's the sad part â if there was more attention to detail, it could BLOW AWAY native development.

    Sorry if I sound like I'm on a rant â it's been a really rough week in Ti module hell.
