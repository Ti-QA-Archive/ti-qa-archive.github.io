Source from device

Just a few minutes ago, while I was using the Organizer's console to see information points from my app (console.log or Ti.API.info points), I came across a line which I often do, but never thought of this angle:

The line read
Oct 24 22:16:47 DZ-iPod Appname[ProcessID] &lt;Warning&gt;: [DEBUG] Loading: &#x2F;var&#x2F;mobile&#x2F;Applications&#x2F;Application Folder (varchar)&#x2F;Appname.app&#x2F;filename.js, Resource: filename_js

Given that, can people, with a jailbroken device and ssh utilities installed, access the files, thereby the source, of my application?

Whatever the answer maybe (I'm hoping for a no), I'd like to know why or why not curious bunny

Regards,
Nikh.

    Yes & no. If the app is installed on a phone, regardless of platform/coding methodology/etc, it can be reverse-engineered.

    If you're asking if your raw js files go into a final production build of the app, no. Here's a great explanation of what happens: http://stackoverflow.com/a/4798547/595249
