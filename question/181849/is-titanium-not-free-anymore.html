    1. Titanium and Alloy remain OSS and free (appcelerator.org)
    2. The new platform adds a lot of new products and services
    3. Development on the new platform is free up to the point where you need to publish your first app
    4. The cheapest tier is the indie seat for $39/seat/month
    5. Existing users (up till March 31) get a free indie seat for live
    6. If you wish to continue to use the OSS version you will have to build the SDK from source yourself

  • Titanium will remain free as Jeff Haynie stated in the presentation he did a couple of days ago, he also added that the project will stay on Github.

    From what I understand, and I might be wrong, sooner or later Titanium framework as we know it will become history: I am not talking about Titanium syntax and namespaces here but the way our Javascript code will be executed natively and the way we are going to extend the framework with native functionality later on.

    If you want to see how the new platform will become you can take a look at the Window implementation at https://github.com/appcelerator/titanium_mobile_windows and specifically the new way Titanium is implemented at https://github.com/appcelerator/titanium_mobile_windows/tree/master/Source/TitaniumKit

    In addition to that, the email Jeff sent said that the new Appcelerator subscription will give you access to "certified releases", so if the whole new Ti is available on Github it means [And I hope that Appcelerator staff correct me if I am wrong] you can still compile your own version of the new versions of Titanium as well.
