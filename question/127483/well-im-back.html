Well... I'm back!

Some of you will laugh. Some of you will shun or mock me. Some of you will welcome me back with open arms. Some will say who the hell are you.

But… I'm back. After my two day affair with PhoneGap, I guess I'm back in the Appcelerator camp. What I found is this… PhoneGap is an absolute thrill to develop in from a layout standpoint and making it responsive to different screen sizes and orientations. The HTML5 and CSS with media queries is just so great… and allows for quickly getting an app from zero to having all the basic function built out very very quickly.

Why am I back then? Because that's about the only thing that's quick. I ditched jQuery Mobile and got some significant speed gains with Zepto.JS and my own code. However, no matter what I did the touch events and swipe events in that WebView didn't cut it for my app. I needed to be able to rapid fire touches at it… and it just completely faltered.

I think both platforms are probably good to have in the back pocket for different jobs. If I had a basic content-driven app, I think I might just use PhoneGap again. But it became quickly obvious that if you need something mission critical performance wise… gotta stick with Appcelerator.

so…….. now………. back to trying to figure out why my app is blowing up. And dealing with the bugs. I hope things get better around here with the funding. The energy seems to be there in the marketing materials. I hope that we see that translate into the bug fixes, documentation, and support…. and not just for those paying customers. If you want to make me a paying customer you better make me fall in love first. Right now I'm not in love with any of my options. And since I'm not making a lot of money on apps (yet) then can't afford what the going rate is.

I would love to be a part of the community more and contribute… if I feel like I'm being heard and supported by the company.

Lastly… think I'm going to open source my framework. Anyone interested or is everyone already flooded with Ti MVC Frameworks?
