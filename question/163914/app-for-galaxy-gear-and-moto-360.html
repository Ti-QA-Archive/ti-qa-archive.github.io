  • Hi

    The standard answer for these sorts of things is that Appcelerator will generally support changes to SDKs when they go live at least for compatibility at launch and more afterwards.

    These are special cases as neither are actually Android SDKs, the Android Wear is its own SDK. Galaxy Gear is a limited device in that only some handheld devices can actually use it and this has nothing to do with Android proper - it is Samsungs own offering.

    For both of these the answer is always wait and see.

    However I do know that there is much interest in Android Wear and as I understand it from (very) early looks it may be possible to bring some of the elements into the main solution. Watch this space.

    Keep in mind whilst I have more knowledge than many on this subject I am NOT an Appcelerator employee and the internal product roadmap is not publicly published and even internally it is subject to revision as the mobile landscape changes.

There are significant changes occurring to the core of Titanium to allow for an even greater range of compatibility.

      BTW the WinDev solution for Galaxy Gear is not park of Android SDK - this is why this is currently not linked, that solution is a distinct and separate dev environment to the Android SKD itself.

You program notifications to the wear device to act on via the Android SDK. The Gear Live is not Samsung specific as were the 2 or Fit but tied into 4.4 and L no differently than say the LG watch.
