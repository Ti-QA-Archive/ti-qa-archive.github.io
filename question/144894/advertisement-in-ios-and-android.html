  • I can confirm that you can use the admob module in both android and ios. We do that with our Out & About mobile app (android, ios).

    The versions of the admob module for the two platforms are different, so the APIs are slightly different.

    Make sure that you create your admob view large enough for a 320x50 ad unit, or you won't get any ads, and you won't get much in the way of diagnostics to help you figure that out, either.

    I can't speak for the business model. I don't think admob pays very much. Don't expect to get rich on this sort of thing. :-)

    • Things like dateOfBirth, gender, and keywords would be ways for admob to target ads to your users. So if you have the date of birth of your user, put it in. If your app doesn't gather that information, then don't include it in what you send to admob.

      I would suggest that you use the testing flag and put it into testing mode.

      My call looks like this:

                  _adview = AdMob.createView({
                publisherId: pubId,
                testing: true,
                top: 0,
                left: 0,
                height: _height,
                width: _width,
                adBackgroundColor: "#000"
            });

      Height and width are calculated based on screen density. 320 x 50 on iOS and medium android devices. Multiply by 0.75 for low-density, 1.5 for high density, and 2.0 for xhigh density.

      If you get null back from the call to createView(), then something is probably wrong with the parameters you're sending.
