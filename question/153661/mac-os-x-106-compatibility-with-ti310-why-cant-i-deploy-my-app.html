    You're running into some terminology confusion here, along with bumping up against Apple's app store requirements.

    iOS SDK is the software development kit for building iOS apps. iOS is the operating system that runs on the phone. While the numbers share the same generation, they are not related like you're implying. An app built with iOS SDK 6 can still run on iOS devices as old as 4.3.

    Now that we have the first part cleared up, the minimum requirements are Apple's, not Titanium. Apple dictates that all new and updated apps must support the 4" retina display. Support for the retina display didn't come until iOS 6 and XCode 4.5.x. XCode 4.5.x will not run on anything older than OS X 10.7. Thus, the chain of minimum requirements.
