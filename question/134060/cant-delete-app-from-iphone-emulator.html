  • Hey James,

    Once you delete an app from the simulator, it usually crashes, because the simulator is mainly opened for that one certain process (your app). Once you kill/delete your app from the simulator, the process is destroyed, therefore the simulator exists. If you run the simulator again for a different project, you will see that on your springboard it won't have the previous app's icon anymore, hence it has been successfully deleted.

    Also, if you want to clear all your apps from the simulator at once, you can do so by navigating to: iOS Simulator -> Reset Content and Settings

    This will delete all your custom apps from the simulator, without having to go through them one by one.

    Hope this helps.
