  • Accepted Answer

    If you register as an Indie developer you'll get access to two different modules that work with PDFs in PageFlip and QuickLook. PageFlip might do what you want as it says: "Display pages of data to your users. Comes stock with easy to turn pages, multiple transition effects (fade, flip, and slide), a quick PDF mode, and a view-based mode.".

    However, it'll cost you monthly being an Indie developer and PageFlip is still pretty limited IMO. You'd probably be better off figuring out a solution on your own, at least until the modules get a few more features.

  • Hi,

    I'm the developer of PDFTouch SDK which is a PDF rendering framework for iOS that also has a module for Appcelerator apps. You can use it to display PDF files in your app or make apps like the iBooks app. It also has features like double page mode and page-curl animations when flipping pages.

    PDFTouch SDK is a commercial product but it comes with the complete source code when you purchase a license.
