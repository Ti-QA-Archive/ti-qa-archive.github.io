iPhone app best practice for handling testing on iPad

Hi guys,

I have my first iPhone app, my intention was solely for iPhone. I tested it on my device, works well. I submitted it to be reviewed and it was rejected.

The reviewer from Apple said the app layout was distorted and out of bound when it was tested for iPad and they said even though i was designing it for iPhone i still have to prepare the app to be tested on different devices.

So, my questions are:

  1. even if we create an iPhone 5 app, we still have to layout them for iPhone 3,4, and 6/6+, iPad?
  2. what is the selector/how to do it for selecting different .tss
  3. any tips and advice for me as a new developer regarding the app submission

thank you all

    Hi Khomeini,

    Don't need to create separate .tss for all controller. if you need to assign different property for different platform then create .tss like this

    test.tss

    "#btnTest[platform=ios]":{
    //This property is for only ios devices(iPad, iPhone, iPod)
}

"#btnTest[platform=ios formFactor=tablet]":{
    //This property is for iPad only
}

"#btnTest[platform=android formFactor=tablet]":{
    //This property is for android tablets
}

"#btnTest[formFactor=tablet]":{
    //This property is for android tablets and iPad
}

"#btnTest[formFactor=handheld]":{
    //This property is for all mobile devices android and iphones
}

"#btnTest[if=Alloy.Globals.isIOS7Plus]":{
    //This property is for ios7 plus.
    //Here Alloy.Globals.isIOS7Plus is global variable
}

  • These numbers to not correlate with your numbered questions:

    1. You were rejected, probably, because your app is targeting both iPhone & iPad. Open up your tiapp.xml file and un-check iPad.

    2. Yes, you should make sure your app looks good on iPhone 4 & 5. You don't have to target 6/6+ at this time, but it won't be long before Apple makes it mandatory too.

    3. What you're looking for in TSS is formFactor=tablet
