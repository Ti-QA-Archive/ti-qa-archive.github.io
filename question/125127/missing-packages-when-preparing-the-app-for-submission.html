    • Yeah, that sounds like the problem I described where it will update another instance on your machine, that is the intended result.

      There is no way to disable that, it's a part of how the packaging works, AFAIK. I agree it is annoying, but that is the way the installer works. Essentially you have to simulate the install as it would happen from the App store, that is why it install from root as the target (i.e., /).

      It must be signed properly also, or it will fail. There is a lot that you should be able to look at that will tell you where exactly it updated. In most cases it will tell you when you do it, but the missing packages must have caused it not to.
