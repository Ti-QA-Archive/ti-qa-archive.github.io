    As to your evaluation:

    It is my hope that Android will finally get some attention. I started out on the iPhone, and then switched to Android as main dev. OS - and it's been horrible. However you discover that a lot of your problems is not with Ti itself, but with the way it is set up (it seems banged together with a brick using dough as cement) and the way almost everything works in a quirky way. The horrible documentation will also be a challenge. The key here is to look at sample apps (kitchen sink for reference, tweetanium for architechural inspiration), besides find the 'Forging Titanium' series, and there is a series of talks as well, that you can both get a general impression from and also scan for concrete facts and advice (haven't got the URL handy, sorry).

    But I believe that Appcelerator has recently hired more javadevs

    I believe that the proposition still stands: Ti can end up with a very strong tool, because the idea is great - it is in fact the only really good crossplatform idea out there if you ask me. But the implementation is, and it pains me to say this, horrible, especially if you're an Android developer.

    I'm from Denmark - in our version of ComputerWorld there was an article about a danish agency trying out with Ti. They ended up joking that it took a little more than double the time to do things in Ti than it did doing it natively first on one then on another platform. However they said some other stuff that made it clear to me that their problems was things I've already solved (they used earlier versions of Ti - it must have been … interesting).

    I'm about to become the lead of at least the second danish team that gives up on Ti - not because I don't like it, but because I've been spending 80% not understanding WTF is going on, and repairing and setting up - and I'm not usually THAT dim. This ratio has to change … but I think it wil.

    I really think that Appcelerator is in danger of missing the ball on this one. It takes serious dedication and a strong stomach or very clever people to get productive with Ti as it stands.

    Well, I'm procrastinating … need to get back, I really can't advise you on wether to go with Ti or not, I'm going with it - and I think that while I get smarter, Ti will get better and it'll all end allright.
