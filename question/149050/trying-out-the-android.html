    Hi

    And you thought it was just the interface on the device that would change :-) joking apart. The two beasts are very different, Android dev work spits out extreme amounts of verbose information. Not all of what you have provided are errors and not all errors are important. For reasons that are beyond me, errors that say files are missing and paths not found for example do nothing to harm the app or the build process.

    If you have access to a physical device you can cut out all the pain by connecting it to (guessing) your mac and using the testing menu and choosing on device. The emulator serves a purpose but is mostly universally hated.

    Your aim is to get the app to the device or emulator and the look for errors. Your own errors I mean, not the geric verbose stuff that appears.

    Create a new default app, hook up a device, go for testing on device and 1-2 minutes later you should be good to go. As this is a standard app the only errors you should worry about is getting it the screen.

    As you have focused on iOS you may well have used many iOS specific features like, tabbed bars, navigation controllers, 3d transformation - all are absent from Android. But you will find your own answers to fill this seemingly strange omissions. Remember build for the device and not to match iOS. Got a little preachy there - sorry.

    If you have issues getting the app to device test, it is likely the connection type on the device when you are connected. Make sure you have set the device for development use.
