Any caveats to doing Android development first on Windows, then port to iOS from a Mac later on?

I don't have a Mac right now, but want to target iOS in the future. I'm focusing on Android first, and am doing my initial development on my Windows machine. My question is; will it be difficult to have developed an Android version of my app and then transfer the project over the the Mac to target iOS, at a later date? Are there any caveats to doing it this way? Once I have a Mac I plan to do ALL future platform development on that but wanted to make sure that I won't be going down a difficult path by developing for Android on the PC, first. Thanks.

  • Accepted Answer

    If you know for certain that you want to build for iOS, do both platforms from the very beginning. If you are building a moderately complex application, you will find that the differences between iOS and android will pile up. You may find the job of "porting" your app to iOS very frustrating.

    In fact, many people who speak poorly of Titanium have done what you are proposing in reverse – they build for iPhone, and then they find that porting to android is extremely painful.

    I find that it's easier to build for both and test daily on both platforms (even better, test multiple times a day on both platforms). This way, you can tackle the API inconsistencies/bad documentation/weird behavior problems one at a time.

    Believe me, there are some problems that are so mysterious, you may find yourself spending 6-8 hours ripping your code apart to isolate the issue. Imagine trying to deal with 4 or 5 of those types of issues simultaneously.

    If you're trying to save money by not buying a Mac right away, you may find that this approach is penny-wise and pound foolish. If you have to, find a used Mac (any MBP released in the past 4 years will be fine for this sort of work).
