Multiple Density Layouts

I am trying to understand how to layout my apps so they work on iOS and Android properly. I thought that the baseline width and height was 320x480. And if I setup my views like this:

var testView = Ti.UI.createView({
    bottom: '0dp',
    width: '320dp',
    height: '35dp',
    backgroundColor: 'red'
});

It should make a red bar across the entire bottom of my screen. This seems to have worked for iOS and my test Android phone (Droid2 @ 480x854). Recently I tried testing on Galaxy Nexus resolution on the emulator (720x1280), and the '320dp' no longer represents 100% width. '360dp' worked though.

So am I doing something wrong?

Here is the pertinent information from my tiapp.xml about android screen sizes:

   <android xmlns:android="http://schemas.android.com/apk/res/android">
        <tool-api-level>10</tool-api-level>
        <manifest android:installLocation="preferExternal">
            <uses-sdk android:minSdkVersion="10"/>
            <supports-screens android:anyDensity="true"
                android:xlargeScreens="true" android:largeScreens="true" android:normalScreens="true" android:smallScreens="true"/>
        </manifest>
    </android>

What is the best way to setup layouts so I'm not having to redefine them for every screen density?

Thanks,
David
