  • Hi

    Android and iOS manage performance an memory in very different ways. TableViews and ListViews are places where these differences can really be seen.

    iOS will normally provide a consistent high performance and responsive interaction with either type - even with very large numbers or rows and several images per row.

    Android however is a very different story. Whilst newer SDKs and better hardware (you tick both of those boxes) have allowed some of these failing to be mitigated - the fact remains, Android struggles here.

    So how much you show at any one time and what sort of thing you are showing will greatly affect the responsiveness of the app. Keep in mind that as a developer your role is to manage these issues. A TableView on iOS requires no real assistance for its performance - but Android does. So you may need to change how the Android version presents its data - to help Android revert back to being more responsive.

  • I have the answer …

    I'am using "com.alcoapps.drawermenu", and this widget do :

    parent.addEventListener('swipe',function(e){

    on parent window, that's why scrolling don't work normally on android

    I remove "com.alcoapps.drawermenu" and rebuild a widget myself without 'swipe' and everything works fine
