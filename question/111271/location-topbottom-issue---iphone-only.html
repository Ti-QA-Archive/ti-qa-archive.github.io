Location top/bottom issue - iPhone only

Here is a nice one I found. If you do not provide the height of an element, top/bottom in iPhone behaves weirdly. Top will be moving downword from the middle of the screen and bottom moving upward. The movement rate is about 50%. In other words setting top to 50% of the screen height will put it about 75% down the screen.
This is only on iphone as android behaves.

Any thoughts?

    I think it is working as designed. If you don't specify top OR bottom (or both), then it assumes the entire vertical span for calculating placement. Then if you also do not specify a height, it gets placed in the center of the vertical span.

    When you specify a top of 50%, then the vertical span used for calculating the placement is the bottom half of the screen. So then if you still do not specify a height for the object, it gets centered in the vertical span you allowed. At that point half way down the bottom half is your 75% mark.

    Conversely, naming a bottom of 50% but no top makes it consider only the top half of the screen. So again without a height the halfway point is at the 25% mark of the whole screen.

    Make sense?

    However, if you specify a height (even if the height is 'auto'), then specify either top or bottom, its top/bottom edge is set where you indicate as opposed to being centered in the vertical space you allowed.
