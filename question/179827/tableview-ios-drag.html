TableView iOS drag

Thanks

I am using a tableview, and I do the scroll vertically; but on iOS when I scroll until the last row, and continue scrolling, the tableview appears like it has been drag, even the tableview up so much that is possible to see the view that contains the tableview.
(it is hard to explain with words).

Anyone knows how to make the iOS tableView, when it scroll until the last row, it stop scrolling and moving the tableview?

  • Hi

    You explained it perfectly.

    The visual experience you are having is perfectly normal and correct. This is a built-in UI/UX feature employed by iOS to tell people there is nothing else to scroll. It does have other purposes when mixed with hidden search bars, refresh controls and more but that is another question and another day.

    The solution is to give the TableView a background colour. Keep in mind the TableView occupies the same width and height as you defined - so providing that sufficiently covers the 'hidden' elements then what you are experiencing is the TableView with a transparent background colour. When you drag to the point that the rows are lower or height than the top or the bottom - this lack of background colour is then noticeable.

    Your rows must already have a background colour. Just set the same colour (or any other) to the TableView itself as well as the TableViewRows.

    BTW the ListView acts in exactly the same for the exact reason (I mentioned this as the two Views visually appear the same - the mechanics are different however. I am NOT suggesting you swap to a ListView to solve the problem..
