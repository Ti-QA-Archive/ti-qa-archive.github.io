The Cost of SQLLite

Hey guys,

I was wondering if you knew what the performance cost of SQLLite was? I am attempting to get our Android version of our app working as fast as possible, and I realized part of the issue may be the fact that I am generating lists and each time that window is loaded I perform a sqllite query to generate it. Are these query's automatically cached in some way? Or should I look into preloading these sql lite queries into our app before the app even loads.

I perform queries like this

curview.query = RICHWED.Database.Vendors.execute('select distinct category from t_categories order by category');

That return about 50-75 results. Would it be worth it for me to spend the time to preload all of these? Would it make my application run substantially faster?

Thank you guys for all of your help!

-John J. Donna II

    I would have that the performance hit was minimal, depending on how many records are in your table/s.

    I have built apps with SQLite databases in that past that run into 300,000+ rows, and it works completely fine as long as you have indexed your tables correctly.
