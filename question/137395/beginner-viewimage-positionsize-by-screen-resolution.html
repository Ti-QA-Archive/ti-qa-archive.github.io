[Beginner] View/Image position/size by screen resolution

Hi, (sorry for the english, i am french)
i just have a little question about the positioning for my app.

I want to place a view at 10px of the top of the screen.
For an iPhone 3/3G/3GS, i will write : top:10, right ?
But, how i have to manage this for the Iphone4 with his retina screen ?
His resolution is the double of normal screen.

For the image, i know i have to have test.jpg and test@2x.jpg (something like that :p), for the both screen.
But, i already have a problem :

An exemple :

var btn_chic = Ti.UI.createImageView({
backgroundImage:'url/image.jpg',
top:10,
left:10
});

I will have an error message : [Object TiUIImageView] has an auto height value of 0, meaning this view may not be visible. Also for the width. And my image does'nt show up.

I have the same error if i say : height:'auto' and width:'auto'.

But, if i say :
var btn_chic = Ti.UI.createImageView({
backgroundImage:'url/image.jpg',
width:'185px',
height:'185px',
top:10,
left:10
});

it will be ok for normal screen, but too small for retina screen, no ?

I hope i am clear : D

Thank you !

  • Accepted Answer

    Remove the px from your dimensions, just as you did for the top and left properties.

    var btn_chic = Ti.UI.createImageView({ 
    backgroundImage:'url/image.jpg', 
    width:185, 
    height:185, 
    top:10, 
    left:10 
});

    As for your original question, the default dimensions for the iPhone are those of the 3GS and Titanium will automatically scale the coordinates (including sizes) when running on retina display.
