Hide animation of hidden view

Greetings.

My app presents data in 2 different ways: charts and a table. When a chart is visible, the corresponding table is hidden. And vice-versa. To switch between the charts and tables, I added a button.

Additionally, there are 3 different charts, thus 3 different tables. To flip between the different charts and tables, I use animation. And here is where I have problems.

When I am viewing a chart and want to flip to another chart, the animation brings the hidden table into view, produces the animation with the table in view, and then hides it again. The opposite does not happen, meaning, if I am viewing a table and want to flip to another table, the chart will not pop into the foreground.

I believe this to be because the tableviews are added after the webviews, as in:

scrollView.add(webView1);
scrollView.add(tableView1);
...
scrollView.add(webView3);
scrollView.add(tableView3);

I have tested by switching the order by which the views are added and the reverse happens.

Can anyone tell me if there there is a way to trigger an animation in a way as if the view is invisible (visible property set to 'false'), the animation will be done (view is repositioned) but it will not be displayed (I will not see the animation on the screen)?

I would also accept hints on how to reposition views (e.g. setting the left property), as this could be an alternative solution…

Thanks in advance.

Michel

  • Greetings.

    I was able to solve my problem. I did it by adding the tableview and the webview into a normal view and animating this view. Something like:

    view.add(webView);
view.add(tableView);

view.animate({left: newPosition, duration: animationDuration});

    Doing this does not cause the hidden view, either tableview or webview, to become visible. The hidden view, whether the tableview or the webview, still remains hidden throughout the whole animation.
