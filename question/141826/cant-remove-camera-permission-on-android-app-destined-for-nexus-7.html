Can't remove Camera Permission on Android app destined for Nexus 7

I wish to remove a permission on an Android app. My app has an option to use the camera but it is NOT essential that the device has a camera for the app to function and by having it there, I am unable to load the app on Nexus 7 devices. I therefore wish to remove that permission.

I did put this code into the tiapp.xml in my resources folder:

<android xmlns:android="http://schemas.android.com/apk/res/android">
    <manifest android:versionCode="6" android:versionName="2.1"/>
    <uses-feature android:name="android.hardware.camera" android:required="false"/>
</android>

But that didn't do the trick.

What can I do to turn the camera permission off.

I tried putting a AndroidManifest.custom.xml file in the build section but it gets ignored and a new AndroidManifest.xml gets created and used instead.

What can I do?

    The code that you've put in your tiapp.xml has the <manifest> tag as a self-closing tag. The <uses-feature> tag needs to be wrapped in the <manifest> tag.

    If you would rather the custom AndroidManifest file, the starting in 1.5.0, you do NOT put your custom manifest into a file named AndroidManifest.custom.xml in the build/android directory. Instead, just name the file AndroidManifest.xml, and put it in the directory platform/android beneath your application's root project directory.
