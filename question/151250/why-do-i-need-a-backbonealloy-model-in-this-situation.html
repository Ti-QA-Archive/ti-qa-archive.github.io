Why do I need a backbone/alloy model in this situation?

This question is a newbie question about using models in titanium alloy. I haven't worked with backbone much and I'm new to the concept of using models in JS.

I have a feeling I could use a model in my titanium alloy application but I'm not sure.

I have two screens (and thus views+controllers)

  • Screen1.js: this shows data from a json feed (but filters on e.g. category A)
  • Screen2.js: this shows data from the same json feed (but filters on e.g. category B)

Switching between screens is done by pressing a button.

I am looking for advice on whether or not to use models here. If yes, can somebody give me some guidance on how to proceed?

I appreciate the time you take to help me wrap my head around this.

    You'll get a bunch of different opinions depending on who you ask. There's absolutely no requirement to use models for anything in Alloy. If your data model is relatively simple, then you probably don't need to bother with models. If you want to structure your data model specifically or leverage Alloy's view data binding, then use models. Heres a link to a collection of model-based test apps. Most are small and should make it relatively easy to understand how they are being used: https://github.com/appcelerator/alloy/tree/master/test/apps/models
