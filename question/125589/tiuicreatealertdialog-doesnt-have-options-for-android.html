Ti.UI.createAlertDialog doesn't have options for Android

Ti.UI.createAlertDialog({title:&#39;Table View&#39;,message:msg}).show(); works fine in iphone and has an ok button but on android there is just the notification with no way to get rid of the alert. I had to use the emulator keyboard back button for the dialog to close.

Why?

  • The "alert" in android is more complex than in iOs and allows you to do more stuff. But because of this you will need to manage yourself the buttons.

    var a = Ti.UI.createAlertDialog({
    title:'Table View',
    message:msg,
    buttonNames:['Cancel','Ok']
});

a.show();

a.addEventListener('click', function(e) {
    //e.index will tell you the button you clicked;
});

    Take a look in KitchenSink to see what else you can do :)

  • Because that is standard behavior for native Android applications. It is extremely common to have to use the hardware back button to close screens.

    It's why they have a back button. :)
