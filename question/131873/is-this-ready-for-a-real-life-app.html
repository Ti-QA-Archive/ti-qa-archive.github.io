  • Frankly, yes it will make your life hell however I think it's the best option for cross-platform development at this time. My advice is to do your debugging on the Android emulator if you are trying to build for both platforms because the Android builds are way, way more finicky and difficult to get working, whereas the iPhone support is great and it almost always works.

    I think of it similarly to building a website but you have to still support IE6. Android on Titanium behaves very much like trying to support IE6. You'll write all of this cool stuff and it works perfectly on the iPhone. Then when you go to test in on the Android and it is hell trying to get it to work right.

    I think it's even worth considering having two different builds that share non-UI code, or else code in a way that the UI uses different classes for Android vs iPhone.
