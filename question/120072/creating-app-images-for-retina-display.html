Creating app images for Retina display

I mean images and icons WITHIN the app, not the splash screen or app icon. This I am able to do without a problem.

(I searched for this, and the only results on QA I found were for very old SDKs and about the default.png and app icons)

If I have an image that I want to be fullscreen, I create it 320x480. On the iPhone 4 it looks pixelated.

Also, I have custom button icons for 30x30 buttons. They also look low-res on the 4. Do I create these images twice the size needed?

    Basically, always refer to your image as "image.png". Just make sure you have two images: "image.png" and "image@2x.png" (which is twice the width and height of image.png). That way, the @2x version will get used on a Retina display. iOS does this relatively cleanly.
