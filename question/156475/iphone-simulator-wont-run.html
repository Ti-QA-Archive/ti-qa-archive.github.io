  • If the app is launching, and immediately crashing (as indicated by the log output), then something is wrong with your app. It's less likely that something is wrong with your environment.

    You could try resetting the simulator:

    1. Open your Applications folder
    2. Right-click Xcode and choose Show Package Contents
    3. From the Content, Applications folder open the iOS simulator
    4. Choose iOS Simulator, Reset content and settings and click yes.
    5. Try building your app again.

    I doubt that's the problem. I think it's your code. Try with a different project – like a brand new, empty project (create one but don't add anything to the code). You could also post your code here so we can take a look at it.

    • It sounds like you have not correctly set up Studio or the command-line interface if your app is not being installed to the simulator.

      At a command prompt, do steps 2 and 4 from the CLI setup instructions.

      In Studio, choose Titanium Studio, Preferences, then expand Studio, Platforms and select iOS. Make sure that the iOS SDK Home points to your Xcode installation. Use the necessary xcode-select command shown there if not, then click Refresh. If the box is empty after that, you didn't do the xcode-select command correctly.
