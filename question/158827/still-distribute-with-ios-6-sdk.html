  • Accepted Answer

    Apple require you to build and submit Apps using the latest iOS / XCode combinations.
    This does not mean that device owners who havent upgraded to iOS7 will not be able to run the App, it just means they will not get the iOS7 look and feel.
    So, the problem is more that iOS7 users may get a less than optimal user experience if you do not adjust the App to work with iOS7

  • Hi

    Whilst Apple hopes you will adopt iOS7 and XCODE 5 they are currently not requiring you only use one and build to the other - although this will come.

    As with iOS6 Apple happily accepted apps for a period of some months before they required iOS6 based apps (which would still work down to iOS 4.3 (if they were written correctly). This was the same time that they enforced the need to support the iPhone 5 taller retina display.

    They do not enforce this change over straight away as it takes time for apps to take advantage of new apis and with the current iOS7 this is also a big UI change for developers to embrace and master.

    Expect this enforcement sometime early 2014, probably around March.

    To learn how to manage both 6.1 and 7.0 SDKs at the same time check out this great article/guide of how to manage it.

    • Build for iOS 6.1 & 7.0 before/after Updating XCode [Titanium Mobile]

    It makes sense to start the transition - but if you have an update to push - I would go ahead and push the update.
