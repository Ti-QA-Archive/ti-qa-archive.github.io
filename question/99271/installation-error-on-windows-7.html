  • Chen,

    I think I have a solution for you, at least this worked for me.

    1) Take a laptop with Win 7 to another location, say a WiFi hotspot, and attempt the installation using the MSI from there. That worked immediately for me indicating something in our satellite setup, probably at the Hughes NOC, was blocking the connection.

    2) Bring the laptop back to your primary office LAN where the setup was failing and copy the "c:\program files\titanium developer" folder from the laptop to the target PC.

    3) On the target PC navigate to the new "c:\program files\titanium developer" folder and open/run "Titanium Developer". In my case at this point the SDK connecetd and downloaded the same files that had been failing using the MSI. After about five minutes when the downloads completed, the main signup/login screen appeared.

    4) Login with your username and password, and hopefully you're all set.

    If you still are having trouble with getting a proper install with the MSI in step one, and have a way to contact me, I would be happy to zip the folder for you and send it to you.

    Best

    Regarding your original issue, I can only reiterate previous suggestions about its cause - either that it is:

    • local firewall
    • local antivirus
    • insufficient disk space
    • insufficient permissions (it's advisable to run TiDev as administrator to install SDKs)
    • network firewall
    • poor quality connection to the internet
    • restrictive local quotas

    Other than this, I'm afraid I am out of ideas.
