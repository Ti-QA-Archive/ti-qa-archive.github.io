  • IMHO, the phrase "poor support" needs to be put in context. Put bluntly, at the free level there is NO guaranteed support from Appcelerator staff, period. But neither are you paying for access to the tools. You do have access to this community based Q&A.

    Even though there is no guaranteed support, if you take the sidebar link for the top 100 most active Q&A members, 5 of the top 10 are Appcelerator employees and 17 of the top 100. Collectively, by some quick math that adds up to nearly 95,000 "points" between them. Moverover, the level of participation by Appcelerator staff has gone up significantly in the last few months, according to my casual observation anyway.

    So if anything I would say they are providing MORE support than promised for community members.

    That said, I also agree with a previous poster that suggested the volume of questions here could be greatly reduced if the documentation was better. I also think they are working toward that goal.

    Do I find it frustrating sometimes? By all means a resounding YES. Do I think it is their obligation to provide free answers? No, as much as in a perfect world that would be ideal.

    Might providing more "free" answers help them garner market share, or keep people on board who experiment with Appcelerator? As an outsider it is certainly easy to say Yes. My gut reaction is that is precisely why they have 95000 points among the staff members, and have 5 of the 10 most active members. But that is just my SWAG as an outside developer myself.
