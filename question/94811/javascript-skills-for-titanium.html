  • I can wholeheartedly recommend the book "JavaScript: The Definitive Guide" by David Flanagan. Ensure to get the sixth edition, which covers the upcoming ECMAscript 5 standard.

    Currenly I read "JavaScript Patterns" by Stoyan Stefanov which discusses various programming patterns that are typical for JavaScript and that are often unfamiliar for developers with classic OO background. Very insightful.

    There is also another book, "JavaScript - The Good Parts" which discusses various pitfalls of the language. I haven't read this book but it might be the next one I buy. After all, knowing why things like 'parseInt("09")' don't deliver the result you'd expect is worth a lot, especially if you learn it from a book rather than through hours of debugging.

    (Disclaimer: It is purely coincidental that all these books are published by O'Reilly - I have no personal or business relationships with this company nor with the authors.)
