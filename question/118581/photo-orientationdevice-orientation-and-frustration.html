Photo Orientation/Device Orientation and frustration

So, I have been working with Titanium for a few months on an idea for a mobile app and have now run in to a show stopper, you cannot reliably determine the orientation of the camera when a photo is taken so cannot store/retrieve it in the proper orientation/aspect ratio in your app.

Here are the approaches/pitfalls what I have encountered so far, hopefully someone has an idea on how to slay this thing…
BTW, Titanium Mobile 1.6.1, Android, Win XP

1) Read the width/height of the image when it is returned from the showCamera() call. Doesn't work, the width and height are always the same, regardless of the orientation.

    Titanium.Media.showCamera({

        success: function(event){
            var image = event.media;
            var eventInfo = Titanium.JSON.stringify(event);
            var parsedData = Titanium.JSON.parse(eventInfo);

alert('Photo width = '+ parsedData.cropRect.width+ ' height = '+parsedData.cropRect.height);
                });

2) I have tried getting the orientation of the device before/after/inside the success return of showcamera and you cannot determine with any consistency, the orientation when the photo is taken. It looks like Titanium releases all control to ShowCamera until it returns and can't read orientation change while the device camera has control.

I have no other ideas and without the ability to figure out the orientation of the device, it is impossible to manage the image in the app to display properly (orientation and aspect ratio). Makes the app feel mostly useless… and unprofessional.

— asked April 15th 2011 by Ray Belisle
