Add Poster Frame to Video Player?

Hey guys,

I was just wondering does anyone know if you can add a poster frame to the Titanium.Media.VideoPlayer? Currently when the video is loaded and it hasn't been played yet it appears black. I would like to have a still frame or image there instead.

I know I could just overlay another imageView on top of the video player and listen for a click event to play the video but wanted to see if there was something built in before I went down that route.

I'm building an iPad app using Titanium 1.5.1 on iOS SDK 4.2.

Thanks,
Pat

    I would do this (and have done) by using an imageView of the thumbnail (possibly with a play button imageView centered on top of it) as you said.

    Then add an eventListener to the play button so that when it's clicked I then create the videoPlayer over/in place of the thumbnail/poster image and button (hiding them too)

    Once the video is played, you can then remove it and display the image/button again.

    A simpler way might be (depending on how you want your app to work) is to use a webView and the HTML5 video tag, which will let you set the poster image as you would in a browser. But then you wouldn't have as much control over the playback/state of the movie wrt the app (withouth some extra JS shenanigans ;) )
