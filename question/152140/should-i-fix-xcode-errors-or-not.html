Should I fix xCode errors or not?

If I open my .xcodeproj, xCode is detecting around 27 issues in my app (which works the way it should in the simulator and on a device). I'm opening the build folder in xCode because I want to profile the app in Instruments.

Should/can I fix these issues or can they be ignored? Screenshot right here: http://d.pr/i/JntZ

  • Accepted Answer

    There's no need to build your app in Xcode in order to profile it in Instruments. Simply open your app in the simulator, open Instruments, and attach to your app's process (not the simulator process). There are more details in the memory monitoring guide.

    And no, there's no reason to worry about those errors. They're warnings, not errors, and are not serious issues. They're also part of the Titanium framework. To fix them, you'd need to fork Titanium, modify the code, and rebuild from source.
