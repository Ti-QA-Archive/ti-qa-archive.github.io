Transformed slider rendering appears broken on Android

I have a simple slider that has a slight rotation (10 degrees) and it appears overdrawn by itself as the slider button moves from left to right. This only happens on Android (2.2) - it appears OK on iOS (4.2)

It looks like it is being clipped/masked by a non-rotated version of itself.

Ti Developer 1.2.2 (both Win XP and Mac OS), Ti SDK 1.5.1, Android 2.2, 2.3.1, and 2.3.3

var slider = Titanium.UI.createSlider({
    min:0,
    max:100,
        transform:Ti.UI.create2DMatrix().rotate(10)
});
win.add(slider);

I also modified the KitchenSink basic slider demo and saw the same problem:

var basicSlider = Titanium.UI.createSlider({
    min:0,
    max:10,
    value:5,
    width:100,
//    height:'auto',
    top:30,
    selectedThumbImage:'../images/slider_thumb.png',
    highlightedThumbImage:'../images/chat.png',
    transform:Ti.UI.create2DMatrix().rotate(10)
});

I commented out the <height:'auto'> line as it causes the app to crash.

Anyone else seeing this?

Any help would be appreciated.
