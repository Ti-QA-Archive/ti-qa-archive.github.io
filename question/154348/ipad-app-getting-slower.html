  • Accepted Answer

    The simulator presents an unrealistic environment for testing. It's running at the full speed of your laptop/desktop's CPU and with access to your computer's memory space. If you run your app long enough in the simulator, I suspect you'll see a similar slow down and eventually crash.

    You likely have a memory leak as Muhammad points out. Use the Instruments tool to examine your app's memory allocations to see if you can determine where the leak is happening. The video is a bit dated, but I demonstrate how to use this tool in this video: http://vimeopro.com/appcelerator/advanced-titanium-mobile-development/video/44338176 It's also covered in the guides. See http://docs.appcelerator.com/titanium/latest/#!/guide/Managing_Memory_and_Finding_Leaks

    Another point is that application-level events are slow compared to callbacks. The simulator might mask the difference. You might want to restructure your app to limit the use of app events.
