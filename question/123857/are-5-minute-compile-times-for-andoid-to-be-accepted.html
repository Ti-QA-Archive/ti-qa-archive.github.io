  • Not to be flippant, but if you are trying to work on a "professional basis", then why are you using a Mac Mini? The latest models have greatly improved the speeds and feeds – especially if paired with a SSD drive – but most generations of the Mac Mini are more "hobbyist" than "professsional", IMHO.

    I attribute the bulk of the (lack of) performance when dealing with Android to be the Android emulator. It is painfully slow. FastDev will not improve the initial load time – it is designed to reduce debug/test/cycle times. So you need to find out why you apparently need to shut down the emulator and delete the AVD. That is certainly not normal.

    But aside from that, here are some other serious suggestions:

    • Attach your phone via USB and test on the device. The Android emulator is horrendously slow. (In contrast to the iOS Simulator, which is actually faster than a real device.)
    • Consider a SSD drive – others have reported their android speeds have doubled.
    • Upgrade your Mac Mini memory if not at its maximum for your model

    And last but not least, in all seriousness, consider prototyping and doing the bulk of the logic debug cycles using iOS instead of Android. Granted, you can't verify all the UI sizing and density support, or test those things which are Android only. But most apps will have a significant portion of the app where the code/test/debug cycle is just working on logic testing, event listeners, etc.

    The iOS simulator runs circles around the android emulator in that regard. So crazy as it may sound on the surface, you may find you are more productive doing as much testing as possible under iOS instead of android.

    You have a leg up on the Windows only based users in that regard.

    YMMV.

    Just my .02

  • On a related note, I picked up a good tip from Pedro Enrique. He suggested to run the following from the command line to view Ti.API.info() responses in real-time while running an app on a USB-connected Android device: 

    adb -d logcat | grep TiAPI

    Very lightweight and simpler than DDMS.

    HTH.
