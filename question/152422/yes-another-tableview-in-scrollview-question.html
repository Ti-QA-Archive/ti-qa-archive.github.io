Yes... another TableView in ScrollView question

Are there any suggestions for getting a tableView to be scrollable within a scrollView?

I've tried setting canCanelEvents on the parent scrollView but then that causes issues with not being able to scroll at all if you happened to try to scroll up while hitting a text field or some other element that is on the scrollView.

I've tried setting listeners on the tableView that disables the scrolling of the parent scrollview until you hit the end of the table.

Is there nothing we can do here?

Time and time again whenever someone posts a question about wanting to be able to do this the standard response is…. Why do you want to do this? A TableView IS a scrollview, rework your code.

I have two issues with this response:

1.) Even though it is considered "bad" by the Appcelerator team I contend that it should function the same way that it does on Android as it does for iOS. Saying it is "bad" design does not mean that it shouldn't function the same way on both platforms.

2.) How in the world am I supposed to handle separation of concerns for a Titanium app? We've done a pretty good job setting up an MVC structure to separate our controls from our models from our UI. In order for this to work I need a tableView to be scrollable within a scrollView because of how we've separated these into individual controls and then are adding the ones we need to a base scrollView. It seems the only way I'd be able to rework all of this code would be to make large monolithic program files to wrap everything in a large tableView. That seems like a terrible idea!

Any help here would be appreciated!

    I think it's common sense not to put a TableView inside a ScrollView, the controller will get confused on what to scroll or not.

    Related to your point 1. - keep in mind that the platforms are very different so it's very difficult to normalize the behaviour.

    Point 2. Just because you have build your app around a (bad) approach doesn't mean is the only way.

    If you really need big scrolling stuff, yes, the reply is a huge table, but that can be populated with sections instead of rows, and each section to be generated by other part of your logic.

    This way you "emulate" the scrollview with a table and the tables inside your scrollview with sections.

    Don't get upset, but you are forcing yourself in finding solutions in an (again) bad design, and you will loose more time because of this.

    The issues go way deeper than a simple rewriting of the app, the way the proxies (the native ones) are build can lead to weird behaviours for complicated layouts just because they are not made in such a way - to be imbricated. The fact that in some cases it works it's almost just a mere coincidence.

    I've been there, and I lost way more time trying to figure out how to do it than rewriting the stuff - which I did in the end.
