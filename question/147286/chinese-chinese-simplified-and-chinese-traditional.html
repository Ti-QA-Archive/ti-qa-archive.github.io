    Hi

    Good question BTW.

    Chinese Traditional and Chinese Simplified are considered mutuality exclusive, if you wish to support both you provide both, if you only provide one it does NOT fall-back to anything.

    There is no Chinese only zh option.

    Just in case you are not sure how to structure the folders (use caps as indicated);

    {appname}/i18n/en/strings.xml
{appname}/i18n/zh-Hant/strings.xml
{appname}/i18n/zh-Hans/strings.xml

    Here is a test for you;

    app.js

    var win = Ti.UI.createWindow({
    backgroundColor: '#fff',
    title: L('Tab', 'None')
});
var tab = Ti.UI.createTab({
    icon: 'KS_nav_views.png',
    title: L('Tab', 'None'),
    window: win
});

Ti.API.info('currentLanguage', Ti.Locale.currentLanguage);

var tabGroup = Ti.UI.createTabGroup();
tabGroup.addTab(tab);
tabGroup.open();

    i18n/en/strings.xml

    <?xml version="1.0" encoding="UTF-8"?>
<resources>
    <string name="Tab">English</string>
</resources>

    i18n/zh-Hans/strings.xml

    <?xml version="1.0" encoding="UTF-8"?>
<resources>
    <string name="Tab">Simplified Chinese</string>
</resources>

    i18n/zh-Hant/strings.xml

    <?xml version="1.0" encoding="UTF-8"?>
<resources>
    <string name="Tab">Traditional Chinese</string>
</resources>

    Then swap the languages on the device to each of the Chinese options and see the difference.
