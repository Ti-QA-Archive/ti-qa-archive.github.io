[Android] Full contents of WebView to image

I have a basic image editor implementation inside a WebView. After the user finishes editing, I have to save the image. For this, I want to render the whole contents of the WebView as an image blob. When I use toImage, it returns only the viewable parts, but I also need which is currently not viewable through the viewport. How can I do this? (I have no canvas inside the WebView.)

— asked August 30th 2012 by Kristof Gruber

  • There is currently no out of the box way to grab the non-visible parts of a view for an image. So you'd have to either use a service that you could send the url off to if its available on the web, or create a native module to do this.

    Or you could try and hack something together by starting at the top of the view, imaging it out, then programatically scrolling down and imaging out the next section, repeating until you have the entire view and then stitching the images together. Not the most elegant solution, nor the easiest, but it might work.
