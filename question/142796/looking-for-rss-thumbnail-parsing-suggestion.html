Looking for RSS thumbnail parsing suggestion

Having spent more than a few hours searching through both this forum and others, I still haven't found a reliable approach to extracting thumbnail images from RSS feeds. While the RSS 2.0 spec indicates that image references should go in optional <media> elements, feeds I'm interested in (both public & private) ignore that and embed the links in <description> |CDATA|

I've looked at the kitchen sink example, which uses namespaced getElements, but doesn't appear to parse CDATA (documentation doesn't say this, but a number of other posters have). I've also looked at yql and google feed APIs, which looked promising, but they don't appear to extract thumbnails from these feeds either.

So…I know I'm not alone in trying to find a way to do this, but haven't yet seen a solution that would apply and would be grateful for any recommendations

  • You need a regex that will extract the image url (most likely the first one) from the text of the XML element.

    Actually, an RSS feed with <media> tags will not validate properly without a separate schema being declared, as <media> is not 'optional' but an extension of the RSS spec. It was introduced by Yahoo specifically for publishers to push media types into Yahoo's system more easily.

    That being said, I feel your pain. In the apps we built that must be fed multimedia via xml, we adapt the feeds generator to use <media> as we find it most useful.

    However, if the feed is not your own, you are left to the mercy of the publisher.
