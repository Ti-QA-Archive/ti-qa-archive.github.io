  • Actually if your are developing a mobile app for reading books, you may hit issues with loading the whole book at one time. Depending on the size of the book.

    I would use paging and load a page at a time, having the next and previous ones loaded… Then when you change page load the next and previous already for use.

    Books tend to read left to right and using paging in this format should be acceptable to most users.

    Also if you had a goto page option it is also acceptable to wait a short while to get to the page.

    Again how I would do it. There are obviously other ways and solutions.

    Hope this helps

  • http://developer.appcelerator.com/question/128609/how-can-i-detect-when-ive-reached-the-end-of-a-scrollview

    Load say, 10 pages at once, and then detect your location in the scroll view on scroll. So when you get to page 8, automatically load up to the next 10 pages and tack onto the end.

    This WILL present memory issues eventually, so you'll need to go the other way too. In other words, you'll need to reload the entire scroll view with just the pages they're looking at. Then you'll need to detect when they scroll to the TOP as well and reload.

    Hope this helps. Good luck with that crazy client ;)
