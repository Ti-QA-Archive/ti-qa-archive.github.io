View height relative to siblings and device height

I've been struggling with this for the past few hours now, I can't seem to achieve the correct result.

The composition of the View is as follows:

– HEADER

– MAIN CONTENT

– FOOTER

The header and footer's size scale to fit their children. I'm struggling with trying to work out the main content height once the header and footers have been rendered, i.e. 'postlayout'.

The standard calculation here might be something like:

mainContent.height = Titanium.Platform.displayCaps.platformHeight - (header.height + footer.height)

This works fine on iOS, but on Android, it's not completely accurate and the mainContent view height is out by about 20 or so pixels every time. No matter what I try, I can't get it working - I do not want to use percentages here either.

Note, I have converted the value returned from Titanium.Platform.displayCaps.platformHeight from pixels to dip on Android before doing this calculation as per this post: https://developer.appcelerator.com/question/125317/calculate-apps-density-pixel-width–height-using-titaniumplatformdisplaycapsdpi

Please help! Thanks!
