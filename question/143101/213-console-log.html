  • I had this happen to me just now and here is how I fixed it.

    What seems to be the problem is the simulator can't write to the log file. For me I was messing around with creating a folder inside my application's Document folder, which is also where the log file is kept. What happened was I turned my Document folder into a file called Document. Now the log file is trying to be written to a place that doesn't exist hence no output in the console.

    Two ways to fix this:

    First is the quick and easy way. Just open your app in the simulator, Press the simulator "Home" button. Click and hold your app icon and click X when it starts jiggling to uninstall it. Once you re-run everything will be re-created. This is why it worked fine when you installed it on another computer because everything was created new and working.

    Second way:
    Open a terminal and go to the apps folder. The path will be something like this..
    /Users/yourUserName/Library/Application Support/iPhone Simulator/6.0/Applications/626EF612-0981-4C37-86C0-BC4718DAAE25/

    Where the long number at the end is the ID of your App. Each App in the simulator has it's own ID. Since you can't use the Ti.API.info to tell you which one is your App's ID because it's not working you will just have to try each one and figure it out.

    In this ID folder you will have the Documents, Library, and tmp folder. Try to enter the Documents folder. If your problem is the same as mine it should say it's not a folder. Delete the Documents file/"folder" and re-run the App. The Documents folder will be re-created and the log file should be inside it. The console should now work.
