    Hi

    Short answer - no.

    Longer answer;

    This may sound like it is a limitation, but it helps you focus on the app and its structure. Having spent 30 years developing software, I have always found that given the need ability to spent a lot of time tweaking each tiny part of a UI on part at a time - usually means that no initial plan is created - no styles are pre-defined, no rules are set, no app wide suitability for each re-used section is taken into account. Until much later.

    In the same way that many authors of books, use notepad, typewriters or old DOS machines - they focus on the app creation. Rather (in their case) what should be bold, what should be red. Those are extremely important - but you spend you time tweaking with them all day.

    But that is just me.

    However something that can aid you is (TiShadow](http://tishadow.yydigital.com/) is will speed up the refresh of the app between compiles so often your changes are instant.
