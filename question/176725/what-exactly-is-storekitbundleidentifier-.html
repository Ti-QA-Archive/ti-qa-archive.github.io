    • I have App for company Foo that will be for their Bar product line.

      My appId will be com.foo.bar

      Will register that with apple.

      That will be whats listed in my <id> in tiapp.xml.

      I will then create an adHoc provisioning profile (which is a distribution) and an App Store provisioning profile (also distribution) in the apple developer portal.

      During development, I will push to my device using the adhoc profile (which is distribution) which allows me to test push notifications (if I'v created a push profile and uploaded it to the appropriate service) and in-app purchases on my device.

      When the time comes to send to the store, I will build with the App Store profile. App Id never changes.

      As for development profiles, I have at max 2 that I care about which are wild cards.

      com.stephenfeather.*
com.featherdirect.*

      These are only used for quick projects I'm using for testing something out so I don't have to register an app id, ect. Can push quick things to a device to confirm/test/reject as needed.

      Any other developer profiles are usually created by xCode and are disposable as far as I am concerned.
