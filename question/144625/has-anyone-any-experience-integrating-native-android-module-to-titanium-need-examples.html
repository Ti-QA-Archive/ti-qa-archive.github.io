  • I will save you a lot of trouble. If you have no experience with Java and native Android development. There is little to no chance you will manage to get it to work. Even by copying and pasting as you suggested, there are pretty complex computing principles at play when developing modules and native Android.

    Just setting up your machine for native module development could provide quite complicated and there will be no way for you to know (for sure) if the errors are coming from your code or your setup.

    Also, after looking at your Android wheel example, this a component destined to be used with native apps. And converting such a component to a module will require knowledge on how Titanium works (natively) under the hood.

    Of course, you could contract someone to develop it for you for a fee. With relatively simple instructions, you can get it setup in your applications and use it using JavaScript.

    Hope this clears it a little more for you and good luck.
