Pricing changes from 2.0?

Hi! I just got a strange phone call from someone claiming to be from Appcelerator asking a few questions about my "business" and suggesting me to become a "partner". That person also told me that if I was making any sort of revenue from my apps I was in breach of terms. That shocked me as I was under the impression that one could sell apps created through the free version of Titanium Studio. She told me that since v2.0 that is no longer true. So I asked what it cost to become a partner and she told me 5000$/year. I gasped and asked what happened to the Indie subscription? She told me no longer exists since 2.0. So…

  1. Was this a prank call, maybe from a competitor?

  2. Can I release apps in the App/Play Store and charge for them with the free edition of Titanium Studio? Or release free apps supported by ads?

  3. If not, what is the cheapest plan that allows me to do so and what is its price?

Many thanks

  • We realize there is a lot of conflicting information out there about this. Our intent is and has always been to provide a free and unrestricted version of the platform. Titanium Mobile is and has always been open source under the Apache 2.0 license, and relies on open source software to power it. That software can be used without financial obligation to Appcelerator in any form. In fact, there is no legally defensible way to claim otherwise.

    Titanium Studio is proprietary software which we make available for free. This is where confusion has arisen internally at Appcelerator. Our pricing terms remain confusing - quite frankly, even I don't understand them fully. We recognize this problem and are working on clarifying our licensing terms as it relates to our toolchain. I will offer as much clarity on this topic as I can, as soon as possible, either through this thread or the developer blog.

    But rest assured, there is no secret agenda to charge for commercial apps built by developers who don't have paid subscriptions. This is an unfortunate breakdown in communications. You can continue to publish apps, free of charge, using Titanium Studio and Titanium Mobile as you always have. We appreciate your patience, and please stay tuned.

    — commented May 23rd 2012 by Kevin Whinnery
