$model['propertyName'] or $model.attributes['propertyName']?

In many cases working with Backbone, I am expecting to be able to call $model[&#39;propertyName&#39;] based on what I am seeing in the Alloy documentation and other libraries. However this is causing undefined errors, which appear to be resolved by calling $model.attributes[&#39;propertyName&#39;] instead. By inspecting a Backbone model object, I understand that Backbone nests the object's properties inside of attributes, but I am afraid that I am doing something wrong. Is Backbone supposed to recognize what I mean by $model.name even though the object's true structure is $model.attributes.name?

    Ugh, I wrote a long description of this and I lost it due to a network error…

    Long story short, always use get() to access attributes on your model. It's documented here: http://backbonejs.org/#Model-get

    You can find more details on attrbutes, and how it is directly related to get() here: http://backbonejs.org/#Model-attributes
