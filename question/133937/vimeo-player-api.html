  • Accepted Answer

    Couple of things:

    1. Apple requires HLS for streaming media to an iOS device. Vimeo's 'brad' has repeatedly been anti-hls.

    2. Vimeo will not expose mobile MP4s. Each time a way has been discovered, they have removed it. They actively prevent this.

    Their only solution is to use their player, which will not load in a web view on Titanium. Their model is based upon their player and Brad Doughtery is often hostile towards those (even PAID PRO users) desiring a cleaner mobile integration (read the forums as far as 2 years back). If you sense some frustration on my part, its because there is. Vimeo is a cheap solution. As such, you get what you pay for.

  • The following assumes your client is making all their videos available for mobile use, which may require a paid account. This works on iOS, but has not been tested on Android yet.

    After getting the RSS feed from a Vimeo channel I get the URL for the video and then replace part of it with "m/" (see code section) to create the mobile URL for the video:

    var videoUrl = videos.item(0).getAttribute("url");
var mobileURL = videoUrl.replace("moogaloop.swf?clip_id=","m/");

    Then I load the mobileURL into a webview where the user can click to watch the video fullscreen similar to a YouTube video.
