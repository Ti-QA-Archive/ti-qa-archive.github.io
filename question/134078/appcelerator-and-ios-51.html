Appcelerator and iOS 5.1

Am I correct in saying that the current version of Titanium Studio (1.0.9.201202141208) does not support iOS 5.1 or Xcode 4.3.1?

I had XCode 4.3.1 running but as Titanium doesn't support it I was unable to run the iOS simulator. I downloaded the preview version of Titanium Studio, but unfortunately iOS simulator crashes when trying to run in that.

My solution to get everything working on the Mac is to have the latest Stable version of Titanium and Xcode 4.2. That's great in that everything works fine on the Mac, except I cannot deploy to my iPad 2 (iOS 5.1) in order to test, because of course Xcode 4.3.1 does not support iOS 5.1

This is a nightmare?!

Is there anyway to get my Titanium Studio App on my iPad 2 (iOS 5.1) in order to test?

My last ditch attempt would be to try and rollback my iPad 2 from iOS 5.1 to iOS 5. I think I can do this through iTunes as I have downloaded an iOS 5 disc image from Apple. I am just worried that this might some how kill my iPad?!

Any help anyone can provide would be most helpful!

    You can try loading it adhoc via TestFlightapp.com
    Will require an adhoc provisioning profile from apple.

    You can build your ipa via the archives in Xcode 4.2.x and sharing it

    This should bypass the local loader problems.

    • In Xcode you have the organizer that keeps tracks of profiles, projects, archives. From the archives, you can select an archive (when you 'build for app store' from titanium, the package is found here) and 'share' it out as an ipa instead of submitting to the app store. You can then upload this to test flight. Now, inevitably someone will hijack this thread and scream "YOU DONT HAVE TO DO THAT GRAB IT FROM THE ITUNES FOLDER" but in your case that IS the problem. grin
