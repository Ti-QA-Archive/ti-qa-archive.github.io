  • I found a solution. It's crucal that read these directions thoroughly before you try them. You can really mess up your app if you don't.

    Go to Titanium > Highlight your app > make sure your at the "Run Emulator" tab and that your on the "iPhone Tab". DON'T CLOSE THIS!!! Go fire up finder and go into the folder of your app. Rename the build folder to "build2". Open the Titanium window again and click launch. And your app should work. If it does just delete the "build2" folder. If anything goes wrong just delete the new "build" folder (if any) and rename the "build2" folder to "build" again and you'll be right back where you started.

    Hope this helps!

  • In my case, it was a problem with the "Default.png" file : XCode was looking for it in "Resources/" directory instead of "Resources/iphone".

    So, you may open the "build/iphone/*.xcodeproj" with XCode, choose "Targets" in the left-side panel, and then "Copy bundle resource". You should see the Default.png file in red color. Right-click, choose "Get Info", and change the path.

    It worked for me. Maybe not for you.
