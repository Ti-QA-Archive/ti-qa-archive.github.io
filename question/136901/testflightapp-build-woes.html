TestFlightApp Build Woes

I am having trouble understanding the difference between Development and Distribution builds of an IPA and I am hoping that someone here can clarify matters.

My original understanding was that one creates an AdHoc build using an adhoc distribution provisioning profile in order to test out an app. The problem is that although I get the app to to build just fine when I try to upload the app to TestFlightApp it complains saying - Developer Build Entitlements must have get-task-allow set to true.

However, if I upload an IPA created with the developer provisioning profile TestFlightApp accepts it without a word, and proceeds to allow me to alert my test users. the app installs correctly and all is well.

Is it just me or is the way the terms Development and Distribution are used a bit confusing. And just what is the use of an AdHoc build if I can simply TestFlightApp a dvelopment build.

I should mention that I am working with Ti 2.0.1.2 dated 13th April 2012. Any help would be much appreciated

  • Development builds are limited to specific registered devices (Max 10 i think). Adhoc provisioning profiles can be distributed to update 100 recipients (per year). After enabling the push notifications against your adhox/reelease provisioning profile, make sure you go back into the provisioning profile and update a field (any field) and re-save before downloading the updated provisioning profile. There's a know bug that the profile needs to be resaved to pick up the pus hentitlement
