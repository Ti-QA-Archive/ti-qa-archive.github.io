Augmented Reality App Approved! Titanium FTW.

I used Titanium Mobile to build an Augmented Reality app for iPhone. I'm using a Web View overlay, and I'm really happy with the results.

Firebomb AR is basically virtual 8-bit firecrackers :p Just in time for the 4th of July.
http://getfirebomb.com/

I started by referencing the AR examples in the KitchenSink app.
https://github.com/appcelerator/KitchenSink

Performance is actually pretty good. Most problems come from poorly written HTML/CSS/JavaScript in the WebView. You really need to minimize your JavaScript and make more use of CSS Transforms, Transitions, and Animations. Also, using translate3d(x, y, 0) for X,Y movement is always faster than using left/top absolute positioning. And translate3d() is also faster than translate().

I came to realize that the volume buttons are disabled when in camera view. I think this is an Apple decision and not a limitation of Titanium, but I'm not 100% sure on that. I decided to have a settings panel outside of the AR camera view where users could adjust volume.

Anyway, I hope some of this helps one of you!

Zach
