    Hi

    You are adding your button and image to the window, but this is the content area of the window, try one of these instead of win1.add(change);

    win1.setLeftNavButton(change);
Or
win1.setRightNavButton(change);
Or
win1.setTitleControl(change);

    This will place your button in the top portion of the screen - the actual title bar.

    • There are some limitations on exactly what can be placed in the "nav buttons" but you can solve your problem by removing your separate imageView and placing the image reference into your actual button.

      This is done by using the backgroundImage property.

      var change = Ti.UI.createButton({
    height: 33,
    backgroundImage: 'sync.png',
    width: 33
});

  • For this, you should use the rightNavButton property.

    ####First, you create your refresh button

    var btnimage = Ti.UI.createImageView({
    image:'/sync.png',
    height:33,
    width:33
});

    ####Then set this same button to the navigation bar

    var home = Titanium.UI.createWindow({
    title:'Vthink',
    url:'list.js',
     rightNavButton: btnimage
});
