create dynamic titanium apps - under your full control even after published to the app store

Now you can create Titanium apps which can be updated at any time (just like web pages), even after they're published to the AppStore, but still are native apps!

www.cloudebug.com is introducing an API to create Titanium applications which can be altered at any time, just like web pages, even after they are published to the App Store, but, unlike web pages, these apps are native apps and can use every feature of the Titanium SDK!

While this feature might not be that important for the Android developers, it is crucially important for the iOS developers, where we may wait unspecified period of time (while our app/update is being reviewed by Apple) just to fix a bug, collecting negative feedbacks while waiting.

This wait is over now! You can have a total and instant control over your code at any time!

Based on our own experience, having your application under your total control at all times totally changing the way of developing apps for the iOS/Apple's AppStore! Changing it for the better!

  • since i keep receiving requests for the dynapp access, i've decided to post this additional information regarding the dynapp technology and the dynapp project status:

    <br />
    first, i want thank all of you for your interest.
    <br />
    <br />

    the dynapp way of designing and distributing apps is as powerful and optimal as the internet/web itself, no need for more explanations.
    <br />
    <br />

    unfortunately, based on some negative feedback coming from the titanium community, and also some implications which dynapp might trigger (but, never did for over a year that i use it) from the apple's side, i've decided to withdraw my offer of a public beta (as i've stated in my previous post here).
    <br />
    <br />

    that said:

    • i'm using dynapp for all my ios and android apps and projects for over a year, and can not even imagine going back to the "brick"-style distribution;

    • quality of my apps went trough the roof. reason: nobody see any bugs/updates; i collect the trace info using cloudebug service, at the run time, and fix bugs before users realize there is a bug :-));

    • app becomes a living entity, making customers to come back to it, to see what else has happen since the last login: best suggestions are being implemented in a matter of hours, sometimes;

    • with commercial customers (this is what my company mostly does: commercial business apps for mobile) - dynapp, as you can, probably, imagine, is priceless: you can demonstrate different solutions/aspects as you communicate with the customer, sometimes - as you speak/demo; you can deploy apps into production with 0-effort, single-point of maintenance is a king, single code-base is a blessing.

    BUT, at this point, dynapp is the hosted environment running on my servers and not open to public.

    i might consider some sort of licensing, but still, at least at this time, would run it off of my hosting environment.

    it is possible that i may provide full distribution in the future, but not at this point.
    <br />
    <br />

    sorry for the less than positive news, but, in business, we all do what makes the most sense in most aspects.
    <br />
    <br />

    all the best with your projects!

    code strong!

    yury
