    Hi

    There is NO philosophy that says if an app becomes successful then you should pay for anything.

    In short; for users of Titanium Studio (free branch) - you are NOT charged to create apps, you are not charged to put apps in the store, you are not charged at some point in the future because your app became successful. So in answer to this part of your question "..why Is Appcelerator hesitant to name clear numbers?" - they are not hesitant - there is no number - gain 10 billion downloads and they will pat you on the back not take from your wallet. They will however want to see if you would like more publicity as you would be a massive success story.

    • Just to add to Malcolm's excellent answer, we provide no specific prices because what we do charge for is very tailored to the specific customer. Our business model is based on selling services to Enterprise customers (Fortune 500) who need specific levels of support, CLAs, Enterprise-grade backend solutions, consulting, etc. None of that is off-the-shelf stuff for which we can list a stock price.

      I don't know if there's an upper limit to the cloud resources you can use before we ask for money. (I'm not on the Sales side of the house.) Reasonably, if your app is so successful to be using gobs of resources, you really should have a sound business model that supports paying for that service.

      I don't recall that chead@ address ever being a valid address, but maybe it was.
