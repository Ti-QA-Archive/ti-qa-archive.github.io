Why can't I play remote videos recorded with an iPhone?

I'm working with Titanium SDK 3.1.1 and deploying for iOS. In my app I can record a video and then upload it to a server. On the server, links to access the video are created, which I can paste on a browser and play them or download them to play on a media player on a computer without any problem. The videos are enconded with H.264 and ACC.

But if I try to use any of these videos on a Titanium Video Player, I get a black screen and no video is played ever. I tried with different videos from another website and the video was played with no problem.

This is the code for my video player:

    var activeMovie = Titanium.Media.createVideoPlayer({
        mediaControlStyle : Titanium.Media.VIDEO_CONTROL_DEFAULT,
        scalingMode : Titanium.Media.VIDEO_SCALING_ASPECT_FIT,
        top : 22 + dpi,
        width : Ti.Platform.displayCaps.platformWidth,
        height : 300 + dpi,
        url : videoUrl,
        autoplay : true,
        sourceType : Ti.Media.VIDEO_SOURCE_TYPE_STREAMING
    });

Where videoUrl is something like this http:&#x2F;&#x2F;api.kinedu.com&#x2F;system&#x2F;moments&#x2F;attachments&#x2F;000&#x2F;000&#x2F;320&#x2F;original&#x2F;baby_temp.mp4

I tried to copy the url on the Clipboard and paste it on the iPhone Safari and no luck playing it there, but on the desktop Safari I can play it.

There doesn't seem to be any problem on the video file since I can play it with any media player so far. So why I can't play it on the Titanium Video Player on my device?

    Unless you're actually 'streaming' video (which apparently, you're not - you're using http) , you don't need the sourceType setting.

    If that doesn't fix it, then it's possible the server hosting the mp4 isn't serving it as the correct MIME type.

    I have built many apps with Titanium which record and upload video.

  • Hi Nenvo, quite recently it also had this problem. Since iOS 7 you have to make sure that the Http Headers of your video files are set to "Accept-Ranges: bytes". Otherwise the "MPMoviePlayerController", the underlying Obj-C Class, cannot stream your content.
