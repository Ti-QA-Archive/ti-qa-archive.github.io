    I am NOT 100% sure on this, but I think that the android:label in the <application> must match the android:label in the <activity>

    In your case you are only modifying part of the manifest, and I think if you pull the manifest from the build directory this will be the case:

    your application probably looks like this: 

    <application android:debuggable="true" android:icon="@drawable/appicon" android:label="Corredores de Seguros" android:name=".CorredoresdeSegurosActivity">

    and your activity like this:

        <activity
        android:label="BCISeguros"
        android:configChanges="keyboardHidden|orientation"
        android:name=".CorredoresdeSegurosActivity"
        android:screenOrientation="portrait" android:theme="@style/Theme.Titanium">
        <intent-filter>
        <action android:name="android.intent.action.MAIN"/>
        <category android:name="android.intent.category.LAUNCHER"/>
            </intent-filter>
    </activity>

  • Solved this problem - Titanium creates the activity based on the name of the app. I had tried to override this in the TiApp.xml defining my own activity as follows:

    <activity
    android:configChanges="keyboardHidden|orientation|screenSize"
    android:label="TRL HDL and CVD"
    android:name=".TRLHDLandCVDActivity"
    android:screenOrientation="portrait" android:theme="@style/Theme.Titanium">
    <intent-filter>
        <action android:name="android.intent.action.MAIN"/>
        <category android:name="android.intent.category.LAUNCHER"/>
    </intent-filter>
</activity>

    but Titanium was adding another one with a slight difference

    <activity
    android:configChanges="keyboardHidden|orientation|screenSize"
    android:label="TRL HDL and CVD"
    android:name=".TrlHdlAndCvdActivity"
    android:screenOrientation="portrait" android:theme="@style/Theme.Titanium">
    <intent-filter>
        <action android:name="android.intent.action.MAIN"/>
        <category android:name="android.intent.category.LAUNCHER"/>
    </intent-filter>
</activity>

    I changed the activity name to what Titanium was generating and that solved the problem, my definition was recognised by Titanium and only one icon got installed.
