Titanium Developer Login Behind Proxy Issues - Win XP Solution

I just tried to install Titanium Developer 1.2.2 on my PC behind a corporate firewall. I booted and got "Request timed out. Make sure you are online" the moment I hit the login button.

Querying for that error, I found this post. It eliminated a check to see if I was online, but that just meant it took 30 seconds to get the time out error instead of getting it immediately.

I found another post about changing the proxy setting in the appcelerator.xml file, but that didn't work. Here's what did.

Step 1: Right click on "My Computer" and select "Properties"
Step 2: Select the "Advanced" tab and click the "Environment Variables" button.
Step 3: Check the system variables list for an HTTP_PROXY and an HTTPS_PROXY variable. In many cases, neither will be there. If they are, you may need to edit them instead of creating new ones.

Here's an important part, you must know your proxy address.

I found mine by going into IE, selecting "Internet Options" from "Tools", selecting "Connection", then selecting "LAN Settings". The LAN settings had "Use an Automatic Configuration Script" option checked, providing a link to the script. I put that link in my browser, looked over the script, and determined my proxy address.

In such a script, a proxy address might look like proxy.yourcorporation.com:8080 (the proxy address and the port).

Step 4: (back in "Environment Variables") - Under the "System Variables" box click "New". Do this two times (click it, fill in info, click "ok", do again). For the variable names, use HTTP_PROXY and HTTPS_PROXY. For the variable values use your proxy address (like "http://proxy.yourcorp.com:8080"). I tried it with and without the "http://" at the beginning and it needs the "http://" in my experience.

This solved the issue.
